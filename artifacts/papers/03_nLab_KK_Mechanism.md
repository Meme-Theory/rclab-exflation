## Idea

The *Kaluza-Klein mechanism*, named after [Theodor Kaluza](/nlab/show/Theodor+Kaluza) and [Oskar Klein](/nlab/show/Oskar+Klein), is the observation that pure [gravity](/nlab/show/gravity) on a [product](/nlab/show/Cartesian+product) [spacetime](/nlab/show/spacetime) X×FX \times F with fixed [metric](/nlab/show/metric) g Fg\_F on FF looks on XX, as an [effective field theory](/nlab/show/effective+field+theory), like [gravity](/nlab/show/gravity) coupled to [Yang-Mills theory](/nlab/show/Yang-Mills+theory) – [Einstein-Yang-Mills theory](/nlab/show/Einstein-Yang-Mills+theory) – for [gauge group](/nlab/show/gauge+group) GG the [Lie group](/nlab/show/Lie+group) of [isometries](/nlab/show/isometries) of (F,g F)(F,g\_F). In particular for F=S 1F = S^1 the [circle](/nlab/show/circle), it yields [electromagnetism](/nlab/show/electromagnetism) coupled to [gravity](/nlab/show/gravity) (a “[graviphoton](/nlab/show/graviphoton)” [field](/nlab/show/field+%28physics%29), but in addition also a [dilaton](/nlab/show/dilaton)) – [Einstein-Maxwell theory](/nlab/show/Einstein-Maxwell+theory).

When applied to [supergravity](/nlab/show/supergravity) then this mechanism produces not just [force](/nlab/show/force) fields but also [fermionic](/nlab/show/fermion) [matter](/nlab/show/matter) in the resulting [effective field theory](/nlab/show/effective+field+theory), hence yields [Einstein-Yang-Mills-Dirac theory](/nlab/show/Einstein-Yang-Mills-Dirac+theory), see the examples below at *[KK-reduction of 11d Supergravity](#KKReductionOf11dSupergravity)*. As such the KK-mechanism is not unlike the idea of [geometrodynamics](/nlab/show/geometrodynamics), according to which fundamentally there is only [dynamical](/nlab/show/dynamics) [spacetime](/nlab/show/spacetime) geometry in the form of [gravity](/nlab/show/gravity) with all matter and other forces being special incarnations of this.

Since in [general relativity](/nlab/show/general+relativity) also the size and shape of the fiber FF is dynamical, generically [effective field theories](/nlab/show/effective+field+theories) arising from KK-compactification contain spurious fields parameterizing the geometry of FF. In the simplest case this is just the [dilaton](/nlab/show/dilaton), encoding the total [volume](/nlab/show/volume) of FF, more generally these fields are often called the *[moduli](/nlab/show/moduli)* fields. Since these moduli fields are not observed in [experiment](/nlab/show/experiment), naive KK-models are generically [phenomenologically](/nlab/show/phenomenology) unviable. However, in variants of gravity such as higher dimensional [supergravity](/nlab/show/supergravity) there are possibilities for the moduli to obtain masses and hence for the KK-models to become viable after all. This is the problem of *[moduli stabilization](/nlab/show/moduli+stabilization)*.

### The mechanism

More precisely, the [Einstein-Hilbert action](/nlab/show/Einstein-Hilbert+action) functional on [pseudo-Riemannian metrics](/nlab/show/pseudo-Riemannian+metrics) on a [product](/nlab/show/Cartesian+product) [spacetime](/nlab/show/spacetime) X×FX \times F

g X×F↦S EH(g X×F)≔∫ X×FR(g X×F)dvol(g X×F)
g\_{X \times F}
\;\mapsto\;
S\_{EH}(g\_{X \times F})
\;\coloneqq\;
\int\_{X \times F} R(g\_{X \times F}) \, dvol(g\_{X \times F})

when restricted to [pseudo-Riemannian metrics](/nlab/show/pseudo-Riemannian+metrics) of the special form

(g X×F KK(x,f))∈Sym 2(Γ(T \*X×F))=(g F(f) A a(x)k a A a(x)k a g X(x)),
\left(g^{KK}\_{X \times F} \left(x,f\right) \right)
\;\in \;
Sym^2\left(\Gamma\left(T^\* X \times F\right)\right)
\;=\;
\left(
\array{
g\_F(f) & A^a(x)k\_a
\\
A^a(x) k\_a & g\_X(x)
}
\right)
\,,

where {k a∈Γ(TF)}\{k\_a \in \Gamma(T F)\} are a [basis](/nlab/show/basis) for the [Killing vector field](/nlab/show/Killing+vector+field)s of (F,g F)(F,g\_F), is equivalently rewritten as

S EH(g X×F KK)=S EH(g X)+S YM(A)+S mod(g F),
S\_{EH}\left(g^KK\_{X \times F}\right)
=
S\_{EH}\left(g\_X\right) + S\_{YM}(A) + S\_{mod}\left(g\_F\right)
\,,

where

- the first term is the [EH action](/nlab/show/Einstein-Hilbert+action) of g Xg\_X on XX
- and the second term is the [action functional](/nlab/show/action+functional) of [Yang-Mills theory](/nlab/show/Yang-Mills+theory) for the [connection](/nlab/show/connection+on+a+bundle) [Lie algebra-valued 1-form](/nlab/show/Lie+algebra-valued+1-form) AA with values in the [Lie algebra](/nlab/show/Lie+algebra) of the [isometry](/nlab/show/isometry) [Lie group](/nlab/show/Lie+group) GG of (F,g F)(F,g\_F);
- and finally the last term is regarded as a [functional](/nlab/show/functional) on the [moduli space](/nlab/show/moduli+space) of this ansatz: the [moduli space of Riemannian metrics](/nlab/show/moduli+space+of+Riemannian+metrics) on the [fiber](/nlab/show/fiber).

More generally, one observes that the [infinitesimal](/nlab/show/infinitesimal+object) transformation of [metrics](/nlab/show/Riemannian+metric) g X×Fg\_{X \times F} of the above form under [diffeomorphisms](/nlab/show/diffeomorphisms) generated by a [Killing vector field](/nlab/show/Killing+vector+field) (x,f)↦λ a(x)k a(f) (x,f) \mapsto \lambda^a(x) k\_a(f) is given on the AA-component as follows:

ddϵA=d Xλ a+[λ,A].
\frac{d}{d \epsilon} A = d\_X \lambda^a + [\lambda,A]
\,.

This is the infinitesimal form of a [gauge transformation](/nlab/show/gauge+transformation): an [isomorphism](/nlab/show/isomorphism) in the [groupoid of Lie algebra-valued forms](/nlab/show/groupoid+of+Lie+algebra-valued+forms). This means than *every* functional (g X×F)↦S(g X×F)(g\_{X \times F}) \mapsto S(g\_{X\times F}) that is invariant under [diffeomorphisms](/nlab/show/diffeomorphisms) will restrict on metrics of the above form to something that looks like an action functional for a [gauge theory](/nlab/show/gauge+theory) of the [gauge field](/nlab/show/gauge+field) AA.

Moreover, all this of course remains true if the [product](/nlab/show/Cartesian+product) X×FX \times F – which we may think of as the trivial FF-[fiber bundle](/nlab/show/fiber+bundle) over XX – is generalized to any [associated bundle](/nlab/show/associated+bundle) E→XE \to X with [fiber](/nlab/show/fiber) FF, associated to a GG-[principal bundle](/nlab/show/principal+bundle) P→XP \to X (hence such that E=P× GFE = P \times\_G F), in which case the above decomposition of the metric applies locally.

Then one finds that the KK-mechanics indeed not only reproduces [gauge fields](/nlab/show/gauge+fields) and their correct [dynamics](/nlab/show/dynamics) from pure [gravity](/nlab/show/gravity) in higher dimensions, but also the [forces](/nlab/show/forces) which they exert on test particles. For instance the trajectory of a [charged particle](/nlab/show/charged+particle) subject to the [Lorentz force](/nlab/show/Lorentz+force) exerted by an [electromagnetic field](/nlab/show/electromagnetic+field) in dd-dimensional [spacetime](/nlab/show/spacetime) is in fact a [geodesic](/nlab/show/geodesic) in the field of pure [gravity](/nlab/show/gravity) of the total space of the corresponding KK-un-compactified [principal U(1)-bundle](/nlab/show/principal+U%281%29-bundle). See ([Bleecker 81](#Bleecker81)) or ([Bartlett 13](#Bartlett13)) for a pedagogical discussion of this effect.

A [pseudo-Riemannian manifold](/nlab/show/pseudo-Riemannian+manifold) of this form (E,g E KK)\left(E, g^KK\_{E}\right) for fixed *[moduli](/nlab/show/moduli)* g Fg\_F is called a **Kaluza-Klein compactification** of the [spacetime](/nlab/show/spacetime) EE. One also speaks of the effective spacetime XX as being obtained by **dimensional reduction** from the spacetime EE.

(Beware that the term “compactification” here is vaguely related to but rather different from the use of the term in [mathematics](/nlab/show/mathematics), as in *[one-point compactification](/nlab/show/one-point+compactification)* etc.).

### Its application in theoretical physics

Various evident generalizations of this ansatz can and are being considered.

Most notably for actual [model building](/nlab/show/model+building) in [physics](/nlab/show/physics) it is of interest to consider the case where g Xg\_{X} and AA are *not* necessarily constant along the fiber FF. Typically in applications these fields are expanded in terms of [Fourier modes](/nlab/show/Fourier+modes) on FF. The [coefficients](/nlab/show/coefficients) of the higher modes appear as *[massive](/nlab/show/mass) [fields](/nlab/show/field+%28physics%29)* in the [effective](/nlab/show/effective+QFT) KK-[action functional](/nlab/show/action+functional). They are called the **higher Kaluza-Klein modes** . These masses are inversely proportional to the metric [volume](/nlab/show/volume) of FF. For physical [model building](/nlab/show/model+building) this volume is therefore chosen to be very small, such that it implies that the [model](/nlab/show/model+%28physics%29) does not predict the [observation](/nlab/show/observation) of the [quanta](/nlab/show/quanta) of these massive modes in existing accelerator [experiments](/nlab/show/experiments) (such as the [LHC](/nlab/show/LHC)).

On the other hand, the extra [moduli](/nlab/show/moduli) [fields](/nlab/show/field+%28physics%29) g Fg\_F do not acquire effective [masses](/nlab/show/masses) on XX this way. Therefore [models](/nlab/show/model+%28physics%29) in *plain* Kaluza-Klein theory are trivially ruled out by [experiment](/nlab/show/experiment): for any choice of FF they predicts the [observation](/nlab/show/observation) on XX of these massless [moduli](/nlab/show/moduli) [fields](/nlab/show/field+%28physics%29), which however are not being seen in actual accelerator [experiments](/nlab/show/experiments).

Therefore if in a [model](/nlab/show/model+%28physics%29) for fundamental physics the Kaluza-Klein mechanism is invoked as a way to explain the existence of the [standard model of particle physics](/nlab/show/standard+model+of+particle+physics) in [Yang-Mills theory](/nlab/show/Yang-Mills+theory) from pure [gravity](/nlab/show/gravity), then the setup needs to be further generalized: other ingredients of the [model](/nlab/show/model+%28physics%29) need to be introduced that serve to equip the [moduli](/nlab/show/moduli) [fields](/nlab/show/field+%28physics%29) with an effective [potential](/nlab/show/potential) with a [positive](/nlab/show/positive+number) [minimum](/nlab/show/minimum), such that these [fields](/nlab/show/field+%28physics%29) to acquire an effective [mass](/nlab/show/mass) on XX.

In [physics](/nlab/show/physics) [model building](/nlab/show/model+building) the problem of constructing such a more general KK-[model](/nlab/show/model+%28physics%29) is called the **[moduli stabilization](/nlab/show/moduli+stabilization) problem** . (See also at *[landscape of string theory vacua](/nlab/show/landscape+of+string+theory+vacua)*.)

### Formalization

KK-compactification along trivial fibrations is closely related to forming [mapping stacks](/nlab/show/mapping+stacks): if Fields n\mathbf{Fields}\_n is the [moduli stack](/nlab/show/moduli+stack) of [fields](/nlab/show/field+%28physics%29) for an nn-dimensional field theory (see at *[prequantum field theory](/nlab/show/prequantum+field+theory)* for more on this), then for Σ k\Sigma\_{k} a kk-dimensional manifold with k<nk \lt n the [mapping stack](/nlab/show/mapping+stack)

Fields n−k≔[Σ k,Fields]
\mathbf{Fields}\_{n-k} \coloneqq [\Sigma\_k, \mathbf{Fields}]

may be thought of as the [moduli stack](/nlab/show/moduli+stack) of fields for an (n−k)(n-k)-dimensional field theory. By the definition [universal property](/nlab/show/universal+property) of the mapping stack, this lower dimensional field theory is then such that a field configuration over an (n−k)(n-k)-dimensional [spacetime](/nlab/show/spacetime) X n−kX\_{n-k}

ϕ:X n−k⟶Fields n−k
\phi \colon X\_{n-k} \longrightarrow \mathbf{Fields}\_{n-k}

is equivalently a field configuration of the nn-dimensional field theory

X n−k×Σ k⟶Fields n
X\_{n-k} \times \Sigma\_k \longrightarrow \mathbf{Fields}\_n

on the [product](/nlab/show/product) space X n−k×Σ kX\_{n-k}\times \Sigma\_k (the trivial Σ k\Sigma\_{k}-[fiber bundle](/nlab/show/fiber+bundle) over X n−kX\_{n-k}).

Traditionally KK-reduction is understood as retaining only parts of Fields n−k\mathbf{Fields}\_{n-k} (the “0-modes” of fields on Σ k\Sigma\_k only) but of course one may consider arbitrary corrections to this picture and eventually retain the full information.

One example of KK-reduction where the full mapping stack appears is the reduction of [topologically twisted](/nlab/show/topological+twist) [N=4 D=4 super Yang-Mills theory](/nlab/show/N%3D4+D%3D4+super+Yang-Mills+theory) on a complex curve CC as it appears in the explanation of [geometric Langlands duality](/nlab/show/geometric+Langlands+duality) as a special case of [S-duality](/nlab/show/S-duality) ([Witten 08, section 6](#Witten08)). Here Fields 4=BG conn\mathbf{Fields}\_4 = \mathbf{B}G\_{\mathrm{conn}} is the universal moduli stack of GG-[principal connections](/nlab/show/principal+connections) (or rther that of GG-[Higgs bundles](/nlab/show/Higgs+bundles)).

## Examples

### Reductions of pure gravity with realistic gauge groups

The [gauge group](/nlab/show/gauge+group) of the [experimentally](/nlab/show/experiment) verified [standard model of particle physics](/nlab/show/standard+model+of+particle+physics) is a [quotient](/nlab/show/quotient) of the [product](/nlab/show/product) of the [special unitary groups](/nlab/show/special+unitary+groups) SU(3)SU(3) and SU(2)SU(2) and the [circle group](/nlab/show/circle+group) U(1)U(1).

In ([Witten 81](#Witten81)) it was observed that the minimal [dimension](/nlab/show/dimension) of a [fiber](/nlab/show/fiber) FF for the KK-reduction to yield the [gauge group](/nlab/show/gauge+group) SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1)SU(3) \times SU(2) \times U(1) is d F=7d\_F = 7 . This may be a meaningless numerical coincidence, but might be – and was regarded as being – remarkable: because it means that the minimum total dimension of a KK-compactification X×FX \times F that could yield a realistic model of observed physics is 4+7=114 + 7 = 11. This is the uniquely specified dimensional of the maximal [supergravity](/nlab/show/supergravity) model: [11-dimensional supergravity](/nlab/show/11-dimensional+supergravity).

While there are many 7-[dimensional](/nlab/show/dimension) [manifolds](/nlab/show/manifolds) FF that do yield the desired [gauge group](/nlab/show/gauge+group) of the [standard model](/nlab/show/standard+model+of+particle+physics), [Witten 81](#Witten81) also shows that for none of them does the remaining [field](/nlab/show/field+%28physics%29) content of the [standard model](/nlab/show/standard+model+of+particle+physics) – the [fermions](/nlab/show/fermions) and the [Higgs field](/nlab/show/Higgs+field) – come out correctly, due to lack of [chiral fermions](/nlab/show/chiral+fermions).

Largely due to this result, the original pure Kaluza-Klein ansatz that starts with just pure [Einstein gravity](/nlab/show/Einstein+gravity) with no other [fields](/nlab/show/field+%28physics%29)) is nowadays regarded as non-viable route towards the [standard model of particle physics](/nlab/show/standard+model+of+particle+physics). But one can further play with the idea and consider more flexible [models](/nlab/show/model+%28physics%29) that still exhibit the essence of KK-reduction in parts.

Notably the [11-dimensional supergravity](/nlab/show/11-dimensional+supergravity) mentioned above contains more [fields](/nlab/show/field+%28physics%29) than just the field of [gravity](/nlab/show/gravity). Specifically it contains the [supergravity C-field](/nlab/show/supergravity+C-field) which is a [higher](/nlab/show/higher+gauge+theory) analog of the [electromagnetic field](/nlab/show/electromagnetic+field). KK-reductions of [11-dimensional supergravity](/nlab/show/11-dimensional+supergravity) and of its conjectured [M-theory](/nlab/show/M-theory) [UV-completion](/nlab/show/UV-completion) for instance on [G₂-manifold](/nlab/show/G%E2%82%82-manifold) fibers turn out to be able to yield [models](/nlab/show/model+%28physics%29) which come at least very close to the [standard model of particle physics](/nlab/show/standard+model+of+particle+physics) when considered on [orbifold](/nlab/show/orbifold) spacetimes. This we come to [below](#KKReductionOf11dSupergravity), see also at *[duality between M-theory and type IIA string theory](/nlab/show/duality+between+M-theory+and+type+IIA+string+theory)*.

### Reductions of type II supergravity

Motivation for further variants of the KK-ansatz has to a large extent come from models in [string theory](/nlab/show/string+theory). During the end of the 20th and the beginning of the 21st century, the widely dominant ansatz followed in the higher energy physics community is to study 10-dimensional [type II supergravity](/nlab/show/type+II+supergravity) models KK-reduced on 6-dimensional [Calabi-Yau spaces](/nlab/show/Calabi-Yau+spaces) FF. (See also at *[supersymmetry and Calabi-Yau manifolds](/nlab/show/supersymmetry+and+Calabi-Yau+manifolds)*.)

The advantage of these type II models is that they naturally involve further [higher gauge fields](/nlab/show/higher+gauge+fields), called the [B-field](/nlab/show/B-field) and the [RR-field](/nlab/show/RR-field). These are modeled by [cocycles](/nlab/show/cocycles) in [ordinary differential cohomology](/nlab/show/ordinary+differential+cohomology) and in [differential K-theory](/nlab/show/differential+K-theory), respectively, which means that their [field strength](/nlab/show/field+strength) ℱ\mathcal{F} is an inhomogeneous closed [differential form](/nlab/show/differential+form) of even or of odd degree. Moreover, restricted to configurations ℱ\mathcal{F} of these forms with specified [cycles](/nlab/show/cycles) in the [fiber](/nlab/show/fiber) FF, the [moduli](/nlab/show/moduli) part of the KK-reduced action functional

(g KK,F)↦S EH(g X)+S YM(A)+S mod(g F,ℱ)
(g^KK, F) \mapsto S\_{EH}(g\_X) + S\_{YM}(A) + S\_{mod}(g\_F, \mathcal{F})

*does* produce the previously missing [positive](/nlab/show/positive+number) [potentials](/nlab/show/potentials) for g Fg\_F proportional to these cycles of ℱ\mathcal{F}. So KK-reduction of 10-dimensional supergravities can – for a suitable ansatz – cure the old problem of [moduli stabilization](/nlab/show/moduli+stabilization) in KK-theory.

This means that physical model building using the specific ansatz of KK-reduction of type II supergravities on Calabi-Yau fibers reduces to a noteworthy enumerative problem in [complex geometry](/nlab/show/complex+geometry): classify all real 6-dimensional [Calabi-Yau manifolds](/nlab/show/Calabi-Yau+manifolds) with given [isometries](/nlab/show/isometries) and given [cycles](/nlab/show/cycles).

While interesting, there are few tools known for performing this classification. The only thing that seems to be clear is that the classification is not sparse: there are many points in this space of choices. Since all this is relevant in model building in [string theory](/nlab/show/string+theory), the space of these choices has been termed the *[landscape of string theory vacua](/nlab/show/landscape+of+string+theory+vacua)*.

For more on this see also

- *[supergravity and Calabi-Yau manifolds](/nlab/show/supergravity+and+Calabi-Yau+manifolds)*,
- *[string phenomenology](/nlab/show/string+phenomenology)*
- *[string theory FAQ – What does it mean to say that string theory has a “landscape” of solutions?](string%20theory%20FAQ#WhatDoesItMeanToSayStringTheoryHasALandscapeOfSolutions)*.

### KK-Reduction of 11d supergravity

…[duality between M-theory and type IIA string theory](/nlab/show/duality+between+M-theory+and+type+IIA+string+theory)…

The lift of the [above](#ReductionsOfTypeIISupergravity) reduction of [type IIA supergravity](/nlab/show/type+II+supergravity) on [Calabi-Yau manifolds](/nlab/show/Calabi-Yau+manifolds) to [M-theory](/nlab/show/M-theory) is the KK-reduction of [11-dimensional supergravity](/nlab/show/11-dimensional+supergravity)/[M-theory](/nlab/show/M-theory) on [G₂-manifolds](/nlab/show/G%E2%82%82-manifolds) X 7X\_7. The role of the [B-field](/nlab/show/B-field) and [RR-field](/nlab/show/RR-field) is now played by the [supergravity C-field](/nlab/show/supergravity+C-field), a [higher gauge field](/nlab/show/higher+gauge+field) in [twisted](/nlab/show/twisted+cohomology) [ordinary differential cohomology](/nlab/show/ordinary+differential+cohomology) of degree 3+1. For non-vanishing [field strength](/nlab/show/field+strength) (“flux”) of the [supergravity C-field](/nlab/show/supergravity+C-field) in the 4d space this is the [Freund-Rubin compactification](/nlab/show/Freund-Rubin+compactification) yielding [weak G₂ holonomy](/nlab/show/weak+G%E2%82%82+holonomy) on X 7X\_7.

### Cascades of KK-reductions

Apart from possibly producing [phenomenologically](/nlab/show/phenomenology) interesting [models](/nlab/show/model+%28physics%29) from [UV-complete](/nlab/show/UV-completion) fundamental [theories](/nlab/show/theory+%28physics%29) on higher dimensional [spacetimes](/nlab/show/spacetimes), KK-reduction generally serves to connect a wide range of [quantum field theories](/nlab/show/quantum+field+theories) with each other in a way that serves to illuminate their general structure and to map the [moduli space](/nlab/show/moduli+space) of all of them.

For instance various deep but rather mysterious properties of 4-dimensional [Yang-Mills theory](/nlab/show/Yang-Mills+theory) (the central theory of the [standard model of particle physics](/nlab/show/standard+model+of+particle+physics)), or at least of its [supersymmetric](/nlab/show/supersymmetry) versions [N=1 D=4 super Yang-Mills theory](/nlab/show/N%3D1+D%3D4+super+Yang-Mills+theory)/[N=2 D=4 sYM](/nlab/show/N%3D2+D%3D4+sYM)/[N=4 D=4 sYM](/nlab/show/N%3D4+D%3D4+sYM), find their natural *geometric* interpretation by understanding this 4d theory as the KK-compactification of the [6d (2,0)-superconformal QFT](/nlab/show/6d+%282%2C0%29-superconformal+QFT) on a [torus](/nlab/show/torus) [fiber](/nlab/show/fiber). Notably the [Montonen-Olive duality](/nlab/show/Montonen-Olive+duality)/[S-duality](/nlab/show/S-duality) of [N=2 D=4 super Yang-Mills theory](/nlab/show/N%3D2+D%3D4+super+Yang-Mills+theory) is the [conformal transformation](/nlab/show/conformal+transformation) remnant of the compactification torus remaining of the [conformal invariant](/nlab/show/conformal+field+theory) of the [6d (2,0)-superconformal QFT](/nlab/show/6d+%282%2C0%29-superconformal+QFT) (see there for more on this).

At the same time, the [6d (2,0)-superconformal QFT](/nlab/show/6d+%282%2C0%29-superconformal+QFT) is itself related to a yet higher dimensional theory, namely to a [7d Chern-Simons theory](/nlab/show/7d+Chern-Simons+theory), not by KK-reduction, but by [AdS-CFT duality](/nlab/show/AdS-CFT+duality). But that 7d Chern-Simons theory in turn is the KK-reduction of the 11-dimensional Chern-Simons term in [11-dimensional supergravity](/nlab/show/11-dimensional+supergravity). Hence all this structure follows again from the maximal dimensional [supergravity](/nlab/show/supergravity) theory ([M-theory](/nlab/show/M-theory)) if in addition to KK-reduction one also considers [AdS-CFT](/nlab/show/AdS-CFT)-boundary relations.

In an analogous way, the supersymmetric version of 4-dimensional Yang-Mills theory with twice that supersymmetry, namely [N=4 D=4 sYM](/nlab/show/N%3D4+D%3D4+sYM), is itself directly related by [AdS-CFT](/nlab/show/AdS-CFT) to [type II supergravity](/nlab/show/type+II+supergravity).

And this process continues further down in [dimension](/nlab/show/dimension): [N=2 D=4 super Yang-Mills theory](/nlab/show/N%3D2+D%3D4+super+Yang-Mills+theory) and [N=4 D=4 sYM](/nlab/show/N%3D4+D%3D4+sYM) themselves have further KK-reductions to 2-dimensional field theories. For N=4N = 4 and after passing to the [topologically twisted theory](/nlab/show/topologically+twisted+D%3D4+super+Yang-Mills+theory), these are an [A-model](/nlab/show/A-model) and a [B-model](/nlab/show/B-model) [topological string](/nlab/show/topological+string) [TCFT](/nlab/show/TCFT), respectively. Now what used to be [S-duality](/nlab/show/S-duality)/[Montonen-Olive duality](/nlab/show/Montonen-Olive+duality) in 4d and [conformal invariance](/nlab/show/conformal+field+theory) in 6d and topological invariant in 7d and 11d here becomes [geometric Langlands duality](/nlab/show/geometric+Langlands+duality) in 2d (see there for more on this) and produces [Donaldson theory](/nlab/show/Donaldson+theory).

Hence by iteratively applying KK-reductions and other [dualities](/nlab/show/dualities) and [topological twists](/nlab/show/topologically+twisted+D%3D4+super+Yang-Mills+theory), one finds long *cascades* of different [quantum field theories](/nlab/show/quantum+field+theories) that all superficially look very different, but which thereby become closely related as different aspects of one single higher dimensional field theory. Not all of these lower dimensional theories can be [phenomenological](/nlab/show/phenomenology) viable [models](/nlab/show/model+%28physics%29), but even the superficially “unrealistic” theories such as the [6d (2,0)-superconformal QFT](/nlab/show/6d+%282%2C0%29-superconformal+QFT) serve, via KK-reduction, to explain and illuminate deep properties of (semi-)realistic theories such as [super Yang-Mills theory](/nlab/show/super+Yang-Mills+theory) in 4d.

### Infinite-temperature thermal field theory

With the formulation of [thermal field theory](/nlab/show/thermal+field+theory) via [Wick rotation](/nlab/show/Wick+rotation) as a [Euclidean field theory](/nlab/show/Euclidean+field+theory) on a [Riemannian manifold](/nlab/show/Riemannian+manifold) X 3×S β 1X\_3 \times S^1\_\beta whose compact/periodic Euclidean time runs along a [circle](/nlab/show/circle) S β 1S^1\_\beta of [circumference](/nlab/show/length) β=1/T\beta = 1/T, the limit of infinite temperature corresponds to the [limit](/nlab/show/limit+of+a+sequence) β→0\beta \to 0 in which this [circle](/nlab/show/circle) [fiber](/nlab/show/fiber) shrinks away.

In terms of the thermal [Euclidean field theory](/nlab/show/Euclidean+field+theory) on X 3×S β 1X\_3 \times S^1\_\beta therefore the [infinite-temperature limit](/nlab/show/infinite-temperature+thermal+field+theory) is given by Kaluaza-Klein-type dimensional reduction along this circle [fiber](/nlab/show/fiber) to a 3-dimensional [Euclidean field theory](/nlab/show/Euclidean+field+theory) on X 3X\_3 ([Ginsparg 80](#Ginsparg80), [Appelquist-Pisarski 81](#AppelquistPisarski81), [Nadkarni 83](#Nadkarni83), [Jourjine 84](#Jourjine84), [Nadkarni 88](#Nadkarni88)).

As usual with [KK-reduction](/nlab/show/KK-reduction), some care must be exercised to ensure that the compactified theory is itself still a [local field theory](/nlab/show/local+field+theory). Depending on how exactly one proceeds this may be subtle ([Landsman 89](#Landsman89)), but there exist robust approaches ([Reisz 92](#Reisz92), [Kajantie-Laine-Rummukainen-Shaposhnikov 96](#KajantieLaineRummukainenShaposhnikov96)).
