## 2. Special Assessment: First-Sound Ring at 325 Mpc

This is Session 43's most distinctive result. The prediction:

- **Observable**: xi(r) peak at r = 325 +/- 20 Mpc
- **Amplitude**: 20.4% of BAO (10.6% in xi*r^2)

- **Ratio**: r_1/r_s = sqrt(3(1+R*)) = 2.211 (zero free parameters)
- **SNR**: 2-5 in DESI DR2

Assessment applying the Sagan standard:

**Positive**: This is a genuine PRE-DICTION (formulated before checking DESI DR2 data). The ratio r_1/r_s = 2.211 is fixed by known baryon loading R* = 0.63. No free parameters. Standard LCDM has no predicted feature at 325 Mpc. If detected, it would be significant evidence for substrate physics.

**Negative**: (1) The physical basis is that c_s = c/sqrt(3) for the photon-baryon plasma IS fabric second sound. But c_s = c/sqrt(3) is conformal invariance -- ANY radiation-dominated fluid gives this (both agents acknowledge this in the workshop). The framework adds no new physics to the sound speed. (2) The "first sound at c" is the metric perturbation propagating at the speed of light. In standard GR, metric perturbations also propagate at c. The claim that this produces a detectable feature at 325 Mpc requires that the metric perturbation couples to the baryon density with specific amplitude A_FS = 0.204. This amplitude is derived from the two-fluid formula, which assumes the substrate literally behaves as a superfluid with two sound modes. (3) The expected SNR of 2-5 means that non-detection at SNR < 5 does not falsify the framework (the signal could simply be too weak to detect). Only detection or definitive non-detection at SNR > 5 is informative. (4) The look-elsewhere effect: the prediction is at a specific scale (325 Mpc), which reduces the trial factor. But searches for "bumps in xi(r)" at various scales have found claimed features before (e.g., at ~120 Mpc, ~300 Mpc) that did not replicate. The field has a history of false positives at intermediate scales.

**BF for KK-CMB-TF-43**: 1.3. The prediction is distinctive but untested. It cannot contribute to the posterior until it is tested against data. What it does is create a pre-registerable test -- the most important contribution of this session. I assign BF = 1.3 for the structural coherence of having a zero-parameter prediction at a specific scale, rather than for any confirmed match.
