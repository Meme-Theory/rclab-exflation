The Jensen deformation changes the METRIC on SU(3) but not its topology, so the compactness guarantee persists across all tau. This provides a FLOOR for scattering persistence at higher tau (softening the KC-3 gap) but does not determine the scattering RATE. Not present at this level of sharpness in any individual collab.

### III. Unresolved Tensions

**T-1. Cosmological constant interpretation (E-5: 10^113 orders too large).**

Three genuinely different positions, all internally consistent. Split: 2-vs-1 (Hawking + Cosmic-Web lean Volovik; Einstein maintains GR coupling).

- **Einstein**: Genuine 113-order problem. General covariance (Paper 06) requires gravity to couple to the ABSOLUTE stress-energy tensor T_{uv}, not departures from a reference state. The field equations G_{uv} + Lambda*g_{uv} = kappa*T_{uv} admit no ambiguity: the vacuum energy IS the cosmological constant. No escape without new physics that modifies GR's coupling to vacuum energy.

- **Hawking**: "Neither surprising nor fatal." The no-boundary proposal (Paper 09) exponentially favors small Lambda. However, applied to the bare spectral action alone, it selects tau = 0 (S25 H-1 NEGATIVE). Hawking shifted toward the Volovik structural interpretation across the three rounds: the ground state energy is not the observable cosmological constant.

- **Cosmic-Web/Volovik**: Structural feature of any condensate. The thermodynamic identity rho_vac(equilibrium) = 0 is exact for any system in equilibrium with a particle reservoir (Volovik Paper 02). The observed Lambda is the departure from this equilibrium. BCS exponential sensitivity Delta ~ exp(-1/(V*g)) provides a mechanism for small Lambda. Cosmic-Web's Round 2 concession: this presupposes EMERGENT gravity, which the framework has not established.

**Key deliberation insight**: The CC severity depends on whether gravity is fundamental (Einstein: 113-order problem) or emergent (Volovik: structural, potentially resolvable). This is a genuine interpretive fork that the framework has not decided. Both positions moved: Einstein acknowledged Volovik's structural point (ground state energy >> excitation energy is generic in condensed matter); Cosmic-Web conceded the thermodynamic identity presupposes emergent gravity. The residual disagreement is precisely characterized: does gravity couple to absolute vacuum energy (GR) or only to excitations above the ground state (Volovik)?

**Cosmic-Web's refinement on fine-tuning**: The BCS mechanism requires one-parameter tuning (V*g in the exponent), which is structurally LESS severe than the standard CC fine-tuning (cancellation of ~10^60 independent contributions). One-parameter tuning vs. multi-term cancellation. This distinction is accepted by all three as a valid structural observation, though Einstein notes it does not eliminate the 113-order discrepancy -- it only recharacterizes the nature of the tuning.

**T-2. Bekenstein bound vs. Bekenstein-Hawking for internal space.**

Hawking initially proposed (H-11) comparing BCS microstate counting with the Bekenstein-Hawking area law S ~ A_internal/(4*G). Einstein objected: there is no horizon on the internal SU(3), so the B-H area law does not apply. Hawking accepted the correction; H-11 deferred to post-renormalization (requires L-8 resolution first). The Bekenstein bound S_max ~ 2*pi*R*E ~ 4200 (Hawking's Eq. 6-7) remains valid as an information-theoretic upper bound. Minor tension, does not affect main conclusions. The microstate counting question remains interesting but is downgraded from "striking structural match" to "information-theoretic constraint." Additionally, Hawking retracted H-12 (Penrose process analog for energy extraction from Jensen deformation) after Cosmic-Web's correction: the re-entrant (2,0) sector cycle is adiabatic thermodynamic work, not Penrose extraction.

**T-3. The scale bridge problem (53-56 orders of magnitude).**

The internal geometry's preferred scales are at ~10^{-31} m (KK compactification radius), while cosmological structure lives at 10^{22}-10^{25} m. Bridging 53-56 orders of magnitude requires a physical mechanism. The only identified candidate is the first-order BCS transition: bubble nucleation at a specific cosmic time t_BCS imprints features at the comoving scale k_transition = a(t_BCS) * H(t_BCS). This is the standard inflationary bridge (quantum fluctuations stretched to cosmological scales by expansion), applied to the BCS transition epoch. The severity is acknowledged by all three: Cosmic-Web's collab (Section 5.4) identifies this as "the deepest question," Hawking frames it as an unresolved tension, and Einstein notes the bridge requires the backreaction calculation that has not been done. The first-order character (L-9) is the structural reason the bridge may work -- bubble nucleation couples the internal transition to the 4D spacetime metric through the latent heat and bubble wall dynamics, unlike a smooth crossover which would decouple.
