    S_b = f_{12}*Lambda^{12}*a_0 + f_8*Lambda^8*a_2 + f_4*Lambda^4*a_4 + ...

After fiber integration, the tau-dependent part of the effective 4D potential comes from two sources:

(i) The a_2 term: proportional to R_K(tau), which is linear in curvature. This is the Einstein-Hilbert contribution from the internal space. It is MONOTONICALLY DECREASING for tau > 0 (proven Session 20a: dR_K/dtau > 0 but the potential has a -R_K sign from the EH action).

(ii) The a_4 term: proportional to (500*R_K^2 - 32*|Ric|^2 - 28*K), which is curvature-squared. This is what Session 20a computed (Gilkey trace for D^2 = nabla*nabla + R/4 on spinors, dim(S) = 16). Session 20a proved that for tau > 0, both da_2/dtau and da_4/dtau are positive -- so they cannot balance each other at the same order of Lambda. BUT the ratio rho = c_4/c_2 = f_4/(60*f_2*Lambda^2) is a free parameter, and for rho ~ O(1) the two terms CAN balance.

The crucial point: Session 20a's SD-1 analysis concluded "CLOSED for tau > 0" because da_2/dtau and da_4/dtau have the same sign. But this conclusion assumed the coefficients f_2 and f_4 are both positive. The spectral action principle (Paper 07) only requires f to be a positive function, but the moments f_k = integral f(u)*u^{k-1} du are indeed all positive for any positive f. So the signs cannot flip.

However, what CAN happen is that a_2 and a_4 grow at different rates. If a_4 grows FASTER than a_2, then V_spec(tau) = c_2*a_2(tau) + c_4*a_4(tau) can have a minimum where the derivative balance:

    c_2*(da_2/dtau) + c_4*(da_4/dtau) = 0

This requires c_2 and c_4 to have OPPOSITE signs. Since c_2 comes from the -R_K contribution to the EH term (negative coefficient in the potential) and c_4 comes from the a_4 term (positive coefficient), this is exactly the structure: -|c_2|*(da_2/dtau) + c_4*(da_4/dtau) = 0. The minimum exists when da_4/da_2 = |c_2|/c_4 at some tau_0.

Tesla's claim that this computation is "20 lines and 30 seconds" is correct. The data exists in r20a_riemann_tensor.npz (R_K, |Ric|^2, K at 21 tau values). The computation is a one-parameter scan over rho = c_4/c_2.

### Tight-Binding Hamiltonian: WELL-POSED BUT REQUIRES CARE

Two cautions:

(a) The "spectral momentum" that Tesla invokes (conjugate to the eigenvalue index) is not a standard NCG quantity. The eigenvalue index is NOT a spatial coordinate.
