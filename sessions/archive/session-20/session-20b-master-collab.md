## III. New Physics From the Collaboration

Six genuinely new insights emerged from cross-pollination that are not present in the original session minutes:

### 1. The Ginzburg Criterion Cuts Both Ways (Landau, unique)
Landau identifies a critical subtlety missed in all previous sessions. While d_int = 8 > d_uc = 4 makes mean-field exact for internal fluctuations (a strength invoked since Session 7), the MODULI fluctuations live in d_eff = 1 (one modulus s), where d_eff < d_uc and fluctuations dominate. The Ginzburg criterion for moduli dynamics is completely different from the one for internal modes. This means the perturbative V_eff is reliable for INTERNAL physics but may not be reliable for the MODULUS dynamics. This distinction has not been made in any previous session.

### 2. The Cubic Term Forces First-Order (Landau, unique)
Landau notes that s is not Z_2-symmetric (g_Jensen(s) differs from g_Jensen(-s)), so a cubic term is allowed in the free energy. Session 17a confirmed V'''(0) = -7.2. By Landau's classification theorem, a non-zero cubic term forces the phase transition to be first-order, via nucleation at a spinodal. A first-order transition does not require dV/ds = 0 from the one-loop potential alone -- it requires a metastable minimum separated by a barrier from non-perturbative effects. The Session 20b CLOSED rules out a continuous transition, not a first-order one.

### 3. The Volovik Gap Equation Is Untested (Tesla, unique)
Tesla proposes that the CLOSED applies only to dE/dtau = 0 (variational condition) but not to the self-consistency gap equation tau = F(tau) (functional fixed point). In He-3B, the gap equation gives non-trivial solutions even when the free energy is monotonic in the gap parameter, because self-consistency is more restrictive than stationarity. The KK analog -- where the spectrum at tau determines the geometry that determines the spectrum -- has not been formulated or tested. This is a genuinely different mathematical question from V_eff minimization.

### 4. The Spectral Action at Finite Cutoff May Differ (Connes, unique)
Connes identifies that the asymptotic expansion Tr f(D^2/Lambda^2) ~ 2f_4 Lambda^4 a_0 + ... is not converged (Session 18 C-1: spectral dimension 0.2-1.0 instead of 8). The EXACT spectral action -- the full trace with a step-function cutoff N(Lambda, tau) = number of eigenvalues below Lambda -- could have a minimum at intermediate tau for specific Lambda values. This zero-cost computation tests whether the asymptotic expansion hides a minimum present in the exact function.

### 5. Rolling Modulus as Prediction, Not Failure (Einstein, SP, Dirac cross-pollination)
Einstein proposes that the monotonically increasing V_eff is the framework's greatest cosmological asset: the modulus naturally rolls toward tau = 0, producing quintessence with w(z) evolving from w > -1 at high redshift toward w = -1 at late times -- precisely matching DESI DR2 data (w_0 ~ -0.7 to -0.8, w_a ~ -0.5 to -1.0). SP adds that Hubble damping 3H ds/dt could arrest the modulus at small s without a potential minimum. Dirac computes the experimental bound: |ds/dt| < 5 x 10^{-18}/yr from atomic clock constraints on alpha-variation, consistent with slow-roll dark energy. The reinterpretation: the CLOSED on static stabilization may convert to a prediction of dynamical dark energy.

### 6. Neutrino Mass Ratio as Independent s_0 Constraint (Neutrino, unique)
Neutrino proposes computing Delta m^2_32 / Delta m^2_21 as a function of tau from existing Tier 1 Dirac data. The observed ratio is 32.6. If R(tau) passes through 32.6 at some tau_neutrino, this value constrains s_0 independently of any stabilization mechanism. If R(tau) never reaches 32.6, the framework is closed for neutrino physics regardless of stabilization -- a hard closure from an orthogonal direction.
