## Annotated Inflection Points

### Peak: S19d (45-52%)

After the TT 2-tensor discovery, the spectral triple appeared to contain a new stabilization mechanism. All researchers converged on the TT Casimir sum as the next computation. The peak reflects maximum optimism before the constant-ratio trap was identified in S20b.

### First Major Collapse: S20b → S21c (32-38%)

The TT Casimir revealed the F/B = constant trap: at all cutoffs, bosonic and fermionic contributions maintain a fixed ratio determined purely by Weyl's law and fiber dimensions. No TT mechanism can escape this. The panel revised downward, though signed sums (S21a) provided a partial recovery route.

### Venus Moment: S23a (8%/5%)

The Kosmann-BCS computation found M_max 6.5-12.9x below the van Hove threshold at mu=0, and the selection rule V(gap,gap) = 0 exactly. This was the most decisive single-session downward revision in the project. Sagan called it the "Venus standard moment" — the computation was specific, pre-registered, and the failure was unambiguous.

### Recovery: S24b → S25 Redux (3% → 8-12%)

Sagan's redux analysis in S25 identified that the 21 closed mechanisms map to only 4 independent structural topics, reducing the effective evidence weight. W5 (Berry curvature vanishing) and W6 (thermodynamic stabilization) were added as walls, suggesting the project had been over-counting negative evidence.

### Second Collapse: S25 → S26-27 (12-18% → 3-5%)

P2/P3 computations in S26 closed BCS cooling trajectories (184/184 FAIL) and the Kerner bridge. S27 T-1 gate partially recovered (first positive boundary gate). Net: structural floor around 3-5%.

### Recovery: S28 → S33b (4-7% → 18%)

The KC chain (KC-1 through KC-5) in S28 showed 4/5 PASS with KC-3 conditional. The retraction of K-1e in S33b and the full Kosmann kernel computation (TRAP-33b M_max = 2.06) drove the largest single-session upward revision since S21a.

### Retraction Cycle: S33b → S34 (18% → ~18%, neutral)

TRAP-33b was immediately retracted in S34 (wrong V matrix: frame-space A_antisym not spinor K_a). The van Hove correction and mu=0 closures approximately cancelled. Session 34 is explicitly described as "approximately neutral, wider uncertainty bands."

### Recovery: S35 (18% → 32%)

Three independent methods (Thouless eigenvalue, ED, renormalization group) resolved N_eff > 5.5. BCS instability confirmed as a 1D theorem (RG-BCS-35). ED paired ground state verified. The largest sustained upward revision in sessions 25-51.

### TAU-STAB Collapse: S36 (32% → 12%)

The mechanism chain was downgraded from UNCONDITIONAL to CONDITIONAL when the Friedmann-BCS computation found the energy shortfall was 38,600x. The self-consistency gate (BAYES-SC-36) initially gave 28% but post-TAU-STAB revision settled at 12%.

### Structural Floor: S37 (12% → 5-8%)

The Spectral Action Monotonicity Theorem closed the entire spectral action route permanently. The instanton gas and Ordered Veil paradigm opened new physics but provided no stabilization mechanism. Sagan placed the framework at the "structural floor" of the spectral action branch.

### n_s Crisis: S44-S45 (23% → 3-8%)

S44 produced the largest upward revision in sessions 37-51: CDM algebraically derived (T^{0i}=0), SAKHAROV-GN within factor 3, DM/DE ratio within 3x. Sagan reached 23%. The collapse came in S45 when KZ-NS-45 found n_s = -0.59 (370 sigma from Planck), closing the Bogoliubov pair-creation route as the 10th+ failed n_s mechanism.

### Final Assessment: S50-S51 (3-5% → 2-4%)

w_0 = -0.509 excluded by BAO at chi^2/N = 23.2. w_a = 0 triple-locked (DESI sees -0.73). The SA-Goldstone route opens n_s viability at K < K* = 0.087 M_KK conditional on >= 3.1 e-folds. With margin 0.2 at tau_i = 10^{-5}, the route is possible but not established. The w_0/w_a exclusions are permanent regardless of the K_pivot outcome.

## Trajectory Summary

The framework probability followed a double-peaked arc: rising from 2-5% (prior) to 45-52% (S19d peak) on structural mathematical results, collapsing to 3% (S24b) as all perturbative mechanisms were exhausted, recovering to 18-32% (S33b-S35) when non-perturbative BCS physics was demonstrated, and settling to 2-4% (S51) as cosmological predictions (w_0, w_a, n_s, alpha_s) failed observational tests. The mathematical framework survives at 100% regardless of cosmological fate, and the single open route (SA-Goldstone at K < K*) awaits the e-fold mapping in S52.
