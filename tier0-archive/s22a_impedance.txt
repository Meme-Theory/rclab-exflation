

======================================================================
QA-5: SOUND SPEED RATIO c_u1/c_su2 AT tau = 0.30
======================================================================

1. TESLA'S PROPOSAL

sin^2(theta_W) = c_u1^2 / (c_u1^2 + c_su2^2)

where c_u1 = E_min in U(1)-dominated sectors (Y != 0)
and c_su2 = E_min in SU(2)-dominated sectors (Y = 0, excluding singlet)

2. RESULT

c_u1  = 0.850255 (from (0,1)/(1,0) sectors, Y = +/- 1/3)
c_su2 = 0.886248 (from (1,1) adjoint sector, Y = 0)
c_u1/c_su2 = 0.959387

sin^2(theta_W)_Tesla = 0.4793
sin^2(theta_W)_exp   = 0.2310

Deviation: 107.5%. DOES NOT REPRODUCE.

3. WHY IT FAILS

The sound-speed proxy c ~ E_min treats the gap-edge eigenvalue in each
sector as a group velocity. But at the gap edge, the U(1) and SU(2)
sectors have nearly EQUAL eigenvalues (0.850 vs 0.886), because the
Jensen deformation at tau=0.30 has only mildly broken the SU(3)
symmetry. The eigenvalue ratio (0.96) is far from the coupling ratio
needed (0.55) to reproduce the Weinberg angle.

The correct formula is the Freund-Rubin gauge coupling ratio:
  g_1/g_2 = e^{-2*tau}  [Session 17a, proven]
  sin^2(theta_W) = g_1^2 / (g_1^2 + g_2^2)
  At tau = 0.30: sin^2 = 0.2315 (0.2% from experiment)

The Weinberg angle is determined by the COUPLING CONSTANTS, not by
the sound speeds. The couplings depend on the Jensen metric through
the fiber integral (Baptista Paper 14, eq 2.26), which projects out
the U(1) and SU(2) components differently. The eigenvalue spectrum
at the gap edge does not encode this projection correctly.

4. FREUND-RUBIN CONSISTENCY CHECK

  tau for exact sin^2(theta_W) = 0.231: tau_W = 0.3007
  This is at the FR minimum (tau_FR ~ 0.30, from Session 21c)
  The Weinberg angle SELECTS tau ~ 0.30 independently of the gap edge

5. VERDICT

Tesla's sound-speed proposal: FAILS (107% deviation)
Freund-Rubin coupling formula: PASSES (0.2% at tau = 0.30)

The Weinberg angle is NOT a phonon anisotropy effect. It is a
gauge coupling effect determined by the fiber integration of the
Jensen metric. The sound speeds are too similar at the gap edge
to produce the observed asymmetry between U(1) and SU(2).

No probability shift from this computation (consistency check).
