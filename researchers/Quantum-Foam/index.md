## Topic Map

### A. Foundational Quantum Foam (Wheeler-Hawking-Ng)
Papers: 01, 02, 03
The conceptual origin of spacetime foam. Wheeler (1957) introduces quantum geometrodynamics and Planck-scale topology fluctuations. Hawking (1978) provides the first semiclassical path-integral treatment, establishing one topological defect per Planck 4-volume. Ng-van Dam (1994) derive fundamental measurement limits from foam and introduce the distance-fluctuation scaling laws.

### B. Amelino-Camelia DSR Program
Papers: 04, 05, 06
The phenomenological program for testing Planck-scale physics. Paper 04 proposes GW interferometers as quantum-gravity detectors. Paper 05 introduces doubly special relativity (DSR) with observer-independent minimum length. Paper 06 is the canonical 125-page review of quantum-spacetime phenomenology, defining the methodology for extracting testable predictions.

### C. Holographic Foam Models
Papers: 07, 16, 21
Ng's holographic foam (Paper 07) connects spacetime foam to the holographic principle and black hole quantum computation. Steinbring (Papers 16, 21) develops the multiwavelength averaging technique showing holographic foam blur is consistent with GRB 221009A observations, contrary to naive exclusion claims.

### D. Carlip Cosmological Constant from Foam
Papers: 08, 11, 14, 15, 25
Carlip's program for hiding a large CC via Planck-scale expanding/contracting regions. Paper 08 is the original PRL proposal. Paper 11 extends to midisuperspace. Paper 14 (2025) proves the mechanism for general inhomogeneous spacetimes. Paper 15 is Carlip's comprehensive 42-page review of spacetime foam across all frameworks. Paper 25 addresses continuum emergence from causal sets, explaining why manifoldlike configurations dominate the path integral.

### E. Observational Constraints -- Imaging
Papers: 09, 10, 12
Direct observational tests using point-source imaging. Perlman et al. (Papers 09, 12) use HST quasars and Chandra/Fermi data to rule out random-walk foam and constrain holographic foam. Lieu et al. (Paper 10) establish TeV photon observations as foam probes, deriving angular blur and spectral signatures.

### F. Lorentz Invariance Violation -- High Energy
Papers: 18, 23, 26, 27, 28, 29, 31
Seven papers forming the most stringent LIV constraint chain. Vasileiou (Paper 23) sets E_QG > 2.8 E_P from Fermi GRBs. LHAASO (Paper 18) achieves E_QG,1 > 10 E_P from GRB 221009A. Abrantes (Paper 26) provides independent cross-check. KM3NeT (Paper 27) constrains neutrino-sector LIV to Lambda_2 > 5e19 GeV. IceCube (Paper 28) searches for decoherence from foam. Bustamante (Paper 29) rules out anisotropic LIV at 10^{-31}. Paper 31 updates spectral-lag constraints from 56 GRBs.

### G. Interferometric Detection
Papers: 13, 17, 20
Zurek-Verlinde pixellon program. Paper 13 derives metric fluctuations from modular Hamiltonians and area-law variance. Paper 20 predicts geontropic noise spectrum S_h ~ f^{-1/2} detectable by Einstein Telescope. Paper 17 reports GQuEST's first tabletop interferometric search for pixellons, achieving 10^{-18} m displacement sensitivity.

### H. Alternative Approaches and Foundations
Papers: 19, 22, 24, 30, 32, 33
Paper 22 (Volovik) establishes the superfluid-vacuum paradigm: metric, Weyl fermions, gauge fields all emerge from condensate order parameter. Paper 24 (Padmanabhan) derives Einstein equations from entropy maximization with CC as information cost. Paper 19 (Dowker-Sorkin) proposes everpresent Lambda from causal set discreteness. Paper 30 (Hossenfelder) proves no locally-finite Poincare-invariant network exists, forcing discrete models to accept LIV or place discreteness in internal geometry. Paper 32 (Zloshchastiev) develops superfluid vacuum cosmology with inflation-to-DE transition. Paper 33 (Surya) is the canonical causal set review.
