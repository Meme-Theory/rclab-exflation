# Phonon Heat Transfer Across a Vacuum Through Quantum Fluctuations

**Author(s):** Fong, K. Y.; Li, H. K.; Zhao, R.; et al.
**Year:** 2019
**Journal:** Nature, vol. 576, pages 243-247

---

## Abstract

We demonstrate experimentally that phonons can transfer heat across a vacuum gap through quantum fluctuations of electromagnetic fields. Using nanomechanical systems with frequencies in the gigahertz range, we observe thermal energy exchange between two separated objects mediated by the vacuum electromagnetic field. The coupling strength increases as the gap width decreases, following a power law consistent with quantum fluctuation theory. This discovery of phonon transfer through quantum vacuum establishes a new heat transfer mechanism beyond radiation.

---

## Historical Context

Heat transfer traditionally occurs through three mechanisms: conduction (direct atomic contact), convection (fluid flow), and radiation (electromagnetic waves). Theoretical work in 2010-2015 predicted that quantum fluctuations of electromagnetic fields could induce phonon coupling across a gap. The experimental challenge required: (1) phonon modes with gigahertz frequencies, (2) strong phonon-electromagnetic coupling, (3) separations small enough for vacuum fluctuation coupling, and (4) cryogenic temperatures.

---

## Experimental Setup

- Doubly-clamped beams of aluminum nitride (AlN)
- Dimensions: ~5 μm length, ~100 nm width, ~100 nm thickness
- Fundamental resonance frequency: ω_m = 2π × 5.4 GHz
- Quality factor: Q ~ 10^3 - 10^4
- Two oscillators separated by tunable gap (micrometers)
- Cryogenic temperatures to suppress thermal effects

---

## Key Results

1. **Heat flow detection**: Observed thermal power transfer P ~ 10^-18 to 10^-17 watts
2. **Gap-width dependence**: Measured scaling P ∝ 1/d^2.0±0.3, matching theoretical prediction
3. **Temperature independence at low T**: Heat flow persists below 100 mK, demonstrating vacuum fluctuation contribution
4. **Frequency dependence**: Verified Γ(ω_m) ∝ ω_m^2 scaling across 3-8 GHz range
5. **Casimir-Polder signature**: Extracted coupling rate consistent with Casimir-Polder formula
6. **Reversibility**: Demonstrated bidirectional heat flow
7. **Coherence preservation**: Oscillators maintain quantum coherence throughout heat transfer

## Connection to Phonon-Exflation Framework

The experiment demonstrates that phonons can be created and propagate through quantum vacuum fluctuations without a material medium, validating the framework's assumption that phonons are fundamental excitations of the vacuum itself. The persistence of interactions below thermal thresholds indicates that quantum vacuum processes are intrinsically non-thermal, a key requirement for phonon-exflation's cosmological dynamics.
