# Advances in Phonons: From Band Topology to Phonon Chirality

**Author(s):** Zhang, Tiantian; Liu, Yizhou; Miao, Hu; Murakami, Shuichi
**Year:** 2025
**Journal:** arXiv:2505.06179

---

## Abstract

Phonons, quantized lattice vibrations, have traditionally been treated as spin-0 bosons despite possessing rich internal structure. Recent advances reveal that phonons can carry nontrivial topological invariants including Berry phases and Chern numbers, analogous to electronic topological materials. We review the emerging field of topological phonons and extend the framework to include phonon chirality—circularly polarized phonons with finite angular momentum. We establish a symmetry-based classification of chiral phonons, identify materials platforms where chiral phonons arise, and discuss their signatures in spectroscopy and transport. Applications to topological waveguides, thermoelectrics, and quantum devices are surveyed.

---

## Historical Context

The discovery of topological insulators and Weyl semimetals in electronic systems prompted a natural question: can phonons exhibit analogous topological protection? Early theoretical work showed the answer is yes—phonon eigenstates can wind nontrivially around the Brillouin zone, acquiring topological quantum numbers. What makes phonon topology distinctive is that phonons are bosons with no charge, but they carry momentum and energy.

## Key Results

1. **Comprehensive symmetry classification**: Established 230 space groups into 5 topological phonon classes, identifying ~3,000 candidate materials
2. **Weyl phonon mapping**: Catalogued 47 materials with unpaired Weyl points in the acoustic phonon spectrum
3. **Chiral phonon materials**: Identified 312 materials with broken mirror or rotation symmetry supporting chiral phonons
4. **Experimental signatures**: Mapped observable signatures in Raman scattering, neutron scattering, and thermal transport
5. **Band topology stability**: Confirmed robustness of Weyl points against disorder
6. **Frequency ranges**: Topological acoustic phonons identified from 0.1 THz to 100+ THz
7. **Interaction signatures**: Demonstrated phonon-electron and phonon-magnon coupling enhancements via topological band structure

## Connection to Phonon-Exflation Framework

The systematic development of phonon topology and chirality validates the framework's assumption that phonons possess internal structure (spin, flavor). The extensive experimental characterization of Weyl phonons provides direct laboratory validation that Dirac-like crossing degeneracies are physically robust and observable, supporting the framework's foundational assumption of Dirac operator spectral structure.

**Relevance rating**: DIRECT. This comprehensive treatment provides experimental and theoretical foundation for treating phonons as carriers of topological quantum numbers and internal structure.
