## Abstract

This monograph proposes that gravity is an emergent phenomenon, not fundamental. Among existing condensed matter systems, superfluid 3He-A most closely represents the quantum vacuum. Its quasiparticles exhibit properties similar to elementary particles; collective modes behave like photons and gravitons. In the limit T->0, the superfluid acquires fundamental symmetries (Lorentz invariance, gauge invariance). The book bridges particle physics, cosmology, and condensed matter, proposing that fundamental physics laws emerge from a quantum liquid ground state.

## Key Arguments and Derivations

### Fermi Liquids and Relativistic Dispersion

In a Fermi liquid at zero temperature, quasiparticles near the Fermi surface have energy-momentum dispersion:

$$E(p) = \sqrt{(v_F p)^2 + m^* c^2}$$

In 3He-A superfluid, quasiparticles have linear dispersion:

$$E = v_F (|p| - p_F)$$

Remarkable: v_F ~ 10^5 m/s, and the form is identical to massless particles in Minkowski spacetime if you identify v_F with c.

Deep in the quantum liquid, interactions are generic—not manifestly Lorentz-invariant. ## Impact and Legacy

Volovik's 2003 book opened a new perspective on fundamental physics. The success of 3He-A as a model inspired research into emergent gravity, topological matter, and quantum information. By 2023, topological phonons and phononic analog gravity experiments validated many of Volovik's predictions.
