## Abstract

This comprehensive Living Review explores analogues of general relativistic gravitational fields within other physical systems, particularly in condensed matter systems. The research program aims to gain new insights into gravitational problems by studying analogous phenomena in distinct physical domains. Over 1000 references are cited, covering the history, results, and future prospects of analogue models.

---

## Historical Context

The idea that gravity might be mimicked by other physical systems dates to the early days of general relativity. By 2005, when this review was first published, the field had grown substantially through work by Unruh, Visser, and others showing that acoustic wave equations in curved spacetime reduce to the same form as in flat spacetime with an "acoustic metric." The 2011 version (v4, latest) has expanded to over 1000 references and 220 pages, reflecting field maturation. Experimental groups were actively building sonic black holes and observing Hawking radiation analogs.

## Key Results

1. Hydrodynamic analogues: Acoustic waves in flowing fluids create effective metrics analogous to general relativity.
2. Electromagnetic analogues: Electromagnetic wave propagation in dispersive media exhibits analogue horizons.
3. Optical analogues: Photonic systems with varying refractive index create Hawking radiation signatures.
4. Bose-Einstein condensates: Phonons in BEC flow create acoustic horizons.
5. Casimir effect: Quantum vacuum energy can be understood via analogue metrics.
6. Hawking temperature universality: Temperature depends on surface gravity, independent of microscopic details.

## Impact and Legacy

By 2011, this review had become the standard reference in analogue gravity. It unified decades of research and provided a comprehensive roadmap for experimental and theoretical work. Impacts include Steinhauer's 2019 Nature measurement of Hawking radiation, topological effects in analogue systems, quantum information perspectives on black holes, and the broader emergent gravity program.
