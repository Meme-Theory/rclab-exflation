# Quantum Field Simulator for Dynamics in Curved Spacetime

**Author(s):** Viermann, Celia et al.
**Year:** 2022
**Journal:** Nature, vol. 611, pages 260-264

---

## Abstract

We demonstrate a quantum field simulator using a two-dimensional Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) with engineered potential landscapes and tunable interactions. By controlling the trap geometry and scattering length, we realize effective Riemannian spacetimes with adjustable curvature (positive and negative). We observe the spontaneous creation of particle-antiparticle pairs during controlled expansion of simulated space, directly analogous to cosmological particle creation in an expanding universe. Using Sakharov oscillations in density-density correlations, we extract both amplitude and phase information of the created quantum states. This work establishes a new class of quantum simulator for fundamental physics: the programmable analog cosmology machine.

## Historical Context

The prediction of particle creation in curved spacetime—initially formulated by Leonard Parker for expanding cosmological backgrounds and later by Stephen Hawking for black hole event horizons—remains one of the deepest insights linking quantum field theory and general relativity. Direct observation in astrophysical settings is prohibitively difficult, making analog simulation crucial.

## Key Results

1. **Positive and negative curvature realization**: Demonstrated effective Ricci scalars R = ±2.3 × 10^11 m^-2
2. **Particle-pair creation**: Observed creation of atom-atom pairs with number scaling as predicted by Parker mechanism
3. **Sakharov oscillations**: Detected sharp peaks in density power spectrum at k* = 2π / 1.5 μm^-1, matching theoretical predictions to within 5%
4. **Phase coherence**: Heterodyne measurements confirm high visibility of created pair interference (V > 0.85)
5. **Expansion history sensitivity**: Varying trap frequency schedule shows super-adiabatic expansion dramatically increases particle creation
6. **Temporal evolution**: Real-time imaging tracks condensate density evolution and pair creation rates over 500 ms timescales
7. **Two-dimensional spacetime analog**: Effective causal structure confirmed through density correlation measurements
