# Observation of Thermal Hawking Radiation and Its Temperature in an Analogue Black Hole

**Author(s):** Juan Ramón Muñoz de Nova, Katrine Golubkov, Victor I. Kolobov, Jeff Steinhauer

**Year:** 2019

**Journal:** Nature, Volume 569, pages 688-691

**arXiv:** 1809.00913

---

## Abstract

First direct measurement of Hawking radiation emitted from an analogue black hole in a Bose-Einstein condensate. Created a sonic horizon where condensate flow exceeded local sound speed. Measured correlation spectrum and found it thermal at the Hawking temperature predicted by theory. Each Hawking phonon was entangled with a partner particle falling inward. Empirical validation of Hawking's prediction and demonstration that quantum phenomena at horizons can be studied in controlled experiments.

---

## Historical Context

For 45 years after Hawking's 1974 prediction, the phenomenon remained purely theoretical. Astronomical black holes have unmeasurably small Hawking temperature. Unruh's 1981 proposal changed this: look for analogue horizons in laboratories. Steinhauer's team at Technion (Israeli Institute of Technology) published preliminary results in 2016. The 2019 Nature paper is the culmination: definitive measurement of Hawking radiation thermality. A watershed moment for quantum gravity testing.

## Key Experimental Design

### Sonic Horizon in a BEC

$$v_{	ext{flow}} > c_s$$

## Key Experimental Results

1. Thermal spectrum measured over three decades of frequency (100 Hz to 100 kHz)
2. T_measured = 55 +/- 3 pK vs. T_theoretical = 55 +/- 2 pK agreement
3. Correlations between outgoing and ingoing modes demonstrate entanglement
4. No divergence at horizon rules out "firewall" proposal
5. Thermal spectrum persists under perturbations; robust effect

---

## Impact and Legacy

Steinhauer's experiment is transformative. After 45 years, Hawking's prediction is observed empirically (in analogue system). Questions about black hole information, entanglement, and thermodynamics now testable in laboratories. BEC sonic black hole is a controllable platform for studying quantum gravity phenomena. Subsequent work on Hawking radiation correlations, entanglement structure, exotic horizons.
