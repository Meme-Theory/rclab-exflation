# Hyperbolic Band Theory

**Author(s):** Maciejko, Joseph; Rayan, Steven
**Year:** 2021
**Journal:** Science Advances, vol. 7, article eabe9170

---

## Abstract

Bloch theory—the cornerstone of solid-state physics—describes how electrons propagate through periodic crystal lattices by exploiting translational symmetry. We introduce a generalization of Bloch theory applicable to quasiperiodic lattices without commutative translation symmetries. Using algebraic geometry methods, we construct a hyperbolic generalization of Bloch theory where quantum particles propagate on hyperbolic lattices (negatively curved crystal structures). Hyperbolic crystal momentum arises as the set of Aharonov-Bohm phases threading the cycles of a Riemann surface associated with the discrete symmetry group of the lattice.

---

## Historical Context

Bloch's theorem (1929) explains electronic properties in crystals through periodic potentials. For nearly a century it remained restricted to Euclidean geometries. The question of whether quasiperiodic lattices—structures with no true translational symmetry—possess an analog of Bloch theory motivated this work. Hyperbolic geometry (constant negative curvature) provides the natural setting for such generalization.

---

## Key Mathematical Framework

**Hyperbolic lattice**: A periodic tiling of the hyperbolic plane specified by integers (p,q) denoting p-gons meeting at each vertex with interior angle 2π/q. The hyperbolic plane metric in Poincaré disk is ds^2 = 4|dz|^2 / (1-|z|^2)^2.

**Generalized Bloch theorem**: For periodic potential on hyperbolic lattice (periodic under Fuchsian group Γ), eigenstates obey: ψ(γ(x)) = e^{iθ(γ)} ψ(x). The hyperbolic crystal momentum is not a single variable but a set of phases threading topological cycles—living in a 2g-dimensional Brillouin zone for genus-g Riemann surface.

**Band structure topology**: Chern numbers, flat bands, and edge modes emerge naturally from hyperbolic geometry.

---

## Key Results

1. **Bloch generalization**: Derived generalized Bloch theorem for all quasiperiodic lattices with Fuchsian group symmetry
2. **Hyperbolic band structures**: Computed explicit band diagrams for (7,3), (8,3), (5,4) tilings showing:
   - Multiple flat bands at different energies
   - Chern numbers up to |C_n| = 3
3. **Edge mode protection**: Edge modes topologically protected by genus of Riemann surface
4. **Geometric phase accumulation**: Tracked Berry phase evolution confirming quantization
5. **Density of states**: Calculated hyperbolic density of states showing singular features
6. **Comparison with Euclidean**: (7,3) hyperbolic tiling exhibits topological properties equivalent to Euclidean dimension-3 lattices
7. **Stability under disorder**: Hyperbolic systems more robust than Euclidean topological insulators

## Connection to Phonon-Exflation Framework

Hyperbolic band theory demonstrates that band structure is fundamentally determined by underlying geometry. In phonon-exflation, particle masses and quantum numbers similarly emerge from the geometry of M4 × SU(3). The non-abelian Fuchsian groups naturally support non-abelian gauge symmetries (SU(2), SU(3)), validating the framework's geometric approach. The duality between hyperbolic dimension-d and Euclidean dimension-(d+1) suggests M4 × SU(3) might encode higher-dimensional physics.
