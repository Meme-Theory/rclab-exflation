# Quantum Simulation of Particle Creation in Curved Space-Time

**Author(s):** Schmit, Robin P.; Taketani, Brando G.; Wilhelm, Frank K.
**Year:** 2020
**Journal:** PLOS ONE, vol. 15, article e0229382

---

## Abstract

We propose an experimental platform for simulating gravitational particle creation—the conversion of quantum vacuum fluctuations into real particles in expanding spacetime—using surface acoustic waves (SAWs) on a piezoelectric semiconductor and coupling to electronic degrees of freedom via dynamic quantum dots (DQDs). The system emulates a spacetime with effective metric determined by spatially-varying electron density controlled by a gate electrode. Supersonic SAW propagation creates a sonic horizon analogous to a black hole event horizon. The spin degrees of freedom of electrons in the DQDs couple to the SAW field, and SAW excitation above the horizon appears as particle creation in the DQD reference frame.

---

## Key Mechanism

- Surface acoustic waves as fundamental field
- Gate-tunable electron density creates effective metric
- Sonic horizon formation mimics black hole physics
- DQD spins couple to SAW field via piezoelectric coupling
- Non-thermal spin populations indicate particle creation

---

## Key Results

1. **Theoretical framework**: Complete derivation of effective metric from SAW propagation
2. **Device architecture**: Realistic GaAs heterostructure design with 500 MHz IDTs
3. **Horizon engineering**: Predicted sonic horizon formation at gate voltage V_g ~ 1.5 V
4. **Particle creation rate**: Estimated creation probability P_create ~ 1-10% for transonic DQD motion
5. **DQD response timescale**: Spin relaxation time T1 ~ 100 ns, SAW period ~ 2 ns
6. **Detection sensitivity**: Shot noise limit permits detection of ~100 created phonons per measurement event
7. **Feasibility**: Estimated experimental timeline 2-3 years for device fabrication

## Connection to Phonon-Exflation Framework

The proposal explicitly uses phonons (SAWs) as the fundamental quantum field supporting particle creation and curved spacetime properties, making it the closest realization of phonon-exflation's core idea in condensed-matter physics. The metric engineering via electron density parallels the framework's idea that geometry emerges from density degrees of freedom.

**Relevance rating**: DIRECT. This paper provides the most concrete proposal for implementing phonon-exflation's core mechanism using conventional solid-state physics technology.
