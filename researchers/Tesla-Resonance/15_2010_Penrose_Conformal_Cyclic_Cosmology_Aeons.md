# Conformal Cyclic Cosmology: Aeons, Conformality, and Infinite Future (2010)

**Author:** Roger Penrose
**Year:** 2010 (major exposition); foundational ideas 2005
**Source:** Penrose, R. (2010) "Cycles of Time: An Extraordinary New View of the Universe" (Bodley Head); Gurzadyan, V.G. & Penrose, R. (2013) European Physical Journal Plus 128: 22

---

## Abstract

Conformal Cyclic Cosmology (CCC) proposes that the universe consists of infinite sequences of "aeons"—each aeon is a spacetime with its own Big Bang and infinite future. The key insight: the infinitely remote future of one aeon (when all massive particles have decayed and the universe is filled only with massless radiation) is mathematically equivalent to the Big Bang of the next aeon (conformal rescaling equates infinite future to singular beginning). The universe is thus cyclic in structure but infinite in duration. CCC avoids the singularity problem without invoking quantum gravity; it is a purely classical general relativity perspective. Hawking radiation plays a central role: supermassive black holes colliding near the aeon's infinite future generate distinctive electromagnetic signals (CCC claims evidence for these in CMB data).

---

## Historical Context

Roger Penrose has long been troubled by cosmological problems:

1. **The arrow of time**: Why does the universe have a direction of time? (entropy increases)
2. **Fine-tuning**: Why was the initial state so special (low entropy, high homogeneity)?
3. **Infinite future**: Standard Big Bang cosmology leads to either a recollapse or infinite expansion with eternal darkness

In 2005, Penrose proposed a revolutionary solution: abandon the idea of a finite past and infinite future. Instead, the universe is cyclic: each aeon is finite in duration (infinite future time, but in conformal geometry they "appear" finite), and the infinite future of one aeon rescales to the Big Bang of the next.

This reframes the problem: instead of asking "why special initial conditions?" ask "what connects the infinite future of one aeon to the Big Bang of the next?"

## Conformal Geometry and Rescaling

### Conformal Transformations

A conformal transformation is a change of metric that preserves angles locally:

$$\tilde{g}_{\mu\nu} = \Omega^2(x) g_{\mu\nu}$$

where $\Omega(x)$ is a smooth, positive function. Under this transformation:
- Distances scale by $\Omega(x)$
- Angles are preserved
- Lightcones are preserved (the distinction between timelike, spacelike, null remains unchanged)

Crucially, conformal transformations preserve the causal structure: if event A causally influences event B in the original metric, the same is true in the rescaled metric.

### De Sitter and Infinite Future

A key observation: the infinite future of de Sitter spacetime (exponentially expanding universe filled with massless radiation) has a mathematical boundary structure described by conformal rescaling.

In the conformal diagram, the infinite future (future infinity, $i^+$) is brought to a finite distance by the transformation $\Omega \to 0$ as we approach $i^+$.

Near $i^+$, the metric approaches a degenerate form that resembles the Big Bang singularity (though without divergent curvature—it's a smooth conformal boundary).

## Structure of a Single Aeon

### Initial Singularity and Big Bang

The aeon begins at a Big Bang singularity (in conformal coordinates). The metric near the singularity is:

$$ds^2 \approx -dt^2 + e^{2Ht} d\vec{x}^2$$

(de Sitter metric, exponential expansion)

This matches observations: the early universe was hot, dense, and rapidly expanding. No inflaton field needed; the metric itself provides exponential growth.

### Evolution Through the Aeon

As time progresses:
1. Massive particles (protons, neutrons, electrons if they decay) gradually disappear
2. Universe becomes filled with radiation (photons, massless neutrinos)
3. Black holes evaporate via Hawking radiation
4. Massive black holes merge, creating gravitational waves
5. At late times: only massless particles remain

### Aeon's Infinite Future

At $t \to \infty$:
- Scale factor $a(t) \to \infty$
- Matter density $\rho \to 0$ (massless radiation only)
- Spacetime approaches a near-conformal state: curvature is dominated by massless particle stress-energy

The geometry becomes approximately de Sitter with infinite extent.

## Connection Between Aeons

### Conformal Rescaling Maps Infinite Future to Big Bang

The infinite future of aeon $n$ (as $t_n \to \infty$) is mapped by conformal rescaling to the Big Bang of aeon $n+1$ (at $t_{n+1} = 0$).

Specifically, near future infinity:

$$g_n^{\mu\nu} (t \to \infty) \approx \Omega^2 g_{n+1}^{\mu\nu}(t \to 0)$$

with $\Omega \to 0$ as $t \to \infty$.

This identification is not a regular gluing of spacetime (which would require matching curvature tensors); instead, it's a conformal identification preserving angles and causal structure.

### Continuity of Hawking Radiation

Here's where CCC becomes testable: the Hawking radiation produced by black holes colliding near the infinite future of aeon $n$ should leave imprints on the conformal boundary—which becomes the beginning of aeon $n+1$.

These imprints manifest as anomalies in the CMB of aeon $n+1$: correlated regions of high-energy photons (from Hawking radiation of the prior aeon's black holes).

## Hawking Points and CMB Anomalies

### Prediction: Hawking Points

CCC predicts specific anomalies in the CMB:

1. **Hawking Points**: Regions of intense Hawking radiation from supermassive black hole mergers. These appear as high-temperature regions in the CMB sky, concentrated in certain directions.

2. **Concentric Circles**: Electromagnetic signals from black hole mergers form concentric circles in conformal space, which appear as specific angular patterns in the CMB.

3. **Number Excess**: CCC predicts an excess number of concentric circles above the statistical background. Penrose claims evidence for this in WMAP and Planck data (though this is controversial).

### Observational Tests

Penrose and collaborators have analyzed CMB maps looking for:
- Concentric circles of lower-than-average temperature variance (multiple photons from one source)
- Deviations from Gaussian randomness
- Anisotropic distributions of anomalies

Some papers claim to find evidence; many independent analyses find no significant signal beyond statistical noise.

## Quantum Gravity Avoided

### Classical General Relativity Only

A key feature of CCC: it avoids quantum gravity. The theory uses only classical general relativity and quantum field theory (for Hawking radiation), not quantum gravity.

This is both a strength (simpler framework, easier to work with) and potentially a weakness (doesn't address deep quantum gravity questions like wavefunction collapse or the measurement problem).

### Entropy and the Arrow of Time

CCC provides a perspective on the thermodynamic arrow of time:

Each aeon starts with low entropy (smooth, homogeneous Big Bang) and evolves toward high entropy (dissipated radiation, evaporated black holes, near-conformal state at future infinity).

The conformal rescaling naturally identifies low-entropy future (aeon $n$) with low-entropy past (aeon $n+1$ initial state). This breaks the apparent asymmetry: the universe is infinite in both directions (past and future), with low entropy at both "ends" (Big Bangs of successive aeons).

## Connection to Phonon-Exflation Framework

CCC and phonon-exflation address similar problems through different mechanisms:

1. **Avoiding singularities**: Both frameworks avoid point singularities (Big Bang). CCC via conformal rescaling (infinite future maps to another's beginning); phonon-exflation via geometric structure of SU(3) (which provides a smooth effective potential avoiding collapse).

2. **No inflaton field**: CCC and phonon-exflation both reject the inflaton paradigm. CCC uses conformal geometry; phonon-exflation uses internal compactification. Both find alternatives to scalar field inflation.

3. **Cyclicity and spacetime structure**: CCC's cycles (aeons) echo the oscillation of internal geometry (the Jensen parameter $s$ varying in phonon-exflation). Both frameworks have periodic or quasi-periodic structure.

4. **Hawking radiation as signal**: CCC uses Hawking radiation from prior aeons to constrain cosmic parameters. Phonon-exflation uses the spectral action, which sums over excitations analogous to Hawking radiation (particle creation). Both take quantum effects seriously in cosmology.

5. **Thermodynamics and entropy**: Both frameworks root themselves in thermodynamic principles. CCC uses the second law (entropy increases per aeon); phonon-exflation uses the spectral action principle (free energy minimization). Both are teleological frameworks: the universe evolves toward specific thermodynamic endpoints.

6. **Conformal vs metric structure**: CCC preserves angles (conformal structure) but scales the metric. Phonon-exflation deforms the metric (Jensen deformation on SU(3)) while preserving certain symmetries. Both modify geometric structure in ways that encode physics.

## Critical Assessment

**What holds up**:
- Conformal geometry is rigorously developed
- Cyclic structure elegantly sidesteps singularity and initial-condition problems
- No quantum gravity needed (uses only GR + QFT)
- Avoids the cosmological initial-condition fine-tuning

**What is controversial**:
- CMB anomaly claims are disputed; independent analyses mostly find no significant signal
- Statistical methods used to identify Hawking points are debated (Moss, Scott, others claim analysis errors)
- Matching conditions between aeons are not uniquely determined (ambiguities in how to identify boundaries)
- Entropy accounting across aeons is subtle (massless radiation has different thermodynamic properties)

**What is speculative**:
- Connection to particle physics and Standard Model is underdeveloped
- Actual observational tests remain inconclusive
- Predictions for structure formation and primordial gravitational waves are still being developed

---

## Legacy and Current Status

CCC remains actively pursued by Penrose and collaborators, with vigorous debate in the community:

1. **CMB analysis**: Continued searches for Hawking points using improved statistical methods
2. **Extended theory**: Incorporating gauge fields, fermions into CCC framework
3. **Gravitational waves**: Predicting CCC signatures in LIGO/Virgo data
4. **Criticisms and refinements**: Many papers debate the validity of CCC claims
