# The Cosmological Constant: A Lesson from the Effective Gravity of Topological Weyl Media

**Author(s):** G. Jannes, G. E. Volovik

**Year:** 2012

**Identifier:** arXiv:1108.5086 [cond-mat.other]

**Published in:** JETP Letters, Vol. 96, No. 4 (2012), pp. 215–219

---

## Abstract

Topological matter with Weyl points, exemplified by superfluid 3He-A, provides an explicit condensed-matter analog of a gravitating universe. Unlike simpler systems (e.g., Bose-Einstein condensates), Weyl media support an effective metric that is closely analogous to real spacetime curvature. The authors examine how the vacuum energy density of the topological medium relates to the effective cosmological constant in the emergent metric. They demonstrate that the cosmological constant **vanishes in the equilibrium vacuum**, contrary to the observed universe's accelerating expansion. This result has profound implications: either the observed universe is far from equilibrium, or additional physics (dynamical dark energy, modified gravity) must be invoked. The paper provides a thermodynamic argument that equilibrium states do not gravitate, suggesting a fundamental distinction between vacuum and gravitating states.

---

## Historical Context

By 2012, the cosmological-constant problem had reached an acute stage. Observations of type Ia supernovae (1998) had conclusively shown that the universe's expansion is accelerating, implying a positive vacuum energy density $\rho_\Lambda \sim 10^{-47}$ GeV$^4$. Yet quantum field theory, even in its Standard-Model formulation, predicts a vacuum energy density 120 orders of magnitude larger: $\rho_\text{QFT} \sim (10^{19} \text{ GeV})^4$. This discrepancy—the "worst theoretical prediction in the history of physics"—remained unsolved.

Parallel to observational cosmology, the study of topological matter in condensed systems had rapidly advanced. Volovik's insight was to use superfluid 3He-A as a **laboratory cosmos**: a macroscopic, controllable system where an effective spacetime geometry emerges from quasiparticle excitations. The Jannes-Volovik paper asks: in this analog system, what is the vacuum energy, and does it gravitate?

The answer—that the vacuum energy of an equilibrium state does not gravitate—was startling. It suggested that the observed universe might be in a **non-equilibrium state**, or that the vacuum energy we observe is not the true "zero-point" energy but rather a disequilibrium excitation.

## Key Arguments and Derivations

### Effective Metric in Superfluid 3He-A

Superfluid 3He-A exhibits a non-trivial order parameter in momentum space, with Weyl points (gap nodes) at specific momenta. Near a Weyl point, the quasiparticle energy is linear in momentum:

$$E(\mathbf{p}) \approx v_F |\mathbf{p}|$$

where $v_F$ is the Fermi velocity. To second order in gradients, the quasiparticle Hamiltonian takes the form:

$$H = v_F \sigma^i p_i + \text{terms involving } \nabla \rho_s, \nabla T$$

where $\rho_s$ is the superfluid density and $T$ is temperature. Volovik showed that gradients in macroscopic variables (density, temperature, order parameter) act like a spacetime metric.

The effective metric is:

$$g_{\mu\nu} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 + 2q(r) & 0 \\ 0 & \delta_{ij} \end{pmatrix}$$

where $q(r) = (\rho_s - \rho_0) / \rho_0$ is a "gravitational potential" related to the deviation of superfluid density from equilibrium.

### Bi-Metric Structure

Unlike simpler BECs where the metric is single-valued, 3He-A admits a **bi-metric geometry**: there exist two distinct quasiparticle branches (particle and antiparticle holes) that couple differently to the order parameter. This introduces two effective metrics:

$$g^{(\text{quasiparticle})}_{\mu\nu}, \quad g^{(\text{antiquasiparticle})}_{\mu\nu}$$

The difference between these two metrics encodes the "cosmological constant" of the effective theory.

### Vacuum Energy in Equilibrium vs. Non-Equilibrium

The key result is that in an **equilibrium state** (minimum of thermodynamic free energy), the vacuum energy density (sum of zero-point energies of all quasiparticle modes) does not contribute to the effective gravitational field. More precisely:

$$\rho_\Lambda^{\text{equilibrium}} \propto \langle \partial_0 S_{\text{matter}} \rangle = 0$$

where $S_{\text{matter}}$ is the matter action. By the equations of motion, at equilibrium $\partial_0 S = 0$, so the cosmological term vanishes.

However, in a **non-equilibrium state** (e.g., a perturbed or evolving vacuum), the vacuum energy becomes effective:

$$\rho_\Lambda^{\text{non-eq}} = \langle T_{\mu\nu}^{\text{vac}} \rangle_{\text{non-eq}} \neq 0$$

This non-equilibrium energy can source the emergent Einstein equations:

$$R_{\mu\nu} - \frac{1}{2} g_{\mu\nu} R = \frac{8\pi G}{\hbar c^4} T_{\mu\nu}^{\text{non-eq}}$$

### Connection to Real Cosmology

If the observed universe follows this analog model, then the observed dark energy must arise from a **non-equilibrium process**. Possible mechanisms include:

1. **Quantum field mixing:** As the universe cools and symmetries break, the vacuum state transitions from one equilibrium to another, generating transient energy density.

2. **Quantum fluctuations:** Vacuum fluctuations far from equilibrium (e.g., during inflation or reheating) can drive an effective cosmological constant.

3. **Dynamical fields:** A scalar field $\phi$ rolling down a potential $V(\phi)$ constitutes non-equilibrium vacuum energy; its energy density $\rho = V(\phi)$ acts like $\Lambda$.

The paper argues that **any realistic cosmological model must embed a non-equilibrium mechanism** to explain the observed acceleration.

### Thermodynamic Interpretation

The vanishing of $\rho_\Lambda$ in equilibrium is a consequence of the first law of thermodynamics:

$$dE = T \, dS - P \, dV + \mu \, dN$$

In the ground state, all chemical potentials are zero ($\mu = 0$ for the superfluid sector), and entropy is minimized ($dS = 0$). The remaining gravitational energy is purely geometric: it comes from the elastic energy stored in spatial curvature, not from the vacuum itself.

## Key Results

1. **Equilibrium Cosmological Constant Vanishes:** The vacuum energy of any system in thermodynamic equilibrium does not contribute to the effective cosmological constant; $\Lambda_{\text{eq}} = 0$ is a thermodynamic necessity.

2. **Non-Equilibrium Generates $\Lambda$:** Only non-equilibrium states, with $T \, dS \neq 0$ and evolving order parameters, produce an effective cosmological term.

3. **Bi-Metric Gravity in Weyl Media:** Topological Weyl media (like 3He-A) naturally produce effective bi-metric gravity, more closely analogous to general relativity than simpler BEC models.

4. **Vacuum Energy Density is Suppressed:** In the analog system, the vacuum energy density is of order $\hbar c / \xi^3$, where $\xi$ is the coherence length, vastly smaller than the Planck density—a natural resolution of the hierarchy.

5. **Universe Must Be Non-Equilibrium:** If the framework applies to cosmology, the observed accelerating expansion requires that the universe be **out of equilibrium**, with an active transient mechanism driving the expansion rate.

6. **Thermodynamic Bound on Dark Energy:** The energy density in dark energy is bounded above by the available "disequilibrium budget"—the total energy distance from the true ground state. Once the universe settles, acceleration must cease.

## Impact and Legacy

This paper shifted the conceptual landscape of dark energy research. Instead of asking "why is the vacuum energy so small?" it asked "why does the vacuum energy gravitate at all?" This inversion has motivated:

- **Emergent-Gravity Programs:** Subsequent work exploring whether gravity itself is a thermodynamic or entropic phenomenon (Verlinde 2011, Jacobson 1995, Padmanabhan 2009-2015).

- **Non-Equilibrium Cosmology:** Investigations into whether the universe undergoes a phase transition or relaxation toward equilibrium over cosmic time, potentially explaining the coincidence problem (why $\rho_\Lambda \sim \rho_m$ today).

- **Analog Gravity Validation:** Efforts to experimentally test predictions of analog gravity in laboratory systems (cold atoms, photonics, acoustics).

- **Modified Gravity Alternatives:** Papers exploring whether effective-metric modifications (f(R) gravity, scalar-tensor theory) provide a non-equilibrium avenue.
