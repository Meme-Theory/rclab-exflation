## Abstract

This paper proposes a unified model in which dark matter is a **supersolid phase** that fills the vacuum of spacetime itself, forming the fundamental fabric of spacetime geometry. In this picture, ordinary matter creates displacement waves in the supersolid medium as it moves, and gravitational forces arise from the supersolid's "displacing back" mechanism—a restoring force resisting the deformation. Dark energy is reinterpreted as pressure from supersolid dark matter flowing outward from a central black hole, driving cosmic acceleration. The supersolid is also the substrate for wave-particle duality (the medium that oscillates in quantum mechanics) and its chaotic dynamics source the probabilistic nature of quantum mechanics.

## Historical Context

The identification of dark matter and dark energy has been among the deepest puzzles in physics. The standard approach treats them as separate components: dark matter (non-relativistic particles) and dark energy (geometric cosmological constant or scalar field). This requires fine-tuning of initial conditions (the coincidence problem: why are $\rho_{DM}$ and $\rho_{DE}$ comparable at $z \sim 0$?) and leaves open the question of their origin.

Cavedon's proposal follows in a tradition of "fabric of spacetime" models dating back to Einstein's geometric interpretation of gravity and including 20th-century ideas of the "ether" (reframed in modern language). The supersolid interpretation is novel: combining properties of **solids** (rigidity, elasticity, support of transverse waves) with **superfluids** (frictionless flow, phase coherence).

In condensed-matter physics, a supersolid is a phase of matter that exhibits both crystalline order and superfluidity—it can flow without viscosity while maintaining a fixed density. Helium-4 under extreme pressure is a candidate supersolid. Cavedon proposes spacetime IS a supersolid: elastic under deformations (gravity), yet frictionless in flow (dark energy outflow).

This model is conceptually adjacent to the phonon-exflation framework but takes an opposite approach: rather than identifying particles as phonons of spacetime, Cavedon identifies spacetime itself as the phononic medium. The framework instead proposes that spacetime geometry (M₄ × SU(3) metric) is emergent from the electronic structure of this phononic medium.

## Key Arguments and Propositions

### Supersolid as Spacetime Substrate

**Proposition 1: Dark Matter ≈ Supersolid Substrate**

The vacuum is a supersolid condensate filling all space. This supersolid:

- Has a well-defined **density** $\rho_{DM}$, constant across space in the unperturbed state
- Supports **elastic deformations** (compressions and shears) with restoring forces
- Can **flow without dissipation** (superfluidity) when driven by pressure gradients
- Has a **crystal structure** at the microscopic scale, but appears continuous at astrophysical scales

**Proposition 2: Displacement and Gravitational Attraction**

When an object (ordinary matter) moves through this medium, it displaces the supersolid. The displacement is:

$$\mathbf{u}(\mathbf{r}, t) = \text{(local perturbation in supersolid density)}$$

By **Newton's third law** (action-reaction), the supersolid pushes back on the object, producing a restoring force. This "push-back" mechanism generates:

$$\mathbf{F}_{grav} = -\nabla U, \quad U \propto \nabla^2 u$$

where the potential $U$ is the elastic energy of the displaced supersolid. For two nearby objects, their displacement fields overlap, creating an attractive interaction—the gravitational force.

The authors claim this reproduces Newton's law of gravitation:

$$F = \frac{G m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

where $G$ is related to the supersolid's **bulk modulus** (elasticity constant).

### Wave-Particle Duality and Quantum Mechanics

**Proposition 3: Quantum Waves = Supersolid Oscillations**

In wave-particle duality, matter exhibits both particle-like localization and wave-like interference. In the supersolid model:

- The **particle** is a localized excitation (topological defect or soliton in the supersolid)
- The **wave** is the phononic oscillation of the supersolid surrounding the particle
- **Superposition** (an electron in two places simultaneously) is the supersolid oscillating in both regions

Double-slit interference arises because the supersolid wave can split and recombine around both slits, and the probability of detecting the particle is proportional to the squared amplitude of the supersolid wave field.

**Proposition 4: Quantum Randomness = Supersolid Chaos**

The probabilistic nature of quantum mechanics arises from the **intrinsic randomness** of the supersolid's microscopic dynamics. While the macroscopic supersolid appears ordered (crystalline), its microscopic fluctuations are chaotic (many degrees of freedom, sensitive dependence on initial conditions). This chaos manifests as quantum uncertainty: an observer cannot predict the outcome of a measurement because the supersolid dynamics are fundamentally chaotic at the relevant scale.

### Dark Energy and Cosmic Expansion

**Proposition 5: Dark Energy = Supersolid Outflow Pressure**

The universe contains a central **black hole** (possibly cosmological in scale). From this black hole, supersolid dark matter continuously flows outward, expanding the universe. This outflow creates **pressure** that pushes all matter outward, driving cosmic acceleration.

The authors propose:

$$\rho_{DE} = P_{outflow}$$

where $P_{outflow}$ is the pressure from the supersolid flux diverging from the central black hole.

This model naturally explains why dark energy density is approximately constant across cosmic time (it's sourced by a steady outflow) and why it drives acceleration (pressure does positive work on the expanding universe).

The model claims to unify gravity, quantum mechanics, and dark energy:

## Proposed Mechanisms

### Gravitational Force via Elastic Restoring

For a point mass $m$ at position $\mathbf{r}_0$, the displacement field is:

$$u(\mathbf{r}) \sim \frac{m}{|\mathbf{r} - \mathbf{r}_0|}$$

(similar to a 3D Green function).

The elastic energy stored in the deformed supersolid is:

$$U = \frac{1}{2} \int d^3r \, \mu (\nabla \mathbf{u})^2$$

where $\mu$ is the shear modulus (elasticity constant).

The restoring force on the supersolid at $\mathbf{r}$ due to a point mass at $\mathbf{r}_0$ is:

$$\mathbf{F}_{restore} = -\nabla U \propto -\frac{\mathbf{r} - \mathbf{r}_0}{|\mathbf{r} - \mathbf{r}_0|^3}$$

This is an inverse-square law, matching Newton's gravity. The gravitational constant is:

$$G \sim \frac{1}{\mu \rho_{DM}}$$

(shear modulus divided by supersolid density).

### Quantum Interference via Superfluid Phase

The superfluid component of the supersolid has a **phase coherence** $\phi(\mathbf{r})$ obeying:

$$\phi(\mathbf{r}) = \arg(|\Psi(\mathbf{r})|)$$

where $\Psi$ is the macroscopic wavefunction of the superfluid.

In a double-slit setup:

$$\Psi(\mathbf{r}) = \Psi_1(\mathbf{r}) + \Psi_2(\mathbf{r})$$

(sum of amplitudes from each slit).

Interference fringes form where $\phi_1(\mathbf{r}) - \phi_2(\mathbf{r}) = 2\pi n$ (constructive) or $\pi + 2\pi n$ (destructive).

2. **Intuition:** Avoids abstract mathematics; relies on familiar condensed-matter physics (supersolids, phonons, elasticity).
3. **Homogeneity:** No separate "dark matter" and "dark energy" entities—one medium with two aspects.

## Open Questions and Challenges

The paper does not address several critical issues:

1. **Quantitative Predictions:** How does one compute G from supersolid parameters? What are $\mu$ and $\rho_{DM}$ numerically?
2. **Relativistic Structure:** GR is manifestly Lorentz-invariant. How does the supersolid model preserve Lorentz invariance? (In most condensed-matter systems, the crystal lattice breaks Lorentz invariance.)
3. **Quantum Field Theory:** Standard particle physics is formulated as quantum fields, with creation/annihilation operators. How do these emerge from the supersolid?
4. **Hawking Radiation:** If spacetime is a supersolid, what is the supersolid description of black hole thermodynamics and Hawking radiation?
5. **Cosmological Tests:** What are the distinctive predictions (different from ΛCDM) that observations could test?
