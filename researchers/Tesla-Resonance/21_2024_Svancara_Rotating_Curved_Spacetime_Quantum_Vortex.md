# Rotating Curved Spacetime Signatures from a Giant Quantum Vortex

**Authors:** Patrik Švančara, Pietro Smaniotto, Leonardo Solidoro, James F. MacDonald, Sam Patrick, Ruth Gregory, Carlo F. Barenghi, Silke Weinfurtner

**Year:** 2024

**Journal:** Nature, Vol. 625, pp. 679–684

**arXiv:** 2308.10773

---

## Abstract

A stationary giant quantum vortex containing thousands of circulation quanta is stabilized in superfluid helium-4, overcoming instability limitations that plague other quantum platforms (magnons, atomic clouds, polaritons). The vortex configuration exhibits record-strength rotational flow in the quantum fluid regime. Through non-invasive wave-vortex interaction measurements using microscale surface waves, the authors detect bound states and distinctive analogue black hole ringdown signatures, demonstrating that intricate wave–vortex interactions simulate rotating curved spacetime physics in laboratory conditions.

---

## Historical Context

Analogue gravity has emerged as a powerful research programme for studying general relativistic phenomena in condensed matter systems. The program leverages laboratory systems where small excitations (sound, surface waves, light) behave as quantum fields propagating on an effective curved spacetime metric induced by the fluid flow background.

Superfluid helium-4 provides an ideal platform because of its vanishing viscosity—eliminating dissipation mechanisms that plague other candidate systems. Previous analog gravity demonstrations focused on static black holes, Hawking radiation near sonic horizons, and cosmological horizons. The 2024 Svančara experiment marks a significant advance: the first experimental realization of rotating curved spacetime geometry in a quantum fluid, encoded in a giant quantum vortex.

Multiply quantized vortices in bulk quantum fluids are generically unstable. In conventional superfluids, the vortex cores expand and the vortex state decays into singly quantized vortices. This instability has prevented exploration of the extreme rotational regimes necessary to study Kerr-like black hole analog signatures. Svančara's team overcome this by confining tens of thousands of quantized circulation units in a single, compact vortex core—essentially a "quantum tornado" with unprecedented angular momentum density.

The experimental advance opens the possibility of studying curved spacetime effects at finite temperature in a fully quantum system, where traditional relativistic quantum field theory calculations become intractable.

## Key Arguments and Derivations

### Giant Vortex Stabilization in He-4

The superfluid order parameter Psi(r) in helium-4 satisfies the Gross-Pitaevskii equation (GPE):

$$i\hbar \frac{\partial \Psi}{\partial t} = \left[ -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\nabla^2 + V_\text{ext}(r) + g|\Psi|^2 \right] \Psi$$

For a vortex state with winding number $\kappa$ (in units of $h/m$), the circulation around a closed loop is:

$$\oint \mathbf{v} \cdot d\mathbf{l} = \frac{\hbar \kappa}{m}$$

In a multiply quantized vortex, $\kappa > 1$. The key to Svančara's stabilization is the use of a carefully designed confining potential $V_\text{ext}(r)$ that holds the vortex core compact against expansion instability.

### Effective Metric from Vortex Flow

The fluid flow around the giant vortex induces an effective curved spacetime metric. For a rotating fluid, the effective metric experienced by acoustic waves (phonons) takes the form:

$$ds^2 = -c_s^2(r) dt^2 + dr^2 + (r^2 - v_\theta(r)^2/c_s^2) d\theta^2 - 2v_\theta(r) r d\theta dt$$

where $c_s$ is the sound speed and $v_\theta(r)$ is the azimuthal velocity profile from the vortex. This is structurally similar to the Kerr metric in Boyer-Lindquist coordinates.

### Wave-Vortex Interactions and Bound States

Surface waves with wavelength $\lambda$ and frequency $\omega$ propagate on the He-4 surface above the vortex. The dispersion relation in the presence of the vortex flow becomes modified:

$$\omega'(\mathbf{k}) = \omega(\mathbf{k}) + \mathbf{k} \cdot \mathbf{v}(r)$$

where $\mathbf{v}(r)$ is the vortex velocity field. Due to the Doppler shift from the rotating background, certain wave modes become trapped near the vortex core, forming bound state resonances.

The trapped modes exhibit characteristic ringdown (exponential decay) behavior:

$$A(t) = A_0 e^{-t/\tau_\text{damp}} \cos(\omega_\text{ring} t + \phi)$$

By measuring the ringdown frequency $\omega_\text{ring}$ and damping time $\tau_\text{damp}$, the experiment directly probes the effective geometry around the vortex.

### Analogue Hawking Effect and Quasinormal Modes

At a critical radius where the flow velocity equals the sound speed, an acoustic event horizon forms. Modes confined inside this horizon experience mode conversion: outgoing waves from the vortex core cannot escape the rotating region, analogous to gravitational trapping at a Kerr black hole's event horizon.

The quasinormal mode spectrum—the set of frequencies and damping rates at which the vortex can oscillate—is given by solving the wave equation in the effective curved metric background. The measured spectrum encodes information about the underlying rotation rate, size, and shape of the vortex geometry.

## Key Results

1. **Giant vortex stabilization**: A stationary quantum vortex with $\kappa \sim 1,000-10,000$ circulation quanta maintained in superfluid He-4, defying the usual decay into singly quantized vortices. Vortex core radius ~10-50 micrometers.

2. **Record rotational flow strength**: The azimuthal velocity near the vortex core reaches $v_\theta \sim 0.5-2$ cm/s, producing effective Lorentz factors $\gamma_\text{eff} \sim 1.01-1.02$ relative to the sound speed, high enough to create acoustic event horizons.

3. **Bound state resonances**: Detection of surface wave modes trapped near the vortex core, exhibiting characteristic trapped oscillation frequencies 10-100 Hz above the background wave continuum.

4. **Ringdown signatures**: Exponential decay of excited surface wave modes with damping times $\tau \sim 0.1-1$ seconds, matching theoretical predictions for analogue black hole ringdown from quasinormal mode analysis.

5. **Temperature correlations**: The giant vortex remains stable across a broad temperature range (0.5-1.5 K), enabling quantum field theory simulations at finite temperature where semiclassical theory predicts strong radiation effects.

6. **Vortex-induced dragging**: Measurement of the velocity field induced by the vortex extending 100+ core radii, confirming the existence of the effective curved spacetime region.

## Impact and Legacy

The Svančara et al. experiment validates two decades of theoretical work on analogue gravity. It demonstrates that laboratory quantum systems can faithfully reproduce key signatures of rotating black hole spacetimes, at least in the linear acoustic wave regime. This opens multiple research frontiers:

**Quantum field theory in curved spacetime**: Traditional QFT calculations in Kerr geometries are notoriously difficult. The analog system allows experimentalists to observe effects like pair creation, mode mixing, and thermal Hawking radiation signatures in real time.

**Rotating black hole thermodynamics**: By studying how the vortex couples to the thermal state of the superfluid, the experiment tests whether the black hole thermodynamic laws emerge naturally from analog gravity, including Hawking's area law and the Kerr black hole temperature formula.

**Fundamental tests of effective field theory**: The experiment probes whether effective metrics derived from averaging over quantum fluctuations in the fluid are consistent with nonlinear perturbations of the background vortex state.
