## Abstract

Martinetti reviews and extends the contributions of Manuele Filaci to spectral-triple formulations of the Standard Model using twisted noncommutative geometry. The paper investigates minimal twists (automorphisms) of the spectral triple and proves that specific twists induce a **Krein-space structure** on the Hilbert space of fermions. Under this twist, the inner product becomes indefinite, transforming the Hilbert space into a Krein space—a mathematical structure intermediate between Hilbert spaces and pseudo-Riemannian geometry. The unitary symmetry group of the twisted inner product recovers the **twistor symmetry group**, suggesting a deep connection between spectral triples and twistor geometry.

## Historical Context

Connes' spectral triple $(A, H, D)$ represents geometry purely through operator data:
- $A$: algebra of observables (noncommutative geometry)
- $H$: Hilbert space (quantum states)
- $D$: Dirac operator (metric structure)

The standard inner product on $H$ is positive-definite, reflecting Riemannian geometry. However, physics often involves indefinite metrics: spacetime (Minkowski) has signature (-, +, +, +); gauge-theory ghosts have negative norm; quantum gravity may have mixed signatures in intermediate regimes.

A **twist** of the spectral triple is an automorphism u: A → A (an inner automorphism generated by a unitary u ∈ A) that acts on the entire structure:

$$u: (A, H, D) \mapsto (uAu^{\dagger}, H, uDu^{\dagger})$$

Under such a twist, the "natural" inner product on H can shift. If u is not self-adjoint, the induced inner product becomes indefinite.

Martinetti's work (building on Filaci's contributions) systematizes this. The key discovery: **minimal twists of the SM spectral triple induce Krein spaces**, opening a gateway between NCG and indefinite-metric geometry.

For phonon-exflation, this is profound. The framework's SU(3) fiber has a **positive-definite metric** (Riemannian). But during the fold transition to M⁴, the external spacetime has a **pseudo-Riemannian metric** (Lorentzian). Is the twist mechanism the geometric bridge? If so, the fold is a **twist transition**, and Krein-space structure is the intermediate regime.

## Key Arguments and Derivations

### Section 1: Twists of Spectral Triples

A **twist** of a spectral triple is a unitary element $u \in A$ such that:

$$\text{Ad}_u(a) = u a u^{\dagger}, \quad u H = H, \quad u D u^{\dagger} = D' \quad \text{(modified Dirac)}$$

For inner twists, u commutes with the algebra A (at least on a dense subspace), so the twisted triple remains a spectral triple.

For the Standard Model (A = C ⊕ ℍ ⊕ M_3(ℂ) × SU(2)_L × SU(3)_c and H is the fermionic Hilbert space), an example twist is:

$$u = e^{i\theta \sigma} \quad \text{(rotation by angle $\theta$ in some sector)}$$

This generates a one-parameter family of spectral triples, parametrized by θ.

Minimal twists are those with **minimal angle** (smallest deviation from identity) that induce non-trivial indefinite-metric structure.

### Section 2: Krein-Space Induction

A **Krein space** K is a Hilbert space equipped with an indefinite inner product $(·, ·)_K$ satisfying:

1. **Conjugate linearity**: $(u, \lambda v)_K = \bar{\lambda} (u, v)_K$
2. **Conjugate symmetry**: $(u, v)_K = \overline{(v, u)_K}$
3. **Non-degeneracy**: $(u, v)_K = 0$ for all v implies u = 0

The signature is (+, -, ..., -) or other split (p positive, q negative) depending on the application.

**Theorem (Martinetti, following Filaci)**: For the SM spectral triple twisted by a minimal unitary u, the Hilbert space H becomes a Krein space with indefinite inner product:

$$(u\psi_1, u\psi_2)_K = (u\psi_1, \psi_2)_H - \text{(correction terms)}$$

where the correction terms encode the twist's asymmetry.

Proof sketch:
1. The twisted Dirac operator D' = uDu† no longer commutes with u.
2. The commutator [D', u] generates an asymmetry in the spectral structure.
3. This asymmetry is captured by an indefinite metric on H:

$$g(u, v) = \langle u | J | v \rangle$$

where J is an indefinite-metric tensor (pseudo-Hermitian operator) determined by the twist.

4. The Krein structure has signature (N_+ : N_-) determined by the number of positive vs. negative eigenvalues of J.

### Section 3: Twistor Symmetry and Unitary Group

A **twistor** is a 2-component spinor $(Z_{\alpha}, Z'_{\alpha'})$ in the spinor representation of SO(3,1). The twistor group is U(2,2), acting on 4-dimensional complex projective twistor space ℂℙ³.

Penrose's twistor programme relates spacetime (conformal structure) to holomorphic geometry on twistor space.

**Theorem (Martinetti)**: The unitary symmetry group U(H_K) of a Krein space (H_K with indefinite inner product) induced by a minimal SM twist is isomorphic to the **twistor unitary group** U(2,2) (or a subgroup thereof).

Proof: The indefinite metric induced by the twist has signature (8+:8-) (for fermionic degrees of freedom: 8 chirality-left + 8 chirality-right across three families and internal charges). The unitary group preserving this split signature is:

$$U(8,8) \supset U(2,2) \times (\text{internal})$$

The U(2,2) factor acts on the external (spacetime-like) sector, recovering twistor transformations.

### Section 4: Twisted Spectral Action

The spectral action on a Krein-space spectral triple is:

$$S_{\text{twisted}} = \text{Tr}(f(D'/\Lambda))$$

where D' = uDu† is the twisted Dirac operator and the trace is taken in the Krein space (using the indefinite metric).

For the Standard Model with minimal twist, the action splits:

$$S_{\text{twisted}} = S_{\text{even}} + S_{\text{odd}}$$

where:
- $S_{\text{even}}$: contributions from positive-metric modes (Riemannian part, gravitational + Yang-Mills)
- $S_{\text{odd}}$: contributions from negative-metric modes (pseudo-Riemannian part, spacetime curvature + ghost fields)

The ratio $S_{\text{even}} / S_{\text{odd}}$ determines the relative contribution of internal (Riemannian SU(3)) vs. external (pseudo-Riemannian M⁴) geometry.

For the fold mechanism, this ratio would evolve: at τ = 0 (start), $S_{\text{even}} \gg S_{\text{odd}}$ (internal dominates); at late times, $S_{\text{even}} \lesssim S_{\text{odd}}$ (external takes over).

### Section 5: Filaci's Contributions and Extensions

Manuele Filaci (2018-2023) developed the theory of real structures on Krein-space spectral triples, extending Chamseddine-Connes' reality conditions to indefinite metrics.

Key contributions:
1. **Reality conditions in Krein spaces**: A real structure J satisfying $(J\psi)^* = J\psi$ can be generalized to Krein spaces via indefinite conjugation.

2. **CPT symmetry in Krein framework**: CPT is realized as a combination of charge conjugation C, parity P, and time-reversal T, with modified action in Krein spaces.

3. **Spin structure preservation**: The spin bundle on M⁴ ⊗ SU(3) can be twisted to accommodate indefinite metrics without breaking spinor structure.

Martinetti's 2026 paper summarizes these results and extends them to the **crossed product** geometry ($M^4 \times SU(3)$ with twist interaction), showing that the full product space (with twist) remains a spectral triple.

## Key Results

1. **Krein-space emergence**: Minimal twists of the SM spectral triple induce indefinite inner products, converting H to a Krein space.

2. **Signature structure**: Fermionic Hilbert space has signature (8+:8-) under twist, with the 8+ modes corresponding to external gravity and 8- to internal gauge.

3. **Twistor group recovery**: The unitary group of the twisted Krein space contains U(2,2), the conformal group of spacetime.

4. **Twisted spectral action**: Spectral action decomposes into Riemannian (internal) and pseudo-Riemannian (external) contributions, with ratio determined by twist parameter.

5. **CPT in Krein spaces**: CPT symmetry is rigorously formulated on Krein-space spectral triples (Filaci-Martinetti result).

## Impact and Legacy

This work opens a new direction in spectral geometry: **Krein-space spectral triples** as a framework for theories with indefinite metrics. It bridges Connes' NCG program with twistor geometry (Penrose), suggesting that conformal symmetry and complex structure are not independent of spectral geometry but emerge from twists.

For quantum gravity, Krein spaces provide a mathematical framework for handling signature change (Euclidean → Lorentzian transitions), which may occur in cosmological contexts or near singularities.
