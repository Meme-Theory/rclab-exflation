# Early Universe Models from Noncommutative Geometry

**Author(s):** Matilde Marcolli, Elena Pierpaoli
**Year:** 2010
**Source:** arXiv:0908.3683

---

## Abstract

Marcolli and Pierpaoli develop a comprehensive cosmological framework based on noncommutative geometry, incorporating the Standard Model with right-handed neutrinos and Majorana mass terms. Using renormalization group analysis, they compute slow-roll inflationary parameters from the spectral-action Higgs potential, analyze the evolution of the gravitational constant, and derive dark-matter predictions. The work extends spectral-action cosmology by including neutrino physics and demonstrates how NCG predicts a running gravitational coupling, conformal gravity effects at intermediate scales, and sterile neutrino dark matter.

---

## Historical Context

The spectral action principle (Chamseddine-Connes) unifies gravity with the Standard Model. However, the minimal Standard Model has several gaps:

1. **Neutrino masses**: Experimentally observed but absent in the minimal SM. Right-handed (sterile) neutrinos + Majorana coupling explain this.

2. **Dark matter**: Not addressed in the SM. Sterile neutrinos are a candidate.

3. **Conformal invariance**: At very high energy, the SM has an approximate conformal symmetry (if Higgs mass is small). This constrains the RGE flow.

4. **Gravity running**: Unlike gauge couplings, the gravitational "coupling" (Newton's constant G) is suppressed by the Planck scale. However, in a quantum gravity theory, G should run.

Marcolli and Pierpaoli incorporate all these elements into a unified spectral-action cosmology, using extended RGE equations that track the evolution from the unification scale (10^15 GeV) down to the electroweak scale (100 GeV) and beyond.

For phonon-exflation, this is relevant because the framework also involves running: the effective coupling in the D_K operator and the BCS pairing strength both evolve with τ. Understanding how NCG handles running at multiple scales informs the fold dynamics.

## Key Arguments and Derivations

### Section 1: Extended Standard Model in Spectral Action

The Standard Model is extended with **right-handed neutrino singlets**:

$$\psi_L \in (2, 1, 1/3) \quad \text{(left doublet)}$$

$$e_R \in (1, 2, 1) \quad \text{(right singlet)}$$

$$\nu_R \in (1, 1, 0) \quad \text{(sterile, neutral)}$$

The Yukawa Lagrangian includes:

$$\mathcal{L}_Y = h_e \bar{e}_L e_R + h_{\nu} \bar{\nu}_L \nu_R + \frac{1}{2} M_R \bar{\nu}_R^c \nu_R + \text{h.c.}$$

where $M_R$ is the Majorana mass (generated by spectral action's Dirac operator).

In the spectral framework, the Dirac operator D on the finite geometry SU(3) × (internal leptons) has eigenvalues that encode Majorana masses. As the internal geometry "size" (controlled by Higgs vev <H>) changes, so do these masses.

### Section 2: Renormalization Group Equations with Extended Content

The one-loop beta functions are:

**For the Higgs coupling λ**:
$$\beta_{\lambda} = \frac{1}{16\pi^2} \left[ 24\lambda^2 + 4\lambda g_2^2 + \ldots - (\text{Yukawa contributions}) \right]$$

where the Yukawa terms involve $h_e$ (electron) and $h_{\nu}$ (neutrino).

**For the top Yukawa $h_t$**:
$$\beta_{h_t} = \frac{1}{16\pi^2} \left[ \frac{9}{2} h_t^3 - (8g_3^2 + \frac{17}{12}g_1^2 + 3g_2^2) h_t + \ldots \right]$$

**For neutrino Yukawa $h_{\nu}$**:
$$\beta_{h_{\nu}} = \frac{1}{16\pi^2} \left[ h_{\nu}^3 + \frac{1}{2} h_{\nu} h_t^2 - g_2^2 h_{\nu} + \ldots \right]$$

**For Majorana mass $M_R$**:
$$\frac{d \log M_R}{d \log Q} = -2 h_{\nu}^2 / (16\pi^2)$$

(Majorana mass running—unique feature of extended SM.)

**For gravitational coupling G**:
$$\frac{d G^{-1}}{d \log Q} = -\frac{\beta_G}{16\pi^2}$$

where $\beta_G$ includes contributions from all matter loops and is generically small (suppressed by $M_P^2$), but non-zero in a quantum gravity context.

### Section 3: Slow-Roll Inflation from Extended SM

With the neutrino and Majorana terms, the effective Higgs potential becomes:

$$V_{\text{eff}}(\phi) = \frac{\lambda(\phi)}{4} \phi^4 + \Delta V_{\text{Majorana}}(\phi)$$

where $\Delta V_{\text{Majorana}}$ arises from the running of $M_R(\phi)$ and its feedback to the Higgs sector.

The slow-roll parameters are:

$$\epsilon(\phi) = \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{M_P}{V} \frac{dV}{d\phi} \right)^2$$

$$\eta(\phi) = \frac{M_P^2}{V} \frac{d^2 V}{d\phi^2}$$

Marcolli and Pierpaoli compute these using the full RGE-improved potential. Key findings:

1. **Slow-roll window**: For $\phi \in [10^{11}, 10^{16}]$ GeV, both ε and η are << 1, satisfying slow-roll conditions.

2. **Spectral index**:
$$n_s = 1 - 6\epsilon + 2\eta \approx 0.96 \quad \text{(at $k_* = 0.05$ Mpc$^{-1}$)}$$

3. **Tensor-to-scalar ratio**:
$$r = 16\epsilon \approx 0.05-0.08 \quad \text{(over slow-roll window)}$$

4. **Running of spectral index**:
$$\alpha_s = \frac{dn_s}{d\log k} \approx -0.004 \quad \text{(weak running)}$$

All these predictions are consistent with WMAP7 and early Planck data.

### Section 4: Neutrino Masses and the Seesaw Mechanism

The seesaw formula gives effective light-neutrino masses:

$$m_{\nu, \text{light}} = -h_{\nu}^T \frac{1}{M_R} h_{\nu} \langle H \rangle$$

In the spectral framework, $M_R$ is generated by the finite-geometry Dirac operator, and it runs via the RGE. At the unification scale, $M_R \sim 10^{14}$ GeV; at the electroweak scale, it remains large (essentially constant below the seesaw scale).

This gives:

$$m_{\nu}^{\text{eff}} \sim \frac{h_{\nu}^2 \times 174 \text{ GeV}}{10^{14} \text{ GeV}} \sim 10^{-2} \text{ eV}$$

(correctly predicting neutrino masses observed in oscillation experiments).

The Majorana nature (ν = ν^c) is a key feature: it violates lepton number and enables the seesaw mechanism. In the spectral framework, this emerges from the charge-conjugation structure of the finite Dirac operator.

### Section 5: Dark Matter from Sterile Neutrinos

Right-handed neutrinos are singlets under all SM gauge groups—they interact only gravitationally (plus Yukawa to Higgs). This makes them **sterile** and a natural dark-matter candidate.

The sterile-neutrino relic abundance is:

$$\Omega_{\nu_s} h^2 = \frac{m_{\nu_s}}{93.14 \text{ eV}}$$

(if produced non-thermally via oscillation). For $m_{\nu_s} \sim 1-100$ keV:

$$\Omega_{\nu_s} h^2 \sim 0.001 - 0.1$$

(potentially all of observed dark matter).

Alternatively, thermal production via Yukawa interactions yields:

$$\Omega_{\nu_s} h^2 \propto h_{\nu}^2 T_{\text{RH}} / M_R$$

where $T_{\text{RH}}$ is reheating temperature. Marcolli-Pierpaoli compute both scenarios and find consistency with structure-formation constraints.

### Section 6: Conformal and Running Gravity

At very high energies (above GUT scale), the SM coupling constants approach conformal-invariant values:

$$g_1(\text{high } Q) \to g_2(\text{high } Q) \to g_3(\text{high } Q) \to g_{\text{conf}}$$

In this regime, gravity becomes **conformally invariant**:

$$S = \int d^4x \sqrt{g} \left[ -\frac{\lambda_{conf}}{2} R^2 + \text{(matter)} \right]$$

(Weyl conformal gravity instead of Einstein).

As energy scales down, conformal symmetry breaks, and Einstein gravity emerges. The transition occurs around the GUT scale.

The running of Newton's constant is:

$$\frac{d G^{-1}}{d \log Q} = -\frac{N_s}{48\pi} \quad \text{(for $N_s$ scalars)}$$

giving $G^{-1}(Q) = G^{-1}(M_P) + \frac{N_s}{48\pi} \log(Q/M_P)$.

This is tiny (suppressed by $1/M_P$) but potentially detectable via gravitational-wave propagation.

## Key Results

1. **Extended SM with seesaw**: Spectral action naturally incorporates right-handed neutrinos and Majorana masses.

2. **Higgs slow-roll inflation**: RGE-improved potential yields $n_s = 0.96$, $r = 0.05-0.08$, consistent with observations.

3. **Neutrino masses**: Seesaw formula produces $m_{\nu} \sim 0.01$ eV from spectral-action Majorana scale.

4. **Sterile neutrino dark matter**: Right-handed neutrinos are viable dark-matter candidates across a wide mass range.

5. **Conformal gravity at high scales**: UV completion involves conformal gravity; Einstein gravity emerges dynamically.

6. **Running gravitational constant**: G evolves logarithmically with energy scale (tiny but non-zero).

## Impact and Legacy

This work extends spectral-action cosmology to include neutrino physics, unifying particle physics (neutrino masses, dark matter) with cosmology (inflation, running gravity). It demonstrates that the spectral framework makes **specific predictions** testable against observations.

The sterile-neutrino dark-matter connection is particularly interesting: it provides a particle-physics explanation for dark matter without invoking WIMPs or other new physics beyond the Standard Model.
