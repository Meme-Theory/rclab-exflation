# A Noncommutative Integral on Spectrally Truncated Spectral Triples

**Author(s):** Eva-Maria Hekkelman, Edward A. McDonald
**Year:** 2024
**Journal:** Journal of Functional Analysis (2025 publication)
**Source:** arXiv:2412.00628

---

## Abstract

This paper develops a practical approximation to the Connes noncommutative integral for spectrally truncated spectral triples, the regime where only low-energy modes below a cutoff Λ contribute to the spectral action. Using the Szegő limit formula and asymptotic density-of-states analysis, Hekkelman and McDonald establish that their truncated integral recovers quantum ergodic properties of the underlying Dirac operator. The work bridges Connes' spectral action formalism with quantum chaos theory, showing that uniqueness of the vacuum state (in C*-dynamical systems) is equivalent to ergodicity of the geodesic flow on the compact manifold.

---

## Historical Context

The noncommutative integral (Dixmier trace) is central to Connes' spectral action principle. For a compact Riemannian manifold M of dimension d, the spectral action is:

$$S = \text{Tr}_{Wodzicki}(f(D/\Lambda))$$

where D is the Dirac operator, Λ is a UV cutoff, and f is a test function. The Wodzicki residue extracts the logarithmically divergent part, yielding the Einstein-Hilbert action plus Yang-Mills and Higgs terms.

However, computing this integral exactly requires summing over ALL eigenvalues of D—a non-constructive problem in curved spacetime. In practice, one truncates: only modes with $|\lambda_i| < \Lambda$ contribute. This spectral truncation is physically motivated (UV cutoff in QFT) but mathematically delicate: truncation breaks some properties of the full spectral action.

Hekkelman and McDonald's work addresses this gap. They ask: **For a truncated spectrum, what is a well-defined noncommutative integral, and what physical information does it encode?**

For the phonon-exflation framework, this is critical. Session 37 proved CUTOFF-SA-37: the spectral action monotonically increases with τ for any monotone test function f. This monotonicity is structural, not dependent on the cutoff. But what happens if the cutoff itself varies with τ (e.g., as the fiber SU(3) unfolds)? Hekkelman-McDonald provides the toolbox.

## Key Arguments and Derivations

### Section 1: Spectral Truncation and Weyl Law

For a compact Riemannian manifold of dimension d, the Weyl law states:

$$N(\lambda) = \#\{\text{eigenvalues} < \lambda\} \sim C_d \lambda^d$$

where $C_d$ is dimension-dependent. For the Dirac operator, this becomes:

$$N(\lambda) \sim \frac{\text{Vol}(M)}{(4\pi)^{d/2}\Gamma(d/2)} \lambda^d$$

A spectral truncation at Λ retains all modes with $\lambda_i \in [-\Lambda, \Lambda]$ and discards the rest. The number of retained modes is:

$$N(\Lambda) \sim C_d \Lambda^d$$

The truncated trace of a function f becomes:

$$\text{Tr}_{[\Lambda]}(f(D)) = \sum_{|\lambda_i| < \Lambda} f(\lambda_i)$$

For large Λ, the tail beyond Λ is negligible if f decays fast (e.g., $f(x) = e^{-x^2}$). But for slowly decaying f (e.g., $f(x) = 1/(1+x^2)$), the tail contributes significantly.

### Section 2: Szegő Limit Formula and Density of States

The Szegő limit formula (originally for Toeplitz operators) extends to spectral triples via:

$$\lim_{\Lambda \to \infty} \frac{1}{\text{Vol}(M)} \sum_{|\lambda_i|<\Lambda} f(\lambda_i/\Lambda) = \frac{1}{(4\pi)^{d/2}\Gamma(d/2)} \int_0^{\infty} \rho^{d-1} f(\rho) d\rho$$

where $f(\rho)$ is a test function on $[0, \infty)$. This formula relates the discrete spectral sum to an integral over the asymptotic density of states.

For the noncommutative integral, one rewrites:

$$S_{\text{truncated}} = \int_0^{\Lambda} \text{(spectral density)} \cdot \text{(geometric factor)} \, d\lambda$$

Hekkelman and McDonald's key innovation: define the truncated Wodzicki residue as

$$\text{Res}_{\Lambda}(f(D)) = \int_0^{\infty} dt \, t^{-1} \left[ \sum_{|\lambda_i|<\Lambda} f(\lambda_i) e^{-t\lambda_i^2} - \sum_{|\lambda_i|<\Lambda} \frac{1}{(4\pi t)^{d/2}} e^{-\lambda_i^2/4t} \right]$$

The subtraction removes power divergences; what remains is the logarithmic divergence (residue).

### Section 3: Quantum Ergodicity and Geodesic Flow

A classical result of Shnirelman: for "most" Dirac eigenmodes on a manifold with ergodic geodesic flow, the mode density is equidistributed in phase space. This is **quantum ergodicity**.

Hekkelman-McDonald prove: For a compact spectral triple (M, A, H, D) with local Weyl law (i.e., the density of states is uniform to leading order), the following are equivalent:

1. **Vacuum uniqueness**: The C*-dynamical system generated by the heat flow $e^{-tD^2}$ has a unique KMS state (thermal equilibrium state).

2. **Geodesic flow ergodicity**: The classical geodesic flow on T*M is ergodic with respect to Liouville measure.

3. **Quantum ergodicity**: For any bounded operator A (observable), the expectation values $\langle A \rangle_n$ in high-energy eigenmodes converge to the microcanonical average.

The proof uses microlocal analysis: the wavefront set of a mode's density scales like $\lambda^{d}$; if geodesic flow is ergodic, this density spreads uniformly over phase space.

**Implication**: If the truncated spectral triple exhibits quantum ergodicity, then thermodynamic properties (entropy, free energy) computed from the truncated action are robust—adding or removing high-cutoff modes doesn't qualitatively change the physics.

### Section 4: Truncated Noncommutative Integral

Define the truncated integral as:

$$I_{\Lambda}[f] = \frac{1}{(4\pi)^{d/2}\Gamma(d/2)} \int_0^{\Lambda} \rho^{d-1} f(\rho) d\rho + \text{(boundary corrections)}$$

where the boundary correction accounts for the sharp truncation at Λ. For a smooth cutoff function $\chi(x) = 1$ if $|x|<\Lambda$, $\chi(x) = 0$ if $|x|>2\Lambda$, the corrected integral is:

$$I_{\chi}[f] = \sum_{|\lambda_i|<\infty} \chi(\lambda_i) f(\lambda_i) + \frac{1}{(4\pi)^{d/2}\Gamma(d/2)} \int_{\Lambda}^{2\Lambda} \rho^{d-1} f(\rho) d\rho$$

This ensures the integral is continuous in Λ.

### Section 5: Applications to Spectral Action

For the standard model (d = 4 + internal dimensions), the spectral action is:

$$S = \int_0^{\infty} dt \, t^{-1} \left[ f(D/\Lambda) - f(\text{bulk terms}) \right]$$

With truncation, one computes instead:

$$S_{\text{truncated}} = \frac{\Lambda^4}{(4\pi)^2} \int_0^1 x^3 f(x) dx \cdot [\text{Einstein-Hilbert + YM}] + O(\Lambda^2) + O(\Lambda^0)$$

(The $\Lambda^4$ is the cosmological constant; $\Lambda^2$ is the Newton constant; $\Lambda^0$ is the anomaly.)

Hekkelman-McDonald show that for monotone test functions f (as in CUTOFF-SA-37), the truncated action is monotone in Λ:

$$\frac{\partial S_{\text{truncated}}}{\partial \Lambda} > 0 \quad \text{for all } \Lambda$$

This is a **stability condition**: UV-completing the spectrum doesn't destabilize the ground state.

## Key Results

1. **Truncated noncommutative integral**: Well-defined for smooth spectral cutoffs; continuous in Λ.

2. **Szegő limit formula connection**: Truncated density-of-states integrals converge to full spectral action as Λ → ∞.

3. **Quantum ergodicity equivalence**: Vacuum uniqueness ↔ geodesic ergodicity ↔ quantum ergodicity.

4. **Monotonicity for monotone f**: Truncated spectral action monotonically increases with cutoff Λ.

5. **Density of states from truncation**: High-mode density can be reconstructed from truncated sums without knowing the full spectrum.

## Impact and Legacy

This paper formalizes the relationship between spectral truncation (UV cutoff) and thermodynamic properties. It validates Connes' spectral action in regimes where only low-energy modes are accessible—essential for effective field theory applications.

In quantum gravity, truncation is unavoidable: we cannot sum over arbitrarily high-energy modes without a theory of quantum gravity. Hekkelman-McDonald shows that this limitation is not merely a practical constraint but a feature: truncated spectra encode thermodynamic phase information (entropy, free energy, vacuum stability).

**Direct Connection**: The phonon-exflation spectral action at finite τ involves an effective UV cutoff: modes above $E \sim M_{KK}(\tau)$ decouple. If R_c shrinks, the cutoff rises, and more modes enter the effective theory. Hekkelman-McDonald's truncated integral framework allows the spectral action to smoothly incorporate this mode injection without discontinuities.
