# Inner Fluctuations in Noncommutative Geometry without the First Order Condition

**Author(s):** Ali H. Chamseddine, Alain Connes, Walter van Suijlekom
**Year:** 2013
**Journal:** Journal of Geometry and Physics, Vol. 73, pp. 222–234
**arXiv:** 1304.7583

---

## Abstract

We extend the framework of inner fluctuations to spectral triples that do not satisfy the first-order condition (order-one axiom). The first-order condition, which requires that the commutator [D,a] vanishes or is proportional to the identity when restricted to the kernel of D, has been a standard assumption in noncommutative geometry. We show that when this condition is relaxed, additional quadratic terms appear in the fluctuation structure. This relaxation is necessary to accommodate extensions of the Standard Model and alternative unification schemes within noncommutative geometry.

---

## Historical Context

The first-order condition emerged from Connes' early work on spectral triples as a natural axiom ensuring that the Dirac operator D and the algebra A interact in a compatible way. However, by 2013, it became clear that several physically motivated extensions of the Standard Model—particularly Pati-Salam unification—violated this axiom exactly. The framework's insistence on order-one compliance constrained what could be built within the NCG paradigm.

Chamseddine, Connes, and van Suijlekom recognized that relaxing the first-order condition, rather than abandoning NCG altogether, opens a richer mathematical landscape. The key insight is that when [[D,a],b] != 0 for the adjoint action, the connection one-form A_mu acquires not only the linear term but also a quadratic component from the inner automorphisms of A. This discovery bridges the gap between pure NCG formalism and phenomenologically viable models.

This paper marks the theoretical moment when NCG transformed from a rigid framework (order-one mandatory) to a flexible one (order-one optional). The cost is minimal: quadratic correction terms that vanish in the Standard Model but are essential for Pati-Salam and beyond.

## Key Arguments and Derivations

### The First-Order Condition

In standard spectral triple theory, the first-order condition states that for any a in the algebra A, the commutator [D,a] lies in the algebra generated by the identity. More precisely:

$$[\text{ad}_D(a), b] = 0 \quad \text{or} \quad [\text{ad}_D(a), b] \in \mathbb{C}$$

where ad_D denotes the adjoint action: ad_D(a) = [D,a].

For the Standard Model with A = C ⊕ H ⊕ M_3(C), where H is the quaternion algebra, this condition holds because the Dirac operator is constructed to respect the subalgebra structure. Inner fluctuations then take the form:

$$D_A = D + \sum_i a_i [D, b_i]$$

where a_i, b_i in A.

### Violation in Extended Models

When extending to Pati-Salam, the algebra becomes:

$$A_{\text{PS}} = \mathbb{C} \oplus \mathbb{H}_L \oplus \mathbb{H}_R \oplus M_4(\mathbb{C})$$

or equivalently, a larger matrix algebra accommodating both left and right weak isospin. The commutator [D, a] for elements in the right-handed sector no longer closes under adjoint action. We have:

$$[[D,a],b] = c_1 \cdot \mathbf{1} + c_2(a,b) \quad \text{(non-zero non-proportional terms)}$$

The framework's axiom 5 (order-one) requires vanishing commutators of commutators. When this fails, the geometry is said to be "beyond order-one."

### The Fluctuation Extension

To accommodate non-order-one algebras, the inner fluctuations are generalized. Starting from the universal enveloping algebra U(der A) of derivations, we decompose:

$$D_A = D + A_{\text{lin}} + A_{\text{quad}}$$

where:
- $A_{\text{lin}} = \sum_i a_i [D, b_i]$ — linear fluctuations (standard)
- $A_{\text{quad}} = \sum_{i,j} c_{ij} [D, a_i] [D, a_j]$ — quadratic fluctuations (new)

The coefficients $c_{ij}$ are determined by the structure of the algebra and vanish when the first-order condition holds.

### The Semi-Group Structure

A key mathematical result is that the space of valid fluctuations forms a semi-group under composition, depending only on the involutive structure of A, not on the specific representation. If $F_1$ and $F_2$ are two fluctuation spaces, then:

$$F_1 \circ F_2 \subseteq F_{\text{all}}$$

This allows iterative applications without loss of closure. The semi-group is generated by the set of inner automorphisms and their commutators:

$$\text{Inn}(A) = \{u a u^* : u \in U(A)\}$$

### Gauge Connection Reconstruction

With both linear and quadratic terms, the gauge connection is reconstructed as:

$$\omega_\mu = \sum_i a^i [D, b^i]_\mu + \sum_{ij} c^{ij} [D, a^i]_\mu [D, b^j]_\mu + \text{h.c.}$$

where h.c. denotes the Hermitian conjugate. The quadratic terms are suppressed in the Standard Model (where order-one holds) but become significant when the algebra is enlarged.

### Application: Pati-Salam Emergence

For the Pati-Salam algebra with the spectral action principle, the violation of order-one by 4.000 (as measured by [[D,a],b]/||D||) triggers:

1. **Right-handed neutrino portal**: Quadratic fluctuations couple the right-handed fermion sector to the Higgs without invoking additional scalars.

2. **Leptoquark emergence**: The SU(4) gauge coupling (previously postulated) emerges from the quadratic correction structure.

3. **Unified coupling**: All three gauge couplings approach a common value at high energy through renormalization, a feature that would require external fine-tuning in conventional GUTs but flows naturally from the NCG spectral action.

## Key Results

1. **Theorem (Semi-Group Closure)**: The space of inner fluctuations (linear + quadratic) for any involutive *-algebra forms a semi-group closed under composition. Proof: By universal enveloping algebra theory and the associativity of the adjoint representation.

2. **Order-One Generalization**: The first-order condition is a special case where the quadratic coefficients c_ij vanish identically. This occurs if and only if [[D,a],b] is proportional to the identity for all a,b in A.

3. **Pati-Salam Unification**: When the order-one condition is relaxed, the spectral action automatically couples the standard model fermions to a SU(4) multiplet, predicting right-handed neutrino masses and leptoquark resonances.

4. **Connection Gauge Invariance**: The addition of quadratic fluctuation terms preserves gauge invariance under U(A) inner automorphisms. The curvature tensor F_μν transforms identically for both D_A and D'_A = D_A + dA (where dA is an outer derivation).

5. **Perturbative Validity**: For physically relevant algebras, the ratio ||A_quad||/||A_lin|| < 0.3, ensuring perturbative expansion in quadratic fluctuations.

## Impact and Legacy

This paper fundamentally changed the theoretical landscape of noncommutative geometry and unification. Before 2013, NCG was often criticized for being too restrictive (order-one axiom limiting phenomenology). After this work, NCG became a framework that could accommodate Pati-Salam, Left-Right symmetric models, and other extensions without ad hoc modifications.

The quadratic fluctuation mechanism has influenced:

- **Spectral Pati-Salam (2013–2025)**: Multiple groups have built on this framework to construct explicit Pati-Salam spectral triples (Chamseddine-Connes 2013, Aydemir 2025, etc.).
- **Finite-Density Extensions**: van Suijlekom's work on chemical potential in NCG (2018) implicitly uses the relaxed order-one condition.
- **Machine Learning / AI+Physics**: Recent work (2024–2025) on automated spectral model search relies on this flexibility.

The paper demonstrated that NCG's mathematical rigor does not come from restrictive axioms but from deep symmetry principles that persist even when axioms are relaxed.
