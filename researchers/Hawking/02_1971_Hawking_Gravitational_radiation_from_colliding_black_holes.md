# Gravitational Radiation from Colliding Black Holes

**Authors**: Stephen W. Hawking
**Year**: 1971
**Journal**: *Physical Review Letters*, **26**, 1344--1346

---

## Abstract (Analytical Summary)

Hawking proves that the total area of event horizons in a spacetime containing black holes can never decrease, assuming the weak energy condition and cosmic censorship. This *area theorem* places fundamental constraints on black hole interactions: in a merger of two black holes with horizon areas $A_1$ and $A_2$, the final black hole must have area $A_3 \geq A_1 + A_2$. This immediately constrains the maximum energy that can be radiated as gravitational waves in a merger. The theorem is the direct precursor to Bekenstein's identification of black hole area with entropy and to the four laws of black hole mechanics.

---

## Historical Context

### Black Holes in 1971

By 1971, the theoretical understanding of black holes had matured considerably. The Kerr solution (1963) described rotating black holes. The uniqueness theorems of Israel (1967, 1968) and Carter (1971) were establishing that black holes are characterized by only mass, charge, and angular momentum ("no hair"). Penrose (1969) had introduced the process of extracting rotational energy from a Kerr black hole (the Penrose process), and Christodoulou (1970) had identified the *irreducible mass* $M_{\text{irr}}$ of a Kerr black hole:

$$M^2 = \left(M_{\text{irr}} + \frac{Q^2}{4M_{\text{irr}}}\right)^2 + \frac{J^2}{4M_{\text{irr}}^2}$$

Christodoulou showed that while the total mass $M$ can decrease (via the Penrose process), the irreducible mass never decreases. Hawking's contribution was to recognize that this is a *general theorem* about event horizons, not merely a property of the Kerr solution.

### The Gravitational Wave Problem

The practical motivation was gravitational wave astrophysics (decades before LIGO). If two black holes collide, how much energy can be radiated? Without Hawking's area theorem, there was no upper bound other than the total mass-energy. With it, a precise maximum emerges.

## Key Arguments and Derivations

### Event Horizons and Their Generators

The event horizon $\mathcal{H}$ is the boundary of the causal past of future null infinity: $\mathcal{H} = \partial J^-(\mathscr{I}^+)$. It is a null hypersurface generated by null geodesics.

A crucial property: generators of the event horizon can *enter* the horizon (from outside) but can never *leave* it. New generators join the horizon at caustic points or crossover points, but once a generator is on the horizon, it remains on the horizon forever (assuming cosmic censorship -- i.e., the horizon is the boundary of a black hole that persists).

### The Area Theorem

**Theorem** (Hawking, 1971): Let $(M, g_{ab})$ be an asymptotically flat spacetime satisfying:
1. The null energy condition: $R_{ab} k^a k^b \geq 0$ for all null $k^a$ (equivalently, $T_{ab} k^a k^b \geq 0$).
2. Weak cosmic censorship: No naked singularities form (the spacetime is globally hyperbolic to the future of some Cauchy surface).

Then the area of the event horizon on any Cauchy surface $\Sigma_2$ to the future of another Cauchy surface $\Sigma_1$ satisfies:
$$A(\Sigma_2) \geq A(\Sigma_1)$$

### Proof

The proof uses the Raychaudhuri equation for the null generators of the horizon. Let $k^a$ be the tangent to the generators, $\theta$ the expansion scalar, and $\sigma_{ab}$ the shear. On the horizon:

$$\frac{d\theta}{d\lambda} = -\frac{1}{2}\theta^2 - \sigma_{ab}\sigma^{ab} - R_{ab} k^a k^b$$

(The vorticity vanishes because the generators are surface-forming.)

**Claim**: $\theta \geq 0$ everywhere on the future event horizon.

**Proof by contradiction**: Suppose $\theta < 0$ at some point $p$ on the horizon. Then by the Raychaudhuri equation with the NEC ($R_{ab} k^a k^b \geq 0$):

$$\frac{d\theta}{d\lambda} \leq -\frac{1}{2}\theta^2$$

This implies $\theta \to -\infty$ within finite affine parameter $\lambda \leq 2/|\theta(p)|$. But $\theta \to -\infty$ means the generators form a caustic -- they cross. After a caustic, the generators would enter the interior of $J^-(\mathscr{I}^+)$ (i.e., leave the horizon). This contradicts the definition of the event horizon as the *boundary* of $J^-(\mathscr{I}^+)$, provided no singularity intervenes (which is guaranteed by cosmic censorship).

Therefore $\theta \geq 0$ everywhere on the horizon. Since the rate of change of area element $dA$ along generators satisfies $\frac{d}{d\lambda}(dA) = \theta \, dA$, the area element never decreases along any generator. Moreover, new generators can join the horizon (at crossover points or caustics of external null congruences), adding area. No generators leave. Therefore:

$$A(\Sigma_2) \geq A(\Sigma_1) \qquad \square$$

### Application to Black Hole Mergers

Consider two Schwarzschild black holes of masses $M_1$ and $M_2$ that merge to form a single black hole of mass $M_3$, radiating energy $E_{\text{rad}}$ in gravitational waves. For Schwarzschild black holes, the horizon area is:

$$A = 16\pi M^2 \quad (\text{in geometrized units } G = c = 1)$$

The area theorem requires $A_3 \geq A_1 + A_2$:
$$16\pi M_3^2 \geq 16\pi M_1^2 + 16\pi M_2^2$$
$$M_3 \geq \sqrt{M_1^2 + M_2^2}$$

Energy conservation gives $M_3 = M_1 + M_2 - E_{\text{rad}}$, so:
$$M_1 + M_2 - E_{\text{rad}} \geq \sqrt{M_1^2 + M_2^2}$$
$$E_{\text{rad}} \leq M_1 + M_2 - \sqrt{M_1^2 + M_2^2}$$

For equal masses $M_1 = M_2 = M$:
$$E_{\text{rad}} \leq 2M - \sqrt{2}M = (2 - \sqrt{2})M \approx 0.293 \, (2M)$$

So at most about 29% of the total mass can be radiated. This is an absolute upper bound; the actual efficiency for non-spinning equal-mass mergers is about 5% (as confirmed by numerical relativity simulations in the 2000s and by LIGO observations starting in 2015).

For Kerr black holes, the area is:
$$A = 8\pi M \left( M + \sqrt{M^2 - a^2} \right) = 8\pi \left( 2M \, M_{\text{irr}} \right) = 16\pi M_{\text{irr}}^2$$

where $a = J/M$ is the spin parameter. The maximum energy extraction occurs when both initial black holes are maximally spinning ($a = M$) and the final black hole is Schwarzschild ($a_3 = 0$). The area of a maximal Kerr hole is $A = 8\pi M^2$ (half the Schwarzschild value). Then:

$$16\pi M_3^2 \geq 8\pi M_1^2 + 8\pi M_2^2$$
$$M_3 \geq \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\sqrt{M_1^2 + M_2^2}$$

For equal masses:
$$E_{\text{rad}} \leq 2M - M = M = \frac{1}{2}(2M)$$

Up to 50% of the total mass can be radiated if the initial black holes are maximally spinning and the spin angular momentum is fully converted. The actual GW150914 event radiated about 4.6% of the total mass.

### Irreducible Mass Connection

Christodoulou's irreducible mass is directly related to the area: $A = 16\pi M_{\text{irr}}^2$. Hawking's area theorem therefore implies:

$$\delta M_{\text{irr}} \geq 0$$

for any process. This was already known for the Penrose process by Christodoulou's calculation, but Hawking's theorem is completely general and applies to arbitrary (not necessarily axisymmetric or stationary) processes.

## Physical Interpretation

### The Thermodynamic Analogy

The area theorem states $\delta A \geq 0$. This is structurally identical to the second law of thermodynamics, $\delta S \geq 0$. Hawking himself noted this analogy but initially dismissed it as merely formal, arguing that a black hole has zero temperature (classically, nothing can escape) and therefore cannot be a true thermodynamic system. Bekenstein would soon challenge this view.

### One-Way Membrane

The event horizon acts as a one-way membrane: matter and radiation can fall in, increasing the area, but nothing classical can escape, so the area cannot decrease. This is directly analogous to the irreversibility of thermodynamic processes in an isolated system.

### Gravitational Waves Carry "Entropy"

The area theorem constrains mergers because gravitational waves carry energy to infinity. Every joule radiated away must be "paid for" by the area budget. The theorem thus provides a profound connection between the geometry of horizons and the energetics of gravitational wave emission.

## Impact and Legacy

### Immediate Consequences

1. **Bekenstein entropy (1972--1973)**: Inspired by the area theorem, Bekenstein proposed $S_{\text{BH}} = \eta \, k_B A / \ell_P^2$ where $\eta$ is an undetermined constant of order unity. Hawking initially opposed this identification, but his own discovery of Hawking radiation (1974) confirmed it with $\eta = 1/4$.

2. **Four laws of black hole mechanics (1973)**: The area theorem became the second law in the Bardeen--Carter--Hawking framework.

3. **Penrose inequality**: A conjectured strengthening: the area of the outermost trapped surface bounds the total mass, $M \geq \sqrt{A/16\pi}$. Proved by Huisken and Ilmanen (2001) and Bray (2001) for the Riemannian case.

### LIGO Verification

In 2021, Isi et al. performed a direct test of the area theorem using LIGO data from GW150914. By analyzing the ringdown, they found the final area exceeded the sum of initial areas at $>97\%$ confidence -- the first observational verification of a theorem from the classical theory of black holes.

### Quantum Violations

Hawking radiation violates the area theorem by introducing negative energy flux across the horizon (quantum stress-energy violates the NEC). The classical area theorem is replaced by the *generalized second law* (GSL): the sum of black hole entropy $A/4\ell_P^2$ and exterior entropy $S_{\text{out}}$ never decreases. The GSL has been proven under various assumptions by Wall (2012) and others.

## Connections to Modern Physics

1. **Holographic principle**: The area theorem, combined with Bekenstein's entropy bound, leads to 't Hooft and Susskind's holographic principle: the maximum entropy in a region is bounded by the area of its boundary in Planck units. This is a cornerstone of AdS/CFT and quantum gravity.

2. **Numerical relativity**: The area theorem provides a consistency check for numerical simulations of binary black hole mergers. Any code that violates $\delta A \geq 0$ has a bug.

3. **Quantum extremal surfaces**: In modern quantum gravity, the classical area theorem is generalized to the *quantum focusing conjecture* (Bousso et al., 2016), which governs quantum extremal surfaces and is central to the resolution of the information paradox via the island formula.

4. **Gravitational wave astronomy**: The area bound $E_{\text{rad}} \leq M_1 + M_2 - \sqrt{M_1^2 + M_2^2}$ (for non-spinning holes) provides the theoretical framework for interpreting LIGO/Virgo/KAGRA observations. Every confirmed detection is consistent with the theorem.

5. **For emergence frameworks**: In any model where horizons are effective (e.g., analogue gravity, phonon horizons in BEC), the area theorem has an analogue involving the entropy of the effective horizon. The connection between geometric area and information content is one of the strongest hints that spacetime may have a microscopic (possibly phononic) structure.
