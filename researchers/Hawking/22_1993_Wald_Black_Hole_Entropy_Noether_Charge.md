# Black Hole Entropy is Noether Charge

**Author(s):** Robert M. Wald

**Year:** 1993

**Journal:** Physical Review D, Vol. 48, pp. 3427–3431

**arXiv:** gr-qc/9307038

---

## Abstract

In any diffeomorphism-invariant gravitational theory, black hole entropy can be expressed as a geometric quantity derived from Noether charges associated with the horizon's Killing field. Wald proves that the first law of black hole mechanics holds for perturbations of stationary spacetimes with bifurcate Killing horizons, and derives a universal formula for entropy that applies across all such theories: $S_{BH} = 2\pi \int_{\Sigma} Q[n_a] \, d(n-2)$, where $Q$ is the Noether charge form and $n_a$ is the Killing field normal to the horizon. This resolves a longstanding question about the generalization of black hole thermodynamics beyond Einstein gravity.

---

## Historical Context

Black hole thermodynamics, initiated by Bekenstein (1973) and Hawking (1974), established that horizons carry an entropy $S_{BH} = A/(4\ell_P^2)$ and a temperature $T_H = \hbar \kappa/(2\pi c)$. However, this was derived specifically for Einstein gravity. As higher-derivative theories (Gauss-Bonnet, Lovelock, etc.) gained interest, a critical question emerged: **Does the entropy formula generalize, or is it Einstein-specific?**

Wald's breakthrough demonstrates that entropy is not an accident of Einstein gravity but rather a **topological property of any diffeomorphism-invariant theory**. The entropy emerges from the Noether charge associated with the symmetry that generates the horizon (its Killing field). This connects thermodynamics to the deeper symmetry structure of gravitational theories.

For the phonon-exflation framework, this is immediately relevant: the spectral action $S_0 + S_spec$ is a diffeomorphism-invariant theory (over M^4 × SU(3)). Any horizon-like structures in the internal geometry or effective lower-dimensional sectors inherit a thermodynamic interpretation via Wald's formula.

## Key Arguments and Derivations

### The Noether Charge Form

For any Lagrangian density $\mathcal{L}$ that depends on the metric and its derivatives, Noether's theorem associates a current $J^a$ to each infinitesimal symmetry. For diffeomorphisms generated by a vector field $\xi^a$, the Noether charge is a (n-2)-form $Q[\xi]$ on (n-1)-dimensional spatial slices:

$$Q[\xi] = \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial R^{ab}_{cd}} \epsilon_{ab}^{cd} \nabla_e \xi^e \, d\text{vol}$$

where $R^{ab}_{cd}$ is the Riemann tensor and $\epsilon$ is the volume form. For Einstein gravity, this simplifies to familiar expressions.

### The First Law

Wald proves that for a stationary black hole with horizon $\mathcal{H}$ and bifurcate Killing field $k^a$ (so $k^a$ vanishes at the bifurcation surface $\Sigma$):

$$\delta M = \frac{\kappa}{8\pi} \delta S_{BH} + \Phi_i \delta Q_i$$

where $\kappa$ is the surface gravity and $\Phi_i$ are potentials conjugate to conserved charges $Q_i$ (electric charge, angular momentum, etc.). The entropy is:

$$S_{BH} = 2\pi \int_{\Sigma} Q[k^a] \, d\Sigma$$

with the integral over the bifurcation surface. This is a purely geometric quantity, independent of the dynamics outside the horizon.

### Generality and Uniqueness

The proof relies only on:
1. Diffeomorphism invariance (so Noether's theorem applies)
2. Existence of a bifurcate Killing horizon (so the Killing field vanishes at $\Sigma$)
3. Stationarity of the background spacetime

No assumption about the form of the Lagrangian is needed. Theories with higher curvature corrections, scalar fields, torsion, or extra dimensions all fall under this framework, as long as they are diffeomorphism-invariant.

For a theory with Lagrangian density that includes terms like:
$$\mathcal{L} = R + \alpha R^2 + \beta R_{ab} R^{ab} + \gamma R_{abcd} R^{abcd} + \ldots$$

the Noether charge is modified accordingly, but the first law structure persists.

### Connection to Bekenstein's Area Formula

In Einstein gravity with no other fields, Wald's formula reduces to:

$$S_{BH} = \int_{\mathcal{H}} \frac{1}{4} \, d\Sigma = \frac{A}{4}$$

(in Planck units). The entropy density per unit area is constant = 1/4, recovering the Bekenstein entropy. This is a consistency check showing that Einstein gravity is a special case, not a fundamental derivation of entropy.

### Thermodynamic Stability

From the first law, Wald derives that the entropy $S_{BH}$ has the correct thermodynamic properties: it is extremized (not necessarily maximized) for fixed energy and conserved charges. For perturbations preserving stationarity, $\delta^2 S_{BH}$ encodes stability conditions.

## Key Results

1. **Universal Entropy Formula**: $S = 2\pi \int_{\Sigma} Q[k^a]$ applies to any diffeomorphism-invariant gravitational theory, generalizing Bekenstein's area law.

2. **First Law Derivation**: The first law $\delta M = (\kappa/8\pi) \delta S + \Phi_i \delta Q_i$ follows directly from the Noether charge structure, not from special properties of Einstein gravity.

3. **Bifurcate Killing Horizons are Essential**: The derivation requires that the Killing field $k^a$ governing the symmetry (e.g., time translation) vanishes at the bifurcation surface $\Sigma$. This ensures that the Noether current integrates to a finite, well-defined entropy.

4. **Entropy is Local and Geometric**: Unlike thermodynamic entropies in ordinary systems, black hole entropy emerges from a local geometric integral on the horizon surface itself, with no explicit reference to the bulk spacetime.

5. **Extension to Non-Einstein Theories**: The formula applies without modification to Gauss-Bonnet gravity, Lovelock gravity, theories with scalar-tensor mixing, and any other diffeomorphism-invariant extension of general relativity.

## Impact and Legacy

Wald's paper became foundational for black hole thermodynamics in modified gravity theories. Key developments:

- **Higher-Derivative Gravity**: Entropy in Gauss-Bonnet and other theories is immediately calculable via Wald's formula, enabling tests of modified gravity via black hole thermodynamics.

- **Gauge/Gravity Duality**: In AdS/CFT, Wald entropy is the gravitational dual of entanglement entropy in the boundary CFT. This connection underlies the gauge/gravity conjecture.

- **Entanglement Islands**: Modern work (Penington 2020, Hartman et al. 2020) applies Wald's formula to quantum extremal surfaces, extending the formula to entanglement entropy in black hole spacetimes.

- **Cosmological Horizons**: The formula extends to de Sitter horizons and other cosmological spacetimes with boundaries, as long as they possess Killing symmetries.

- **Quantum Gravity Phenomenology**: Any effective quantum gravity theory (loop quantum gravity, causal sets, asymptotic safety) can be tested by computing the entropy via Wald's formula and comparing to observations or semiclassical limits.
