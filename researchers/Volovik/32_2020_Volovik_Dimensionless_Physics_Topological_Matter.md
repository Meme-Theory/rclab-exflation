# Dimensionless Physics

**Author(s):** Grigory E. Volovik

**Year:** 2020

**Journal/Source:** arXiv:2006.16821 (submitted June 30, 2020; final revision July 27, 2020)

---

## Abstract

This paper examines two emergent gravity models where metric dimensions shift to inverse square length, making physically observable quantities dimensionless—including Newton's constant, scalar curvature, cosmological constant, and particle masses. In the first model, the quantum vacuum is treated as a superplastic crystal with gravity describing elastic deformations. In the second, gravitational tetrads arise from fermionic field bilinear forms. Both frameworks reveal a deep connection between dimensionless physics and topological properties of the vacuum.

---

## Historical Context

The dimensionless formulation emerges naturally from diffeomorphism invariance in quantum vacuum contexts. Traditional approaches to quantum gravity struggle with the fundamental nature of the metric: is it a dynamical field or an emergent property of a deeper structure? Volovik's approach suggests that when gravity emerges from quantum condensed matter, the dimensional structure itself is emergent, not fundamental. This has profound implications for understanding the cosmological constant problem, particle masses, and the role of topology in physics. The paper bridges condensed matter analogies with fundamental physics by showing that topological properties can determine physical dimensions.

## Key Arguments and Derivations

### The Superplastic Crystal Model

In Volovik's first approach, the quantum vacuum is modeled as a superplastic crystal. A crystal naturally has an elastic strain tensor $u_{ij}$ describing small deformations. In such a medium, gravity emerges as the elasticity theory of this substrate:

$$g_{ij} \propto \delta_{ij} + u_{ij}$$

where $g_{ij}$ is the metric tensor and $u_{ij}$ represents elastic strain. The key insight is that in elastic theory, the strain is **dimensionless** (it is the ratio of deformation to lattice spacing). Therefore, the metric becomes dimensionless, implying that Newton's constant $G$ has inverse area dimensions, not the traditional length-squared dimensions.

The scalar curvature $R$ and cosmological constant $\Lambda$ similarly become dimensionless quantities. The gravitational action takes the form:

$$S_g = \int d^4x \sqrt{g} \left[ \frac{R - 2\Lambda}{16\pi} + \text{matter terms} \right]$$

where both $R$ and $\Lambda$ are dimensionless topological invariants.

### Fermionic Bilinear Tensors

In the second approach, the gravitational tetrad $e_a^\mu$ emerges from bilinear forms of fermion fields:

$$e_a^\mu = \bar{\psi} \gamma^a \partial^\mu \psi$$

The fermionic fields $\psi$ carry canonical mass dimension 3/2 in 4D. Their bilinear products have dimension 3, and spatial derivatives have dimension 1. This naturally yields $e_a^\mu$ with dimension 2 (length squared), making the metric $g^{\mu\nu} = \eta^{ab} e_a^\mu e_b^\nu$ emerge with the correct dimensions for curvature and physics. However, when viewed through the emergent lens, the metric arises from a dimensionless coupling of fermions to the substrate geometry.

### Dimensional Shift and Topological Operators

When Newton's constant becomes dimensionless (formally $G \to \infty$ in units where $\hbar = c = 1$), certain gravitational operators become **topological**, meaning they contribute integer or fractional prefactors independent of scale:

$$\text{Topological operator} = \int d^4x \sqrt{g} \left( R + \frac{1}{6} \tilde{R} \right)$$

where $\tilde{R}$ represents torsion-related curvatures. These become Chern-Simons-like invariants that count winding numbers or monopole charges in spacetime.

### Intrinsic Quantum Hall Effect

In 4D spacetime, the dimensional shift enables a 3+1 dimensional intrinsic quantum Hall effect:

$$\sigma_H = e^2 / h \cdot \nu$$

where $\nu$ is a dimensionless topological integer (Chern number). This demonstrates that electromagnetic and gravitational responses are fundamentally unified through topological invariants in the dimensionless framework.

### Nieh-Yan Anomaly with Torsion

The Nieh-Yan topological density involves torsion $T^a$:

$$\text{Nieh-Yan} = T^a \wedge T_a$$

In the dimensionless formulation, this quantity contributes directly to gravitational anomalies and chiral fermion production, without needing dimensional factors to cancel divergences.

## Key Results

1. **Dimensionless Metric Emerges from Elasticity**: When gravity is treated as elasticity of a quantum vacuum crystal, the metric becomes dimensionless by construction. Newton's constant and the cosmological constant become topological invariants.

2. **Fermionic Emergence of Geometry**: Gravitational tetrads naturally arise from fermion bilinear forms, providing a UV completion that avoids traditional infinities by making dimensional factors irrelevant.

3. **Topological Quantization of Physical Constants**: Fundamental constants (gravitational coupling, cosmological constant, particle masses) are quantized by topology, not by ad-hoc symmetries or fine-tuning.

4. **Unified Quantum Hall Effects**: The 3+1 dimensional quantum Hall effect becomes the natural expression of electromagnetic-gravitational coupling in a topologically ordered vacuum.

5. **Anomaly Transparency**: Chiral and gravitational anomalies become transparent (i.e., they appear naturally without divergence cancellations) in the dimensionless framework.

## Impact and Legacy

This paper reframes the dimensional structure of physics as an emergent property rather than fundamental. It influenced later work on:

- **Emergent spacetime models**: The idea that gravity and dimensions emerge from a dimensionless substrate has resonances in holographic models, matrix theories, and tensor network approaches.
- **Topological field theory applications**: The paper strengthens the view that topological invariants, not dimensional analysis, determine the structure of physical laws.
- **Cosmological constant problem**: By treating $\Lambda$ as a topological integer, the paper suggests the cosmological constant is fundamentally discrete, not a continuous free parameter.
