## Topic Map

### A. Schwarzschild Exact Solutions & Maximal Extension
Papers: 01, 02, 07
Schwarzschild exterior vacuum metric (01), interior uniform-density fluid with Buchdahl 8/9 bound (02), Kruskal-Szekeres maximal extension revealing four regions, Einstein-Rosen bridge, and the coordinate vs curvature singularity distinction (07). Foundation for all subsequent causal analysis.

### B. Penrose Conformal Methods & Cosmic Censorship
Papers: 03, 04, 05
Conformal compactification bringing infinity to finite boundary (03). The 1965 singularity theorem: trapped surface + NEC + non-compact Cauchy surface implies geodesic incompleteness (04). Cosmic censorship conjecture (weak and strong), Penrose process for Kerr energy extraction, irreducible mass, Penrose inequality (05).

### C. Singularity Theorem Reviews & Generalizations
Papers: 11, 16, 17
Hawking-Penrose 1970 unified theorem requiring SEC + generic condition, applicable to collapse and cosmology without global hyperbolicity (11). Senovilla-Garfinkle pedagogical exposition of 1965 theorem with emphasis on non-compact Cauchy surface requirement (17). Senovilla critical appraisal: energy condition fragility under quantum effects, robustness of mathematical structure (16).

### D. Spinor & Twistor Formalism
Papers: 06, 08, 09, 27, 28
Newman-Penrose null tetrad formalism: 12 spin coefficients, 5 Weyl scalars, 18 NP field equations, Petrov classification, Goldberg-Sachs theorem, peeling theorem (08). Penrose-Rindler treatise: 2-spinor calculus, curvature decomposition Psi_ABCD + Phi_ABA'B' + Lambda, massless field equations, twistor equation, quasi-local mass (09). Twistor algebra: incidence relation omega^A = ix^{AA'}pi_{A'}, Robinson congruence, SU(2,2) conformal symmetry, Penrose transform H^1(PT,O(n)) (06). Modern twistor lectures (27) and AdS5 extension (28).

### E. Higher-Dimensional Exact Solutions & Black Holes
Papers: 12, 18, 19, 22, 25, 26
Emparan-Reall review: Myers-Perry rotating black holes, black rings (D>=5 only), uniqueness failure in higher D, entropy scaling S ~ M^{(D-2)/D} (12). Trapped surfaces in higher D: topology and formation complexity (18). Gregory-Laflamme instability of black strings, critical wavelength lambda_GL ~ r_h (19). WCC violations in D>=6 black hole collisions (22). String theory resolution of GL singularity via D-brane condensation (25). Rasheed general KK black holes with two U(1) charges (26).

### F. Compactification Constraints & No-Go Theorems
Papers: 13, 14, 15, 20
Witten bubble of nothing: KK vacuum M^4 x S^1 unstable to gravitational instanton, stabilized by fermions/SUSY (14). GMN no-go from Raychaudhuri: static compact internal + dS external forces SEC violation (13). Time-dependent compactification no-go: smooth evolution to dS requires NEC/DEC violation or singularity (20). Brown-Dahlen exact dynamical compactification from higher-D dS (15).

### G. Conformal Cyclic Cosmology
Papers: 10, 21
CCC: infinite aeons connected by conformal identification of future null infinity with next Big Bang. Weyl curvature hypothesis (C=0 at Big Bang). Hawking point signatures in CMB (10). 2025 update: gravitational wave epoch evidence, NP spinor formalism across aeon boundaries (21).

### H. Higher-Dimensional Formalism & KK Reduction
Papers: 23, 24, 29
Higher-D Newman-Penrose: spinors via Clifford algebras, Cartan-Siegel-Petrov classification replacing Petrov types (23). Higher-D GHP formalism: multispinors, edth operators, computational efficiency for KK metrics (24). Gauss-Codazzi-Ricci decomposition for KK dimensional reduction: curvature splitting under foliation (29).

## Paper Entries

### Paper 01: Schwarzschild Exterior Vacuum Solution (1916)
- **Citation**: K. Schwarzschild, Sitzungsber. Preuss. Akad. Wiss. Berlin (Math. Phys.), 189-196 (1916)
- **Content**: First exact solution to Einstein's vacuum field equations. Spherically symmetric, static ansatz with unimodular coordinates. Derives ds^2 = -(1-2M/r)dt^2 + (1-2M/r)^{-1}dr^2 + r^2 dOmega^2. Three classical tests: perihelion precession (42.98"/century), light deflection (1.75"), gravitational redshift. Birkhoff theorem (Jebsen 1921): uniqueness of spherically symmetric vacuum.
- **Key equations**: Metric (eq 12), Kretschmann K=48M^2/r^6 (eq 21), perihelion Delta_phi=6piM/p (eq 35), ISCO r=6M, photon orbit r=3M.
- **Framework relevance**: Exact solution methodology applied to SU(3) modulus space. Birkhoff rigidity analog: block-diagonality theorem for D_K.

### Paper 02: Schwarzschild Interior Fluid Solution (1916)
- **Citation**: K. Schwarzschild, Sitzungsber. Preuss. Akad. Wiss. Berlin (Math. Phys.), 424-434 (1916)
- **Content**: Interior metric for uniform-density perfect fluid sphere. Spatial sections are portion of S^3. Pressure profile p(r) = rho c^2 (cos eta - cos eta_g)/(3cos eta_g - cos eta). Central pressure diverges at 2GM/Rc^2 = 8/9 (Buchdahl limit). Interior is conformally flat: C_{abcd}=0. Darmois-Israel junction conditions at surface.
- **Key equations**: Interior metric, TOV equation, Buchdahl bound r_s/R < 8/9, surface redshift z < 2.
- **Framework relevance**: Conformal flatness of interior parallels WCH at tau=0 (|C|^2=5/14 minimum).

### Paper 03: Penrose Conformal Compactification (1963)
- **Citation**: R. Penrose, Phys. Rev. Lett. 10, 66-68 (1963)
- **Content**: Conformal rescaling g~=Omega^2 g brings infinity to finite boundary. Five components of conformal infinity: I+, I-, i^0, i+, i-. Asymptotically flat/simple spacetimes. Peeling theorem: Psi_n = O(r^{-(5-n)}). Bondi news N_{ab}, mass-loss dM_B/du <= 0. BMS group = SL(2,C) x supertranslations. Weyl tensor conformally invariant: C~=C.
- **Key equations**: g~=Omega^2 g, Omega=2cos(p)cos(q), K=Omega^{-1}C (rescaled Weyl), Bondi mass-loss formula.
- **Framework relevance**: Conformal structure at I+ defines black hole (B = M\J^-(I+)). Penrose diagram methodology applied to modulus space transit.

### Paper 04: Penrose Singularity Theorem (1965)
- **Citation**: R. Penrose, Phys. Rev. Lett. 14, 57-59 (1965). Nobel Prize 2020.
- **Content**: If (1) R_{uv}k^u k^v >= 0 (NEC), (2) non-compact Cauchy surface exists, (3) closed trapped surface exists, then spacetime is null geodesically incomplete. Proof by contradiction: focusing theorem forces compact boundary of future, which cannot embed in non-compact Cauchy surface. Does NOT specify singularity type, curvature divergence, or whether singularity is censored.
- **Key equations**: Raychaudhuri d theta/d lambda = -theta^2/2 - sigma^2 - R_{uv}k^u k^v, focusing bound theta -> -inf within lambda <= 2/|theta_0|.
- **Framework relevance**: L-3 PET isomorphic to this theorem. NEC audit at each tau value. Trapped surface formation in internal SU(3) during compactification.

### Paper 05: Cosmic Censorship & Penrose Process (1969)
- **Citation**: R. Penrose, Riv. Nuovo Cim. 1, 252-276 (1969)
- **Content**: WCC: generic collapse produces singularities hidden behind horizons. SCC: maximal Cauchy development is inextendible. Penrose process: particle splitting in Kerr ergosphere extracts up to 29.3% of mass-energy. Irreducible mass M_irr = sqrt(A/16pi). Blue-shift instability at Cauchy horizons. Penrose inequality M_ADM >= sqrt(A/16pi). Event horizon H+ = boundary(J^-(I+)).
- **Key equations**: Christodoulou M^2 = M_irr^2 + J^2/(4M_irr^2), Kerr Delta=r^2-2Mr+a^2, Omega_H=a/(r_+^2+a^2).
- **Framework relevance**: Clock constraint = cosmic censorship analog. Dump point = extremal horizon (kappa=0). SCC and Cauchy horizon instability parallel van Hove blueshift at spectral walls.

### Paper 06: Twistor Algebra (1967)
- **Citation**: R. Penrose, J. Math. Phys. 8, 345-366 (1967)
- **Content**: Twistor space T=C^4, Z^alpha=(omega^A, pi_{A'}). Incidence relation omega^A=ix^{AA'}pi_{A'}. Projective twistor space PT=CP^3. Klein correspondence: points in spacetime = lines in PT. Hermitian form Sigma=Z^alpha Z-bar_alpha of signature (++--) partitions PT into PT+/PN/PT-. Robinson congruence = Hopf fibration. SU(2,2) conformal group acts linearly. Infinity twistor I_{alpha beta}. Penrose transform: H^1(PT,O(n)) <-> massless fields of helicity h=-(n+2)/2.
- **Key equations**: Incidence relation, twistor norm = helicity s=(1/2)Z^alpha Z-bar_alpha, Penrose contour integral.
- **Framework relevance**: Potential twistor description of U(1)_7 gauge field emerging from KK. Linear conformal action simplifies higher-D analysis.

### Paper 07: Kruskal Maximal Extension (1960)
- **Citation**: M.D. Kruskal, Phys. Rev. 119, 1743-1745 (1960)
- **Content**: Kruskal-Szekeres coordinates (T,X) with ds^2 = (32M^3/r)e^{-r/2M}(-dT^2+dX^2) + r^2 dOmega^2. Four regions: exterior I (our universe), black hole II (future singularity), parallel universe III, white hole IV (past singularity). Horizon at T=+/-X (null surfaces). Singularity at T^2-X^2=1 (spacelike hyperbola). Einstein-Rosen bridge non-traversable. Maximal: unique simply-connected analytic vacuum extension.
- **Key equations**: X^2-T^2=(r/2M-1)e^{r/2M}, T/X=tanh(t/4M), r=0 singularity is spacelike, Delta tau_max=piM.
- **Framework relevance**: Wall circumvention = Kruskal extension (restricted patch -> full manifold). Maximal extension paradigm applied to modulus space.

### Paper 08: Newman-Penrose Spin Coefficient Formalism (1962)
- **Citation**: E.T. Newman & R. Penrose, J. Math. Phys. 3, 566-578 (1962)
- **Content**: Null tetrad {l,n,m,m-bar} with l.n=-1, m.m-bar=1. Four directional derivatives D,Delta,delta,delta-bar. 12 complex spin coefficients (kappa,sigma,rho,tau,...). 5 Weyl scalars Psi_0..Psi_4. 18 NP field equations (Ricci identities). 8 Bianchi-NP equations. Petrov classification I,II,D,III,N,O. Goldberg-Sachs: vacuum algebraically special iff shear-free geodesic congruence (kappa=sigma=0). Peeling: Psi_k=O(r^{-(5-k)}). Bondi mass-loss via Psi_4.
- **Key equations**: Raychaudhuri in NP: D rho = rho^2+sigma sigma-bar (geodesic+affine), D sigma = 2rho sigma + Psi_0. Petrov discriminant D=I^3-27J^2. Psi_4=-h-ddot_+ + i h-ddot_x.
- **Framework relevance**: Petrov classification at dump point (D->II). Weyl scalar decomposition for modulus space curvature.

### Paper 09: Penrose-Rindler Spinors and Spacetime (1984/1986)
- **Citation**: R. Penrose & W. Rindler, Cambridge University Press (1984, 1986), 2 volumes
- **Content**: Vol 1: SL(2,C) double cover, abstract index notation, spinor metric epsilon_AB, Infeld-van der Waerden symbols, curvature decomposition R=Psi+Phi+Lambda (20 components: 10+9+1), ZRM equations nabla^{AA'}phi_{AB...L}=0, Maxwell spinor phi_AB. Vol 2: Twistor equation nabla_{A'(A}omega_{B)}=0, incidence relation, PT=CP^3 with signature (++--), Penrose transform, GHP formalism, quasi-local mass via kinematic twistor A_{alpha beta}, peeling.
- **Key equations**: g_{ab}=epsilon_{AB}epsilon_{A'B'}, R=Psi_{ABCD}+Phi_{ABA'B'}+Lambda, Psi_2=-M/r^3 (Schwarzschild), Psi_2=-M/(r-ia cos theta)^3 (Kerr).
- **Framework relevance**: Curvature decomposition applied to SU(3) deformation. Weyl spinor factorization = principal null directions = Petrov type.

### Paper 10: Conformal Cyclic Cosmology (2010)
- **Citation**: R. Penrose, Cycles of Time (The Bodley Head, 2010)
- **Content**: CCC: universe as sequence of aeons, each Big Bang to infinite future. Conformal identification of I+ (aeon n) with Big Bang (aeon n+1). Weyl curvature hypothesis: C=0 at Big Bang, grows via gravitational clumping. Requires all massive particles to eventually lose rest mass. Observational prediction: concentric temperature anomalies in CMB from prior-aeon supermassive BH evaporation.
- **Framework relevance**: WCH constrains tau=0 state: is it conformally flat? |C|^2(0)=5/14 is minimum but non-zero. CCC aeon boundary parallels BCS transition boundary.

### Paper 11: Hawking-Penrose Unified Singularity Theorem (1970)
- **Citation**: S.W. Hawking & R. Penrose, Proc. R. Soc. Lond. A 314, 529-548 (1970)
- **Content**: Unified theorem: SEC (R_{uv}u^u u^v >= 0) + generic condition + no CTCs + (trapped surface OR compact achronal set OR reconverging light cone) => timelike or null geodesic incompleteness. Does not require global hyperbolicity. Generic condition: curvature not specially aligned with every geodesic.
- **Key equations**: Raychaudhuri (timelike): d theta/d tau = -theta^2/3 - sigma^2 + omega^2 - R_{uv}u^u u^v. Blowup at tau=3/|theta_0|.
- **Framework relevance**: SEC check during tau evolution. Generic condition: does SU(3) left-invariance violate it? If so, singularities may be avoided by symmetry.

### Paper 12: Emparan-Reall Higher-D Black Holes Review (2008)

Uniqueness failure in higher D. Entropy S ~ M^{(D-2)/D}. BPS solutions in supergravity. First law dM=T_H dS + Omega_i dJ_i + Phi dQ.
- **Framework relevance**: D=10 entropy scaling S~M^{4/5}. KK black hole stability on M4xSU(3). GL instability constrains internal radius vs black hole size.
