# The Physics of Conformal Cyclic Cosmology

**Author(s):** Meissner, Penrose
**Year:** 2025
**Journal:** arXiv:2503.24263

---

## Abstract

Conformal Cyclic Cosmology (CCC) proposes that the universe undergoes infinite cycles (aeons), each beginning with a big bang and ending in an infinite future that serves as the big bang for the next aeon. Recent developments extend CCC to include gravitational wave phenomenology and Hawking point signatures. This paper synthesizes the geometric framework of CCC with observational predictions from gravitational wave epoch evidence and temperature correlations in the CMB. The conformally invariant structure preserves the spinor geometry across aeon boundaries through identification of the infinite future of one aeon with the singular past of the next, enabled by vanishing of Weyl curvature at null infinity.

---

## Historical Context

Penrose's Conformal Cyclic Cosmology emerged as a radical alternative to eternal inflation and the multiverse paradigm. Rather than infinite space spawning infinite universes, CCC proposes infinite time: a single, eternal cosmos composed of infinite successive aeons. Each aeon is itself a full universe (with its own Big Bang and expansion history), yet causally connected to the previous aeon through the conformally invariant limit at null infinity.

The key insight is that the infinite future of one aeon (where all particles have been infinitely redshifted to zero energy) is mathematically indistinguishable from the Big Bang of the next aeon. Conformal geometry—which preserves angles and the causal structure but not distances—is precisely the tool that allows this identification. Under a conformal rescaling $g_{\mu\nu} \to \Omega^2 g_{\mu\nu}$, the metric becomes singular at the boundary, but all null geodesics and conformal structure remain well-defined.

Early CCC papers (Penrose 2010, 2012) focused on the scalar geometry and cosmological predictions. The 2025 update introduces three major extensions:

1. **Gravitational wave epoch**: Direct GW signatures from the final moments of the previous aeon
2. **Hawking spots**: Temperature anomalies in the CMB from Hawking evaporation of supermassive black holes in the prior aeon
3. **Detailed spinor formalism**: Rigorous Newman-Penrose treatment of null geodesics across aeon boundaries

## Key Arguments and Derivations

### Conformal Structure and the Infinite Future

In CCC, the metric is rescaled:
$$\tilde{g}_{\mu\nu} = \Omega^2 g_{\mu\nu}$$

where $\Omega \to 0$ as one approaches null infinity $\mathcal{I}^+$. The conformal factor $\Omega$ is chosen such that:

- **Null geodesics remain null**: A photon's worldline is unchanged (it remains a null curve)
- **Causal structure preserved**: Light cones don't change (angles at points remain invariant)
- **Weyl curvature vanishes**: $C_{\alpha\beta\gamma\delta} = 0$ in the limit, leaving only Ricci curvature

The Weyl tensor, which encodes tidal forces and gravitational radiation, is conformally invariant:
$$\tilde{C}_{\alpha\beta\gamma\delta} = C_{\alpha\beta\gamma\delta}$$

At null infinity, where the conformal metric becomes singular ($\Omega \to 0$), the Weyl tensor vanishes. This is the foundation of the **Weyl Curvature Hypothesis**: the Big Bang is a state of vanishing Weyl curvature (smooth, isotropic), while the infinite future approaches a state of increasingly turbulent Weyl structure (as black holes merge, gravitational waves accumulate, etc.).

### Hawking Point Signatures

In the previous aeon, supermassive black holes evaporate via Hawking radiation. Their evaporation produces a characteristic temperature spectrum:
$$T_{\text{Hawking}} = \frac{\hbar c^3}{8\pi k_B G M}$$

For a $10^9 M_\odot$ black hole at redshift $z \sim 20$, the evaporation temperature in the prior aeon is extremely low. However, under conformal rescaling, these ancient low-temperature events map into the CMB temperature window of the current aeon.

Specifically, Penrose proposes that Hawking evaporation produces point-like temperature anomalies in our CMB—**Hawking points**—with angular size $\theta \sim 1°$ and temperature deviation $\Delta T / T \sim 10^{-4}$ to $10^{-5}$. The statistical signature is:

- Power law clustering in angular distance
- Alignment with supermassive black hole positions inferred from their entropy
- No correlation with known instrumental artifacts

The conformal identification maps the black hole's final entropy $S_{\text{BH}} = k_B A / (4\ell_p^2)$ into a dimensionless conformal invariant that predicts the CMB temperature fluctuation magnitude.

### Gravitational Wave Epoch

As the previous aeon nears infinity, gravitational waves accumulate from:
1. Black hole mergers and ringdown
2. Neutron star collisions
3. Early-universe phase transitions

These waves encode information about the previous aeon's history. Under conformal identification, they map into the initial gravitational wave background present at the Big Bang of the current aeon.

The energy density in primordial gravitational waves is predicted by CCC to be:
$$\Omega_{GW,0} h^2 \sim 10^{-14} \text{ to } 10^{-16}$$

This is extraordinarily small but potentially detectable by next-generation interferometers (Einstein Telescope, Cosmic Explorer) or through stochastic searches at pulsar timing arrays.

The Newman-Penrose formalism applied to the conformal metric gives the two-component shear tensor $\sigma_{\mu\nu}$ along null geodesics:
$$\nabla_V \sigma_{\mu\nu} = \rho \sigma_{\mu\nu} + \Psi_2$$

where $V$ is the tangent to the null geodesic, $\rho$ is the expansion coefficient, and $\Psi_2$ is a component of the Weyl tensor encoding gravitational wave amplitude.

## Key Results

1. **Conformal boundary conditions**: The infinite future of aeon $n$ is identified with the Big Bang of aeon $n+1$ through a smooth conformal rescaling that preserves all null geodesics and preserves causal structure.

2. **Hawking point predictions**: Temperature anomalies of order $\Delta T/T \sim 10^{-4}$ to $10^{-5}$ at angular scales $\sim 1°$, clustering in power-law distribution with $n(< \theta) \propto \theta^2$ to $\theta^{2.5}$.

3. **Primordial gravitational wave background**: CCC predicts $\Omega_{GW,0} h^2 \sim 10^{-14}$ to $10^{-16}$, below current constraints but within reach of future detector networks.

4. **Weyl curvature hypothesis**: The Big Bang has minimum Weyl curvature (entropy production maximum at future infinity), providing a low-entropy initial condition without appeal to fine-tuning.

5. **No singularities**: Conformal geometry removes metric singularities; all physical invariants remain finite through the aeon boundary.

6. **Inflation not required**: CCC explains the smoothness and flatness of the universe without invoking a separate inflationary epoch, instead attributing these properties to the infinite past of prior aeons.

## Impact and Legacy

CCC has become a major alternative framework in quantum cosmology, attracting both vigorous support and substantial criticism. Key impacts:

- **CMB anomaly reanalysis**: Multiple studies have re-examined the Planck data for Hawking point signatures, with contested results (some groups find $> 3\sigma$ correlations; others attribute them to noise)
- **Gravitational wave astrophysics**: CCC makes definite predictions about the stochastic GW background that will be testable by LISA and ground-based detectors
- **Twistor-conformal methods**: CCC has revived interest in conformal geometry, twistor theory, and spinor methods in cosmology
- **Philosophical impact**: Challenges the multiverse paradigm and proposes a deterministic, causally connected universe with infinite temporal structure

The 2025 update consolidates CCC predictions into a quantitative framework testable by next-generation astronomy.

**Framework-relevant**: CCC offers an alternative boundary condition for the universe's initial state via conformally invariant geometry. The phonon-exflation framework uses **internal compactification** (M4 x SU(3)) to generate particles and drive expansion, whereas CCC uses **temporal topology** (infinite aeons) to avoid singularities.

However, both frameworks share the insight that **conformal invariance** constrains the low-energy dynamics. In phonon-exflation, the spectral action of the Dirac operator on the SU(3) fiber is conformally weighted. CCC's conformal rescaling of the metric could be reinterpreted as a scale-dependent flow of the SU(3) fiber structure itself—suggesting a unified picture where **conformal transitions in spacetime mirror phase transitions in the internal geometry**.

Additionally, CCC's Hawking points provide a novel observational test: if CMB anomalies are confirmed, they would support the existence of primordial gravitational waves and ancient black hole physics, both consistent with the instanton-driven pair creation mechanism of phonon-exflation's particle genesis epoch.

**Closest connection**: Newman-Penrose spinor formalism and conformal geometry are essential tools for analyzing the Dirac spectrum on curved SU(3) in phonon-exflation; CCC demonstrates the power of these methods in cosmological applications.
