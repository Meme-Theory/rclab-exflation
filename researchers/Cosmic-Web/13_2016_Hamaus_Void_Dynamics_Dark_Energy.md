# Constraints on Cosmology and Gravity from the Dynamics of Voids

**Author(s):** Nico Hamaus, Alice Pisani, Paul M. Sutter, Guilhem Lavaux, Stéphanie Escoffier, Benjamin D. Wandelt, Jochen Weller

**Year:** 2016

**Journal:** Physical Review Letters, Volume 117, Article 091302

---

## Abstract

This landmark paper demonstrates that cosmic void dynamics directly constrain the growth rate of structure, dark energy, and modified gravity. By measuring the expansion rate of voids as a function of redshift using SDSS galaxy data, the authors find that the observed void dynamics are consistent with ΛCDM and General Relativity, ruling out certain modified gravity theories. The method is theoretically clean: voids evolve in the linear regime where perturbation theory is accurate, making void dynamics a precision probe of fundamental physics.

---

## Historical Context

While Sutter's VIDE work (2014-2015) established methodology, Hamaus et al. (2016) provided the first dynamical analysis with real data and strong cosmological conclusions. Their measurement showed that voids could be used to test modified gravity at a level competitive with cluster and weak-lensing methods, but with the advantage of simpler theoretical modeling.

## Key Arguments and Derivations

### Void Dynamics in Linear Theory

For a spherical void in an expanding universe, the dynamics are governed by the equation of motion:

$$\ddot{R}_v + 2 H \dot{R}_v = - \frac{G M_{\text{shell}}}{R_v^2}$$

where $M_{\text{shell}}$ is the mass in the shell at the void boundary. This can be rewritten as:

$$\frac{\ddot{R}_v}{R_v} = - \frac{4\pi G}{3} \bar{\rho} (1 + 3 w_\Lambda) = - \frac{4\pi G}{3} \bar{\rho} (1 + 3 w(z))$$

for ΛCDM where $w_\Lambda = -1$ is the dark energy equation of state.

In the **linear regime** (early times, small density perturbations), the void growth is described by:

$$\delta_v(a) = - D(a) / D(a_0)$$

where $D(a)$ is the linear growth factor. Observationally, the growth rate can be measured from redshift-space distortions:

$$\beta = f / b_v = \frac{d \ln D}{d \ln a} / b_v$$

where $b_v$ is the bias of voids.

### Measuring Void Expansion from Surveys

From a galaxy survey, one measures the **void size** at different redshifts. For a void identified at redshift $z$, its comoving radius evolves as:

$$R_v(z) = R_v(z_0) \frac{D(z)}{D(z_0)}$$

By measuring voids at multiple redshifts and fitting to the growth factor, one infers the expansion history.

Additionally, the **redshift-space distortions** in void clustering encode the growth rate. The void-void correlation function in redshift space shows characteristic anisotropy:

$$\xi_{\text{void}}(r_\perp, r_\parallel) \propto [1 + \beta \mu^2]^2 \xi_{\text{matter}}(r)$$

where $\mu = r_\parallel / r$ and $\beta = f / b_v$. Fitting this anisotropy measures $f$ directly.

### Modified Gravity Predictions

Different theories predict different growth rates. Examples:

- **ΛCDM+GR**: $f(\Omega_m) = \Omega_m^{0.55}$ (approximate)
- **f(R) gravity**: $f \approx \Omega_m^{0.68}$ (typically steeper)
- **DGP model**: $f$ varies strongly with redshift

By measuring $f(z)$ using void dynamics, one can distinguish between theories.

### Results from SDSS Data

Hamaus et al. measured voids in SDSS and found:

- **Growth rate $f$**: Consistent with ΛCDM predictions at multiple redshifts
- **Modified gravity constraints**: f(R) models ruled out at ~2-3σ level
- **Dark energy constraints**: Results consistent with $w = -1$ (cosmological constant)

## Key Results

1. **Voids probe linear growth regime**: Voids evolve in the linear regime where theory is clean and predictive.

2. **Precise growth rate measurement**: Void dynamics measure $f$ to ~10% precision, competitive with cluster methods.

3. **Modified gravity tests**: Certain modified gravity theories (e.g., f(R) with large coupling) are ruled out.

4. **Consistency with ΛCDM+GR**: No significant deviations from General Relativity and Λ CDM found.

5. **Complementary to other probes**: Void measurements provide independent constraints on dark energy, improving when combined with other methods.
