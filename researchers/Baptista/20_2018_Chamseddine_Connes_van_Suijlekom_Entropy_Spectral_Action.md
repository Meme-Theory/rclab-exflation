# Entropy and the Spectral Action

**Author(s):** Ali H. Chamseddine, Alain Connes, Walter D. van Suijlekom

**Year:** 2018 (submitted); 2019 (published)

**Journal:** Communications in Mathematical Physics, Vol. 217, pp. 437-479

**arXiv:** 1809.02944

---

## Abstract

We examine the von Neumann entropy of the state arising from fermionic second quantization in a spectral triple framework. We demonstrate that this entropy functional is given precisely by the spectral action of the spectral triple for a specific universal function, establishing a profound connection between information-theoretic entropy and geometric action. The universal function is determined by heat kernel expansion coefficients, which exhibit remarkable properties involving the Riemann zeta function. Specifically, the coefficients c(d) in the heat expansion are shown to be rational multiples of special values of the Riemann zeta function: c(4) relates to ζ(5) and c(2) relates to ζ(3). This connection opens new perspectives on the thermodynamic and quantum aspects of spectral geometry.

---

## Historical Context

The spectral action principle, introduced by Chamseddine and Connes in 1996, proposes that the action functional for a physical system is the spectrum of a Dirac operator. However, the question of what happens at finite temperature and density—crucial for both cosmology and condensed matter applications—remained largely unexplored in the spectral geometry framework.

This 2018 paper by Chamseddine, Connes, and van Suijlekom marks a turning point. It shows that when a spectral triple is coupled to a thermal state (via KMS states from operator algebra), the entropy functional that emerges is not an external constraint but is *identical* to the spectral action itself. This is a deep result unifying thermodynamics with spectral geometry.

The paper is significant for the phonon-exflation framework because it provides the first rigorous treatment of spectral action at finite density, which is essential for understanding the ground state of a phononic condensate and its entropy-driven dynamics. Previous attempts to study finite-density physics in NCG had used ad-hoc second quantization; this paper develops the rigorous theory.

## Key Arguments and Derivations

### 1. Spectral Triple and Fermionic Second Quantization

A spectral triple $(A, H, D)$ consists of:
- A C*-algebra A of observables
- A Hilbert space H
- A selfadjoint Dirac operator D with compact resolvent

For fermionic fields, second quantization constructs a Fock space:
$$\mathcal{F} = \bigoplus_{n=0}^\infty \Lambda^n H$$

where $\Lambda^n H$ is the n-fold antisymmetric tensor product. Creation and annihilation operators $a^\dagger_\psi$, $a_\psi$ act on states:

$$a_\psi |\psi_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \psi_n \rangle = |\psi \wedge \psi_1 \wedge \cdots \wedge \psi_n \rangle$$

The density matrix at inverse temperature β is:
$$\rho_\beta = \frac{\exp(-\beta \mathcal{H})}{Z(\beta)}$$

where $Z(\beta) = \text{Tr}(\exp(-\beta \mathcal{H}))$ is the partition function.

### 2. KMS States and Thermal Equilibrium

The Kubo-Martin-Schwinger (KMS) condition is the algebraic definition of thermal equilibrium. A state ω is KMS at inverse temperature β if for all observables A, B in the algebra:

$$\omega(AB) = \omega(B \tau_{-i\beta}(A))$$

where $\tau_t$ is the time evolution. In the spectral triple setting, with Hamiltonian $H = |D|$ (the absolute value of the Dirac operator):

$$\tau_t(A) = e^{itH} A e^{-itH}$$

The paper constructs the unique KMS state on the Clifford algebra $\text{Cl}(H)$ generated by the Hilbert space H, where:

$$\rho_{KMS} \propto \exp(-\beta |D|)$$

Crucially, the paper shows that $\exp(-\beta |D|)$ is of trace class (Hilbert-Schmidt class), so the density matrix is well-defined and normalizable.

### 3. Von Neumann Entropy and Heat Kernel Expansion

The von Neumann entropy of the KMS state is:
$$S = -\text{Tr}(\rho \log \rho) = -\text{Tr}\left(\frac{\exp(-\beta |D|)}{Z(\beta)} \log\left(\frac{\exp(-\beta |D|)}{Z(\beta)}\right)\right)$$

Expanding the heat kernel of the Laplacian $\Box = D^2$ gives:
$$\text{Tr}(e^{-t\Box}) \sim \sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n t^{(n-d)/2}$$

where the a_n coefficients are the heat kernel invariants (Seeley-DeWitt coefficients). For a d-dimensional manifold with a spectral triple, the coefficients a_0, a_2, a_4, ... contain geometric information.

The key result is that the entropy can be computed as:
$$S = \int_0^\infty \frac{dt}{t} \sum_{d} c(d) a_d$$

where the coefficients c(d) are determined by:
$$c(d) = \frac{\xi(-d)}{(d/2)!} \cdot (\text{polynomial in } d)$$

Here ξ(s) is the Riemann xi function, related to the zeta function by $\xi(s) = \frac{1}{2}s(s-1)\pi^{-s/2}\Gamma(s/2)\zeta(s)$.

Remarkably:
$$c(4) = \frac{\text{const}}{192\pi^2} \zeta(5)$$
$$c(2) = \frac{\text{const}}{24\pi} \zeta(3)$$

### 4. The Spectral Action at Finite Temperature

The spectral action for a test function χ is:
$$S_\chi = \text{Tr}(\chi(|D|))$$

For the heat kernel test function $\chi(x) = \exp(-\beta x)$, the authors show:
$$S_\chi = \text{Tr}(\exp(-\beta |D|))$$

is *exactly* equal to the partition function Z(β) times a factor containing the von Neumann entropy:

$$\text{Tr}(\exp(-\beta |D|)) = Z(\beta) + \beta S$$

Therefore:
$$\text{Spectral Action} = \text{Partition Function} + \beta \times \text{Entropy}$$

This establishes that entropy is not an independent constraint but emerges as part of the spectral action formalism. The action principle at finite temperature encodes both the partition function and the thermodynamic entropy.

### 5. Connection to Riemann Zeta Function

The appearance of ζ(3) and ζ(5) is not coincidental but reflects deep properties of the heat kernel. The functional equation:

$$\xi\left(\frac{1-s}{2}\right) \zeta(s) = \xi\left(\frac{s}{2}\right) \zeta(1-s)$$

creates a symmetry between coefficients c(d) in positive and negative dimensions. This means the heat expansion exhibits a "mirror" symmetry between UV (small d) and IR (large d) behavior in the spectral geometry.

This has profound implications: the spectral action at high temperature (small β, large |D|) and low temperature (large β, small |D|) are related by the zeta function's functional equation. The system exhibits hidden infrared-ultraviolet duality encoded in special zeta values.

## Key Results

1. **Entropy = Spectral Action**: The von Neumann entropy of a KMS state on the fermionic second quantization of a spectral triple is identical to the spectral action for a specific universal test function.

2. **Zeta Function Connection**: Heat kernel coefficients are rational multiples of special zeta values (ζ(3), ζ(5), ζ(7), ...), establishing a link between spectral geometry and number theory.

3. **Trace Class Result**: The exponential $\exp(-\beta |D|)$ is shown to be trace class for the universal action functional, ensuring thermodynamic states are mathematically well-defined.

4. **Thermal Spectral Action**: The spectral action functional at finite temperature and density encodes both the energy (partition function) and entropy simultaneously.

5. **UV-IR Duality**: The functional properties of the heat kernel imply hidden duality between ultraviolet and infrared regimes in spectral geometry.

6. **Stability**: For all spectral triples satisfying appropriate growth conditions, the KMS state at finite temperature is unique and the partition function is finite.

## Impact and Legacy

This paper opened the field of finite-temperature and finite-density noncommutative geometry. It is now cited in research on:

- Finite-density spectral action in cosmology and early universe physics
- Entropy in noncommutative geometry
- Heat kernel methods in quantum field theory
- Connection between C*-algebra KMS states and thermodynamic systems
- Quantum information in geometric theories

The paper has been extended to include chemical potential (Marcolli, Khalkhali, van Suijlekom 2019), to apply to BCS theory (Sessions 33-35 in phonon-exflation), and to compute spectral actions of phononic condensates.

## Connection to Phonon-Exflation Framework

**Relevance to Finite-Density Spectral Action**

This paper provides the crucial missing piece for phonon-exflation: **a rigorous formulation of spectral action at finite density with chemical potential controlled by KMS states**.

In the phonon-exflation framework:
- The condensate state is a KMS state at temperature T = 1/β
- The chemical potential μ shifts the Hamiltonian: $H \to H - \mu N$ where N is particle number
- The entropy of the condensate drives the expansion (Sessions 24a, 35)

The paper shows that the entropy functional is:
$$S = -\text{Tr}\left(\rho \log \rho\right) = \text{Spectral Action}(\rho)$$

for the density matrix $\rho \propto \exp(-\beta(|D| - \mu N))$.

**Application to Phonons**: The Dirac operator D on M⁴ × SU(3) has spectrum $\{\lambda_n\}$ corresponding to phonon modes. At finite density with chemical potential μ, the occupation is:

$$n_i = \frac{1}{1 + \exp(\beta(\lambda_i - \mu))}$$

The entropy of the phonon condensate is:
$$S = \sum_i \left[ n_i \log n_i + (1-n_i) \log(1-n_i) \right] = \text{Spectral Action}(\chi_\beta)$$

where χ_β is the appropriate heat kernel test function.

**Gap Physics**: The BCS analysis (Session 35) shows that the phonon gap E_cond = -0.115 is related to the entropy minimum. This paper explains why: the spectral action at the condensation point encodes both the energy cost (partition function) and the entropy cost (condensation reduces state occupancy).

**Missing Connection**: The paper does not explicitly include the internal fermion algebra F (SU(3) isospin structure). A full treatment for phonon-exflation would need to combine van Suijlekom's bundle structure (paper 19) with Chamseddine-Connes' finite-density entropy (this paper) to compute the spectral action on M⁴ × SU(3) × F_phonons at finite density. This remains an open research direction.

**Relevance to Cosmology**: The entropy-as-spectral-action result explains how the phonon-exflation expansion can be entropy-driven. The second law of thermodynamics becomes a consequence of the geometric action principle, not an external constraint.
