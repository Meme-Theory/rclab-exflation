## Abstract

The lack of clear new-physics signals at the Large Hadron Collider motivates models that can guide current and future collider searches. The spectral action principle within the noncommutative geometry (NCG) framework provides such models with distinctive phenomenology. Certain versions of Pati-Salam (PS) models with gauge coupling unification and limited scalar content can be derived from an appropriately chosen noncommutative algebra. In this paper, I review gauge-coupling-unified Pati-Salam models and discuss phenomenological aspects, including the scalar leptoquark S_1. These NCG-derived PS models exhibit automatic gauge coupling unification at naturally lower scales with constrained scalar sectors and geometric Yukawa couplings.

---

## Key Results

The paper demonstrates that:

1. **NCG-Derived Unification**: Pati-Salam models can be derived from noncommutative geometric principles with gauge group SU(4)_C × SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R emerging naturally.

2. **Automatic Coupling Unification**: Gauge couplings unify geometrically at M_GUT ~ 10^14 GeV without traditional GUT mechanisms.

3. **Constrained Scalar Sector**: Only minimal leptoquarks and Higgs fields appear, determined by representation theory.

4. **Geometric Yukawa Couplings**: Fermion masses and mixings arise from spectral geometry, making predictions highly constrained.

5. **Leptoquark Phenomenology**: The universal S_1 scalar leptoquark provides distinctive collider signatures.
