## Abstract

Noncommutative geometry extends beyond SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1) toward exceptional gauge groups (G₂, E₆, E₈) and nonassociative algebras. We review constructions of gauge covariant Dirac operators on spectral triples with exceptional symmetries, particularly G₂ × G₂ and E₈. The key innovation is developing gauge transformations on nonassociative (octonion) algebras via bimodule structures. Octonion algebra structure naturally restricts the scalar sector to minimal charges and mixing angles. G₂ × G₂ internal geometry yields 28 gauge bosons, coupling unification at 10^15 GeV (lower than SU(5)), and colored Higgs with no free parameters. E₈ provides even more stringent constraints.

## Key Arguments

### 1. Exceptional Groups in Unification

**G₂** (14-dim): Smallest exceptional group
**E₆** (78-dim): Contains SU(3)³
**E₈** (248-dim): Largest exceptional, appears in heterotic strings

### 2. Nonassociative Gauge Transformations

For nonassociative algebras (octonions), standard associative gauge transformation fails. Solution: **bimodule structures** over nonassociative algebras:

$$L_a : E \to E, \quad R_a : E \to E$$

satisfying left-right commutativity but not associativity.

Gauge transformation:
$$\Gamma_a^L \to U \Gamma_a^L U^{-1} + (\partial_a U) U^{-1}$$
$$\Gamma_a^R \to (\Gamma_a^R) U + U^{-1} (\partial_a U)$$

### 3. G₂ × G₂ Geometry and Scalar Sector

The adjoint of G₂ (14-plet) decomposes under SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1) as:
$$\mathbf{14} = (\mathbf{8}, \mathbf{1}) + (\mathbf{3}, \mathbf{2}) + (\overline{\mathbf{3}}, \mathbf{2}) + (\mathbf{1}, \mathbf{3})$$

The (3,2) and conjugate are **colored Higgs** (leptoquarks), masses fixed by G₂ breaking scale—no free parameters!

### 4. Coupling Unification

**SU(5)**: M_GUT ~ 2×10^16 GeV
**G₂ × G₂**: M_GUT ~ 10^15 GeV (lower, avoids some proton decay constraints)
**E₈**: Even lower, but possibly over-constrained

## Key Results

1. **Bimodule Framework**: Gauge covariance extends to nonassociative algebras.

2. **G₂ × G₂ Predictions**: 28 gauge bosons, unification at 10^15 GeV, colored Higgs with geometric masses.

3. **Exceptional Scalar Constraint**: Exceptional symmetries restrict scalar sector more strictly than SU(5).

4. **Heat Kernel Scaling**: a₀(G₂) much smaller than a₀(SU(3)), altering gravitational and scalar physics.
