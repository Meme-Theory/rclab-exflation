# Gauge Threshold Corrections in Warped Geometry

**Author(s):** Kiwoon Choi, Ian-Woo Kim, Chang Sub Shin

**Year:** 2010

**Journal:** New Journal of Physics, Vol. 12, Article 075014

**arXiv:** 1001.1473

---

## Abstract

In theories with warped extra dimensions, Kaluza-Klein threshold corrections to low-energy gauge couplings can be substantial, particularly when the warping is large. We derive explicit one-loop threshold expressions for generic five-dimensional gauge theories on a slice of anti-de Sitter space (AdS₅) with symmetry breaking via orbifold boundary conditions and/or bulk Higgs vacuum values. The key innovation is accounting for mass mixing between bulk fields with different orbifold parities. We show that in strongly warped geometries, threshold corrections can enhance or suppress gauge coupling unification depending on the choice of bulk matter representation.

## Key Arguments

### 1. Warped Metric and KK Spectrum

The Randall-Sundrum metric with warp factor $e^{-2kz}$ produces exponentially suppressed KK masses:
$$m_{phys} = e^{-k\pi R} \cdot m_{Planck}$$

### 2. One-Loop Threshold Formula

The KK contribution to running couplings is:
$$\Delta_i^{KK} = \frac{1}{4\pi} \sum_n \left[ C_i(n) \log\left(\frac{M_n^2}{M_Z^2}\right) - 2 C_i(n) \right]$$

The warp factor gives exponential suppression:
$$C_i(n) \propto e^{-2k\pi R n}$$

making the sum convergent and dominated by low modes.

### 3. Mass Mixing Effects

Bulk mass mixing between orbifold-even and orbifold-odd fields modifies the physical KK spectrum, affecting unification predictions by 5-10%.

---

## Key Results

1. **Warp-Factor Suppression**: KK thresholds are exponentially suppressed, dominated by low modes.

2. **General Formula**: Explicit computable expressions for any 5D gauge theory on warped slices.

3. **Unification Modification**: Warped geometry reduces predicted unification scale from 10^16 to ~10^15 GeV.

4. **Coupling Ratio Stability**: Ratios of couplings are stable despite absolute shifts.
