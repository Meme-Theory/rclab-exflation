## Abstract

Gauge coupling extraction from KK dimensional reduction involves normalization ambiguities that have caused 40 years of confusion. We distinguish three conventions: (1) GUT/Dynkin (standard particle physics), (2) Killing Form (KK reduction from continuous manifolds), (3) Democratic (NCG spectral action). These differ by O(1) factors like √(3/5) and √(2/3) from generator trace norms and Dynkin indices. We provide explicit transformation formulas between conventions and clarify the phonon-exflation coupling ratio g₁/g₂ = e^{-2τ} depends critically on which convention is used and whether running is included.

## Key Arguments

### 1. Three Conventions Explicitly Defined

**GUT Convention** (Dimopoulos-Georgi 1981):
$$\frac{1}{g_1^2} = \sqrt{\frac{3}{5}} \frac{1}{g_5^2}$$
(from SU(5) embedding with canonical normalization)

**Killing Form** (KK from M⁴ × SU(3)):
$$\frac{1}{g_s^2} = \frac{\text{vol}(SU(3))}{\lambda_s \cdot \kappa^2}$$
where $\lambda_s = 4$ for SU(3)

**Democratic** (NCG spectral action):
$$\frac{1}{g_s^2} \propto C_s(F) = \text{Casimir of representation F}$$

### 2. SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1) Couplings

**Killing Form**:
$$\frac{g_s^2}{g_w^2} = \frac{1.5 \times 5.3}{4 \times 117.4} \approx 0.017 \implies \alpha_s / \alpha_w \approx 0.5$$

**Democratic**:
$$\frac{g_s^2}{g_w^2} = \frac{C_w}{C_s} = \frac{3/4}{4/3} \approx 0.56 \implies \alpha_s / \alpha_w \approx 1$$

Different by factor ~10! Convention choice matters enormously.

### 3. Transformation Formulas

$$g_i^2|_{dem} = g_i^2|_{Killing} \times \sqrt{\frac{C_i^{dem}}{C_i^{Killing}}}$$

All normalizations give physically identical results when applied consistently to the same problem.

## Key Results

1. **Three Distinct Conventions**: GUT, Killing Form, and Democratic give couplings differing by O(1) factors.

2. **No Physics Difference**: Factors arise purely from representation-theoretic conventions.

3. **Transformation Rules**: Explicit formulas to convert between conventions.

4. **Phonon-Exflation Implication**: The g₁/g₂ = e^{-2τ} result requires careful specification of which convention is used and whether RG running is included.
