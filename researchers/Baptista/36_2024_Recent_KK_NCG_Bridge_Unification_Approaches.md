## Abstract

Recent work (2010-2025) has substantially resolved the tension between Kaluza-Klein (KK) dimensional reduction and Connes' noncommutative geometry (NCG) spectral action. Both frameworks derive gauge fields from internal manifold geometry but through different mathematics (KK uses Riemannian metrics; NCG uses Dirac spectral data). The heat kernel expansion (Seeley-DeWitt coefficients) serves as the universal bridge. We synthesize five major recent advances: (1) van Suijlekom's unbounded Kasparov product formalism (gauge theory from Hilbert bundles), (2) finite-density spectral action (Chamseddine-Connes-van Suijlekom 2018-2020), (3) spectral action on product spaces M⁴ × K × F, (4) KK threshold corrections in warped geometries (exponential suppression), (5) unified gauge coupling extraction (normalization ambiguities resolved). We identify open problems and propose directions for phonon-exflation completion.
