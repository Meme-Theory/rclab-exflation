## Topic Map

### A. Classical KK Unification (1914-1938)
Papers: 01, 02, 03, 04
Nordstrom's 5D scalar precursor, Kaluza's 5D metric ansatz g_MN -> (g_uv, A_u, phi),
Klein's S1 compactification and charge quantization, Einstein-Bergmann's three axioms
(Riemannian 5D, periodicity, variational principle) and the dilaton problem.

### B. Non-Abelian Generalization and Fiber Bundles (1964-1981)
Papers: 05, 06, 09, 23
DeWitt's fiber bundle perspective and non-abelian KK idea, Kerner's explicit derivation
of Yang-Mills from higher-D Einstein equations, Witten's D=11 coincidence (maximum
SUGRA = minimum SM gauge group), Manton's Higgs-from-geometry in 6D.

### C. D=11 Supergravity and S7 Compactification (1977-1986, 2025)
Papers: 07, 08, 10, 11, 14
Nahm's D_max=11 theorem, CJS D=11 SUGRA construction, Freund-Rubin flux-driven
spontaneous compactification (AdS4 x K7), DNP complete KK spectrum on S7 (round
and squashed), consistent truncation to N=8 gauged SUGRA.

### D. Stabilization Mechanisms (1977-2006)
Papers: 15, 18, 21, 29, 32
Cremmer-Scherk spontaneous compactification from gauge backreaction, Appelquist-Chodos
Casimir energy V_C ~ -1/rho^4, RSS monopole-flux stabilization on S2, Goldberger-Wise
bulk-scalar radion potential, Carroll-Johnson-Randall self-stabilization.

### E. Coset Space Dimensional Reduction and Chirality (1979-1984)
Papers: 17, 30, 33
Forgacs-Manton CSDR: Higgs from coset geometry, consistent truncation theorem.
Batakis-Kehagias: torsion resolves chirality obstruction. Scherk-Schwarz: SUSY breaking
from twisted boundary conditions on S1.

### F. Modern Phenomenology: Large/Warped Extra Dimensions (1998-2003)
Papers: 16, 19, 20, 25
DDG power-law coupling running from KK tower. ADD hierarchy solution via large
flat extra dimensions. Randall-Sundrum warped geometry and exponential hierarchy.
ACD precision electroweak bounds on UED (1/R > 350 GeV for 1 extra dim).

### G. Vacuum Instability (1982)
Papers: 13
Witten bubble of nothing: M4 x S1 vacuum unstable via gravitational instanton.
Tunneling rate ~ exp(-pi R^2/G_5). Fermions required for stabilization.
CRITICAL constraint for any KK-based framework.

### H. KK Dark Matter and Dark Energy (2003-2025)
Papers: 22, 24, 26, 27, 28
Servant-Tait LKP dark matter from UED with KK parity. Montero-Vafa dark dimension
(micron-scale extra dim from swampland, sterile neutrino tower). CMS/ATLAS 2024:
scalar KK DM ruled out, vector viable at M_KK ~ 40 TeV. Low-reheating resurrection.
Evolving dark sector with DESI DR2 fit.

### I. Reviews and Lecture Notes
Papers: 12, 31
Overduin-Wesson: compactified vs projective vs noncompactified approaches.
Pope lecture notes: full pedagogical treatment including CY, AdS/CFT, moduli.

## Paper Entries

### Paper 01: Nordstrom (1914)
- **Citation**: G. Nordstrom, Phys. Zeitschr. 15 (1914) 504
- **Content**: 5D scalar gravity unifying EM and gravity in flat 5D spacetime. Uses 5D Maxwell-like field equations with fifth coordinate w. Identifies charge conservation with 5th momentum component. Predates Kaluza by 7 years.
- **Note**: OCR quality very poor (garbled Unicode). Key equations partially readable: antisymmetric tensor fmn, Poynting vector S = c[F,H] - w_t w, energy density = (1/2)(F^2 + H^2 + (w')^2 + w_t^2/c^2).
- **Relevance**: LOW. Historical priority only. Physical content superseded by Kaluza.

### Paper 02: Kaluza (1921)
- **Citation**: Th. Kaluza, Sitzungsber. Preuss. Akad. Wiss. Berlin (Math. Phys.) 1921, 966-972
- **Content**: Founding paper. 5D Riemannian metric g_rs with cylinder condition. Sets g_0u = 2q_u (EM potential), g_00 = 2g (scalar). Christoffel symbols split into gravity + EM + scalar gradient. Ricci tensor R_uv -> gravitational eqs, R_0u -> Maxwell eqs, R_00 -> Poisson for scalar. Geodesic equation gives Lorentz force law. Charge = 5th momentum component.
- **Key equations**: (1) Gamma_0u = F_uv (rotation); (2) R_0u = -nabla F -> Maxwell; (3) geodesic -> Lorentz force; (4) rho_0/rho_mass = kappa = 3.06e-14.
- **Relevance**: CRITICAL. Defines the program. Framework ansatz M4 x K generalizes this.

### Paper 03: Klein (1926)
- **Citation**: O. Klein, Zeitschr. f. Phys. 37 (1926) 895-906
- **Content**: Connects Kaluza's 5D theory to de Broglie/Schrodinger quantum theory. Compactifies 5th dimension on circle S1 of period l. Klein-Gordon equation in 5D with periodic BC gives quantized charge e_n = n*e. Derives charge quantization from p_5 = n*hbar/R. Estimates R ~ 10^{-30} cm from e and G.
- **Key equations**: (1) ds^2 = gamma_ik dx^i dx^k with gamma_05 = alpha*phi_u; (2) charge quantization e = sqrt(16piG*hbar/c)/R; (3) KK mass m_n = n/R.
- **Relevance**: CRITICAL. Establishes compactification paradigm. KK tower m_n = n/R.

### Paper 04: Einstein-Bergmann (1938)
- **Citation**: A. Einstein, P. Bergmann, Ann. Math. 39 (1938) 683-701
- **Content**: Three axioms: (I) 5D Riemannian space, (II) periodicity gamma_AB(x,x5+L) = gamma_AB(x,x5), (III) variational principle from 5D Einstein-Hilbert action. Metric decomposition ds^2 = g_uv dx^u dx^v + phi^2(dx^5 + A_u dx^u)^2 gives 15 = 10+4+1 components. Maxwell: nabla(phi^3 F^uv) = 0. Scalar eq: Box phi = (phi/4) F^2. Scalar phi = dilaton/radion controlling circle radius. Einstein set phi=const by hand (breaking 5D symmetry).
- **Key equations**: (1) 15-component metric decomposition; (2) phi^3 Maxwell; (3) scalar sourced by F^2; (4) e_n = n*hbar/R charge quantization.
- **Relevance**: CRITICAL. First modern KK formulation. Dilaton = modulus = tau analog.

### Paper 05: DeWitt (1964)
- **Citation**: B. S. DeWitt, Les Houches 1963, Gordon and Breach (1964) pp. 585-820
- **Content**: Non-abelian KK: replace U(1) by compact Lie group G, S1 by group manifold G or coset G/H. Metric on principal fiber bundle decomposes into gravity + YM connection + scalars. Gauge transformations = diffeomorphisms of fiber. Structure constants from Killing vector commutators. Background field method. Heat kernel / Seeley-DeWitt expansion: K(t,x,x) ~ (4pi*t)^{-d/2} sum a_n t^n. Problem #77: work out non-abelian KK explicitly.
- **Key equations**: (1) ds^2 = g_uv dx^u dx^v + h_ab(dy^a + A^alpha K^a_alpha dx^u)(dy^b + A^beta K^b_beta dx^v); (2) [K_alpha, K_beta] = f^gamma_{alpha beta} K_gamma; (3) Heat kernel a_n coefficients.
- **Relevance**: CRITICAL. Seeds entire non-abelian KK program + spectral action.

### Paper 06: Kerner (1968)
- **Citation**: R. Kerner, Ann. Inst. H. Poincare A 9 (1968) 143-152
- **Content**: Explicit derivation: principal fiber bundle with Killing metric on semi-simple G. Bundle metric from (a) horizontal perp vertical, (b) projection = base metric, (c) vertical = Killing form. Computes Christoffel symbols, Ricci tensor. R(bundle) = K(base) - (1/4)g_ab F^a_ij F^{bij} (eq 26). Yang-Mills eqs emerge from R_AB = 0. Geodesic on bundle -> Lorentz-like force with generalized charge Q_a = g_ab (dx^b/ds).
- **Key equations**: (1) L = sqrt(g)(K - (1/4)g_ab F^a F^b); (2) nabla_j(sqrt(g) F^{aij}) = 0 (YM on curved spacetime); (3) T_rs = F^a_{rk} F^{ak}_s - (1/4)g_rs F^a F^a (EM tensor analog).
- **Relevance**: CRITICAL. Proves YM from KK. Killing metric on G = our Baptista setup.

### Paper 07: Nahm (1978)
- **Citation**: W. Nahm, Nucl. Phys. B135 (1978) 149
- **Content**: Proves D=11 is maximum spacetime dimension for supergravity with spins <= 2. Classification of supersymmetry algebras and their representations.
- **Note**: File contains only "(No extractable text - may be scanned/image-based PDF)".
- **Relevance**: HIGH. D_max=11 theorem. Combined with Witten D_min=11 for SM -> D=11 coincidence.

### Paper 08: CJS / D'Auria-Fre (1978/1982)
- **Citation**: E. Cremmer, B. Julia, J. Scherk, Phys. Lett. B76 (1978) 409; R. D'Auria, P. Fre, Nucl. Phys. B201 (1982) 101
- **Content**: File is actually D'Auria-Fre 1982 (geometric formulation). Identifies hidden supergroup of D=11 SUGRA via Cartan integrable system. Generators: J_ab (Lorentz), P_a (translation), Q (SUSY), Z_{a1a2} and Z_{a1...a5} (skew-symmetric tensors), Q' (additional spinorial). Curvatures given in eqs (1.5a-d). Osp(32|1) proposed as supergroup candidate.
- **Key equations**: (1.5a-d) Curvature forms of D=11 SUGRA on Cartan integrable system.
- **Relevance**: HIGH. D=11 SUGRA = master theory. 3-form A_MNP -> Freund-Rubin flux.

### Paper 09: Witten (1981)
- **Citation**: E. Witten, Nucl. Phys. B186 (1981) 412-428
- **Content**: D=11 is simultaneously max (Nahm) and min for SM gauge group SU(3)xSU(2)xU(1). S7 has Isom=SO(8) containing SM group. Rank argument: rank(SM)=4, need dim(K)>=2*rank=8, but D=11 gives K7. Resolution: S7 works because SO(8) has rank 4. Chirality obstruction: Dirac index on K7 vanishes for Einstein manifolds with R>0 (Lichnerowicz), so 4D fermions are vector-like. Torsion proposed as possible fix.
- **Key equations**: (1) D=11 = max(SUGRA) = min(SM via KK); (2) index(D_K7) = 0 on Einstein K7; (3) Isom(S7) = SO(8) contains SU(3)xSU(2)xU(1).
- **Relevance**: CRITICAL. Defines the D=11 program and its chirality limitation.

### Paper 10: Freund-Rubin (1980)
- **Citation**: P. G. O. Freund, M. A. Rubin, Phys. Lett. B97 (1980) 233-235
- **Content**: 4-form flux F_MNPQ along 4D spacetime drives spontaneous compactification to AdS4 x K7. Extended space is anti-de Sitter (negative Lambda), compact space has positive curvature. Two branches: M4xK7 (physical) and M7xK4. Ratio R_AdS/R_K7 = -8/7 (parameter-free). Round S7 preserves N=8 SUSY and is stable.
- **Key equations**: (1) T_uv = -(s-1)^2/(2s) f^2 g_uv; (2) T_ij = +(s-1)/(2s) f^2 g_ij; (3) R_AdS4/R_S7 = -8/7 for d=11,s=4.
- **Relevance**: CRITICAL. Spontaneous compactification mechanism. FR potential used in framework V_eff = V_FR(tau) + eta*V_spec(tau).

### Paper 11: Duff-Nilsson-Pope (2025)
- **Citation**: M. J. Duff, B. E. W. Nilsson, C. N. Pope, arXiv:2502.07710 (2025)
- **Content**: Retrospective invited chapter. Round S7 (N=8), left-squashed S7 (N=1), right-squashed S7 (N=0). Topics: spontaneous compactification, holonomy and SUSY, topology vs geometry, squashing and Higgs, vacuum stability, consistent truncations, complete squashed S7 spectrum. Improved formalism on general reductive coset spaces G/H (Section 8.1).
- **Key results**: Left-squashed S7 stable (N=1), right-squashed unstable (N=0). Consistent truncation to N=8 gauged SUGRA proven.
- **Relevance**: HIGH. Squashed S7 spectrum methods directly analogous to Jensen-deformed SU(3).

### Paper 12: Overduin-Wesson (1997)
- **Citation**: J. M. Overduin, P. S. Wesson, Phys. Rep. 283 (1997) 303-378
- **Content**: Comprehensive review from GR perspective. Three approaches: compactified (standard KK), projective (extra coords not physically real; Jordan, Thiry), noncompactified (large/infinite extra dims). Historical overview from Kepler to modern. Cosmological and astrophysical constraints. Key insight: projective theories avoid compactification entirely but sacrifice physical reality of extra coordinate.
- **Relevance**: MEDIUM. Broad review. Projective approach connects to Jordan-Brans-Dicke scalar-tensor theories.

### Paper 13: Witten (1982)
- **Citation**: E. Witten, Nucl. Phys. B195 (1982) 481-492
- **Content**: Bubble of nothing: gravitational instanton mediates decay of M4 x S1 vacuum. Circle radius shrinks to zero inside bubble. Tunneling rate ~ exp(-pi R^2/G_5). Bubble expands at c, consuming spacetime. Violates positive energy conjecture via topology change. Fermions required for stabilization (spinor zero modes on instanton modify path integral).
- **Key equations**: (1) S_inst ~ pi R^2/G_5; (2) rho(t) = sqrt(R^2 + t^2) (bubble wall); (3) exponent ~ (M_Pl/m_KK)^2/16.
- **Relevance**: CRITICAL. Must-address for any KK framework. pi_1(SU(3))=0 is framework's topological defense, but explicit instanton analysis needed.

### Paper 14: Duff-Nilsson-Pope (1986)
- **Citation**: M. J. Duff, B. E. W. Nilsson, C. N. Pope, Phys. Rep. 130 (1986) 1-142
- **Content**: Canonical reference for KK SUGRA. 11D SUGRA action. Consistent truncation on S7 to N=8 gauged SUGRA in 4D. Full KK mass spectrum: m_n = sqrt(n(n+6))/rho on S7. Coupling emergence: g^2 ~ rho^{-7}. Stability: classical S7 stable, quantum Casimir tends to contract rho. SO(8) representation content of massless sector.
- **Key equations**: (1) lambda_n = n(n+6) (S7 scalar Laplacian); (2) m_n = sqrt(n(n+6))/rho; (3) g^2 ~ rho^{-7}; (4) M_P^2 = pi*rho^7/(2*kappa_11^2).
- **Relevance**: CRITICAL. Mass spectrum formula. Spectral methods for KK on coset spaces.

### Paper 15: Appelquist-Chodos (1983)
- **Citation**: T. Appelquist, A. Chodos, Phys. Rev. D28 (1983) 772; PRL 50 (1983) 141
- **Content**: One-loop Casimir energy on M4 x S1. V_C(rho) = -zeta(4)/(2pi^2 rho^4) for single scalar. Always attractive (shrinks extra dim). For M4 x K_d: V_C ~ -1/rho^{d+1}. External stabilization required. Casimir from infinite KK tower zero-point energy.
- **Key equations**: (1) V_C ~ -alpha/rho^4 (5D); (2) V_C ~ -1/rho^{d+1} (general); (3) m_n^2 = n^2/rho^2 (S1 KK masses).
- **Relevance**: HIGH. Template for quantum stabilization. Framework's V_spec(tau) is spectral analog of Casimir.

### Paper 16: Dienes-Dudas-Gherghetta (1998)
- **Citation**: K. R. Dienes, E. Dudas, T. Gherghetta, Phys. Lett. B436 (1998) 55-65
- **Content**: KK tower modifies gauge coupling running: power-law corrections above compactification scale M_c. Intermediate-scale GUT at 10^{10}-10^{12} GeV becomes possible. Yukawa hierarchy from KK power-law corrections. Proton decay suppressed by KK selection rules.
- **Key equations**: (1) b_a -> b_a + Delta b_a^{KK} (modified beta functions); (2) power-law Yukawa corrections ~ ln(M_c/mu); (3) GUT unification at M_c << M_GUT.
- **Relevance**: HIGH. DDG power-law running with full 992-mode tower is a pipeline priority (resolves M_KK ambiguity).

### Paper 17: Forgacs-Manton (1980)
- **Citation**: P. Forgacs, N. S. Manton, Commun. Math. Phys. 72 (1980) 15-35
- **Content**: CSDR formalism. Higher-D YM on M4 x G/H. Symmetric (H-invariant) fields reduce to 4D gauge theory of H with Higgs mechanism. Scalars emerge from internal gauge components. Higgs potential V(phi) ~ F^a_{IJ} F^{aIJ} entirely geometric. Consistent truncation theorem proved. Representation-theoretic decomposition gives branching rules for matter content.
- **Key equations**: (1) S_4 = int d4x [-(1/4)F_H^2 + (D_u phi)^2 - V(phi)]; (2) V(phi) ~ Tr([A_i,A_j]^2); (3) Branching: adj(G) -> sum of H-reps.
- **Relevance**: CRITICAL. CSDR branching rules needed for B/F assignment (pipeline priority 1). Higgs from coset = framework's Higgs from second fundamental form.

### Paper 18: Randjbar-Daemi-Salam-Strathdee (1983)
- **Citation**: S. Randjbar-Daemi, A. Salam, J. Strathdee, Nucl. Phys. B214 (1983) 491-511
- **Content**: 6D Einstein-Maxwell on M4 x S2 with monopole flux. Exact classical solution. Radius determined by monopole charge n: rho = n/sqrt(2pi G_6). V_eff(rho) has true stable minimum (d^2V/drho^2 > 0). Low-energy theory: SU(2)xU(1) with chiral fermions.
- **Key equations**: (1) F_{theta phi} = n sin(theta)/rho^2; (2) V_eff = 1/(2rho^2) - n^2/(8pi G_6 rho^4); (3) rho_eq = n/sqrt(2pi G_6).
- **Relevance**: HIGH. Prototype for dynamical compactification. Topology (flux) determines geometry (radius).

### Paper 19: Arkani-Hamed-Dimopoulos-Dvali (1998)
- **Citation**: N. Arkani-Hamed, S. Dimopoulos, G. Dvali, Phys. Lett. B429 (1998) 263
- **Content**: Hierarchy problem solved by large flat extra dimensions. M_P^2 = M_{4+n}^{2+n}(2pi R)^n. For n=2, R ~ mm gives M_{4+n} ~ TeV. Sub-mm gravity deviates from 1/r^2. KK graviton tower density rho(E) ~ E^{n-1}. Collider signatures from real/virtual KK graviton production.
- **Key equations**: (1) M_P^2 = M_*^{2+n} V_n; (2) V(r) ~ -1/r^{1+n} for r < R; (3) rho(E) ~ E^{n-1}.
- **Relevance**: HIGH. Establishes phenomenological reality of KK modes. Framework operates at different scale but same structural principles.

### Paper 20: Randall-Sundrum (1999)
- **Citation**: L. Randall, R. Sundrum, Phys. Rev. Lett. 83 (1999) 3370-3373
- **Content**: Warped 5D metric ds^2 = e^{-2ky} eta_uv dx^u dx^v + dy^2. Exponential hierarchy from warp factor: m_obs = e^{-2k pi r_c} m_Pl. For 2k pi r_c ~ 35, maps Planck to TeV. KK graviton spectrum non-uniform; first mode at m_1 ~ k*e^{-k pi r_c}. Radion is flat direction (needs Goldberger-Wise).
- **Key equations**: (1) e^{-2k pi r_c} ~ 10^{-15}; (2) m_1^{KK} ~ k*e^{-k pi r_c}; (3) Conformal: ds^2 = (1/k^2 z^2)(eta + dz^2) = AdS5 Poincare.
- **Relevance**: HIGH. Warped geometry paradigm. Framework's g'/g = sqrt(3)*e^{-2tau} is analogous exponential hierarchy.

### Paper 21: Goldberger-Wise (1999)
- **Citation**: W. D. Goldberger, M. B. Wise, Phys. Rev. Lett. 83 (1999) 4922-4925
- **Content**: Stabilizes RS radion via bulk scalar Psi with brane-localized potentials. Psi satisfies Bessel equation in AdS5 background. Boundary conditions Psi(0)=v_P, Psi(pi r_c)=v_T generate radion potential V ~ e^{-2k pi r_c} lambda(v_P,v_T). Radion mass m_phi = kappa*k*e^{-k pi r_c} ~ TeV. No significant fine-tuning.
- **Key equations**: (1) Psi'' - 2k Psi' + m^2 e^{2ky} Psi = 0; (2) V_radion ~ e^{-2k pi r_c}; (3) m_radion ~ k*e^{-k pi r_c} ~ TeV.
- **Relevance**: HIGH. Modulus stabilization paradigm. Framework's BCS stabilization is the non-perturbative analog.

### Paper 22: Servant-Tait (2003)
- **Citation**: G. Servant, T. M. P. Tait, Nucl. Phys. B650 (2003) 391-419
- **Content**: LKP (lightest KK particle) as WIMP dark matter from UED. KK parity P_KK = (-1)^n ensures stability. Thermal freeze-out gives Omega h^2 ~ 0.12 for m_LKP ~ 1 TeV (WIMP miracle). Coannihilation with heavier KK states. Direct detection sigma_SI ~ 10^{-45} cm^2.
- **Key equations**: (1) m_n = sqrt(m_0^2 + n^2/R^2); (2) Omega h^2 ~ (3e-27 cm^3/s)/sigma_v; (3) P_KK = (-1)^n.
- **Relevance**: MEDIUM. KK DM concept. Framework DM arises differently (GGE quasiparticles, not thermal relic).

### Paper 23: Manton (1979)
- **Citation**: N. S. Manton, Nucl. Phys. B158 (1979) 141-153
- **Content**: Weinberg-Salam model from 6D pure YM. Gauge group SU(3) -> SU(2)xU(1) via compactification. Higgs = A_5 (internal gauge component). Higgs potential V(phi) ~ Tr([A_i,A_j]^2)/R^2 is geometric. Wilson line W = exp(i oint A_5 dy) acts as order parameter. Higgs mass m_H ~ 1/R, quartic lambda ~ g_YM^2.

### Paper 29: Cremmer-Scherk (1977)
- **Citation**: E. Cremmer, J. Scherk, Nucl. Phys. B118 (1977) 61
- **Content**: First demonstration that extra dimensions can acquire finite size dynamically. YM energy density backreacts on 5D metric, compressing fifth dimension. Equilibrium radius b_eq ~ g_5/sqrt(M_5^3 E_i). Modulus becomes dynamical radion field. 4D gauge coupling g_4^2 ~ g_5^2/b_eq emerges geometrically.

### Paper 30: Batakis-Kehagias (1984)
- **Citation**: N. A. Batakis, A. A. Kehagias, J. Math. Phys. 25 (1984) 2696
- **Content**: Torsion in 5D spin connection resolves chirality obstruction. Zero-mode equation with torsion T: (gamma^5 d_5 + T/2)chi_0 = 0. Oriented torsion makes one chiral zero mode normalizable, other not. One massless chiral fermion per torsion component. Excited modes remain vector-like.
- **Key equations**: (1) D = gamma^A(e_A^u d_u + (1/4)omega_{AB} Sigma^{AB}); (2) chi_0^{L/R} ~ exp(-int T_{L/R}/2 dy); (3) m_n^2 = (n/R)^2 + torsion corrections.

### Paper 32: Carroll-Johnson-Randall (2006)
- **Citation**: S. M. Carroll, M. L. Johnson, L. Randall, Phys. Rev. D73 (2006) 124019
- **Content**: Self-stabilization of radion from gravitational backreaction of KK tower. Each KK mode shifts potential; cancellation at special radius gives m_radion ~ M_KK. No external fields needed (unlike Goldberger-Wise). Requires specific boundary kinetic terms c_P + c_T ~ 0.
- **Key equations**: (1) V_grav(phi) = sum_n (m_n(phi)/M_Pl)^4 g_n(phi); (2) m_radion ~ 0.5-1.0 M_KK at self-stabilization.
- **Relevance**: MEDIUM. Self-stabilization principle. Framework's spectral action plays analogous role to gravitational backreaction.

### Paper 33: Scherk-Schwarz (1979)
- **Citation**: J. Scherk, J. H. Schwarz, Phys. Lett. B82 (1979) 60
- **Content**: SUSY breaking from twisted boundary conditions on S1. Bosons periodic, fermions antiperiodic. Fermion KK masses shifted: m_n^{ferm} = (n+1/2)/R. Ground state splitting Delta m = 1/(2R). M_SUSY = M_KK/2. No scalar potential needed. No moduli problem (unique vacuum). LSP mass = 1/(2R), stable DM candidate.
- **Key equations**: (1) m_n^{boson} = n/R; (2) m_n^{fermion} = (n+1/2)/R; (3) M_SUSY = 1/(2R) = M_KK/2.
- **Relevance**: MEDIUM. Geometry determines spectrum (twisted BC analog of Jensen deformation). Framework uses deformation not twist.
