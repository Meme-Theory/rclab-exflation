# The Hierarchy Problem and New Dimensions at a Millimeter

**Author(s):** Nima Arkani-Hamed, Savas Dimopoulos, Gia Dvali

**Year:** 1998

**Journal:** arXiv:hep-ph/9803315

---

## Abstract

We propose solving the hierarchy problem—the enormous ratio between the electroweak scale and the Planck scale—via large extra spatial dimensions rather than supersymmetry or technicolor. We argue that the apparent weakness of gravity stems from the dilution of gravitational flux through compact extra dimensions, while the gauge interactions remain confined to four dimensions. For n≥2 extra dimensions of size R, gravity becomes strong at energy scales $M_* \sim (M_P^2 / R^n)^{1/(2+n)}$, naturally yielding a weak four-dimensional Planck scale despite a fundamental physics scale of order TeV. We show that gravitational effects modify from inverse-square to inverse-r^{-2-n} at distances below R, making sub-millimeter gravity measurements a direct probe of extra dimensions. Standard Model particles confined to a four-dimensional brane exhibit energy loss through Kaluza-Klein graviton production in collider experiments.

---

## Historical Context

The hierarchy problem—why gravity is 32 orders of magnitude weaker than electromagnetism—has motivated theoretical physics for decades. The standard response was to invoke supersymmetry, stabilizing the Higgs mass through cancellation of bosonic and fermionic loop contributions. However, ADD (Arkani-Hamed, Dimopoulos, Dvali) provided a radically different answer: gravity is not weak intrinsically; it simply spreads across extra dimensions.

This 1998 paper inaugurated the modern era of large extra dimensions in phenomenology. It bypassed the traditional solution (introducing SUSY at the TeV scale) by geometrizing the hierarchy: the Planck scale in 4D is a derived quantity, not fundamental. The five-dimensional Planck scale M_5 can be as low as ~TeV if the extra dimension is millimeter-scale. This immediately made the framework testable through gravitational experiments and motivated a vigorous program of collider searches for Kaluza-Klein graviton resonances.

The paper was provocative because it violated the conventional wisdom that extra dimensions must be Planck-scale-sized. It opened the possibility that the universe might have macroscopic extra dimensions—a bold departure from string theory orthodoxy at the time.

## Key Arguments and Derivations

### The Hierarchy Problem in 4D

In four spacetime dimensions, gravity couples with Newton's constant G_N, related to the Planck mass:

$$M_P = \sqrt{\frac{\hbar c}{G_N}} \approx 1.22 \times 10^{19} \text{ GeV}$$

The electroweak scale is:

$$M_{EW} \sim 100 \text{ GeV}$$

The hierarchy is:

$$\frac{M_P}{M_{EW}} \approx 10^{17}$$

The Higgs mass radiative corrections grow logarithmically with the cutoff scale, requiring fine-tuning to keep the Higgs at 100 GeV if the fundamental scale is $10^{19}$ GeV.

### Gravity in Extra Dimensions

In a 4+n-dimensional spacetime with n compact extra dimensions of uniform radius R, the gravitational action is:

$$S = \int d^{4+n}x \sqrt{-g} \frac{M_{4+n}^{2+n}}{2} R$$

where $M_{4+n}$ is the fundamental Planck scale. Compactifying the extra dimensions on a torus $T^n$, the four-dimensional effective Planck mass is:

$$M_P^2 = M_{4+n}^{2+n} \cdot V_n = M_{4+n}^{2+n} \cdot (2\pi R)^n$$

where $V_n = (2\pi R)^n$ is the volume of the compact space. Thus:

$$M_P^2 = M_{4+n}^{2+n} (2\pi R)^n$$

Rearranging:

$$M_{4+n} = \left( \frac{M_P^2}{(2\pi R)^n} \right)^{1/(2+n)}$$

For n=2 and choosing $(2\pi R)$ to be millimeter-scale (10^{-3} m), one finds $M_{4+n} \sim 1$ TeV—the natural weak scale.

### Kaluza-Klein Tower

Once the extra dimensions are compactified, the metric components along extra directions become scalar fields in 4D. The graviton propagating in 4+n dimensions decomposes into a KK tower:

$$g_{\mu\nu}^{(4+n)} = g_{\mu\nu}^{(4)} + \phi^{(1)}_{\mu\nu}(x) \cos(m n_1 / R) \cdots$$

where $n_1, n_2, \ldots$ are winding numbers. Each KK level has mass:

$$m_{n_1, n_2, \ldots} = \sqrt{\sum_i (n_i / R)^2}$$

For millimeter R, the spacing is $\Delta m \sim 1 / R \sim 10^{-3}$ eV, creating a very dense tower near the TeV scale.

The density of states at energy E in d spacetime dimensions is:

$$\rho(E) \propto E^{d-3}$$

For d=6 (n=2), $\rho(E) \sim E^3$, meaning KK graviton production becomes highly probable at TeV collider energies.

### Cross Sections for KK Graviton Production

In a 4+n-dimensional theory with fundamental scale $M_* \sim 1$ TeV, the 4D gravitational coupling becomes strong. The cross section for a Standard Model process (e.g., $e^+ e^- \to \gamma \gamma$) to proceed through KK graviton exchange grows dramatically at center-of-mass energies approaching $M_*$.

For large-n, the amplitude for Drell-Yan-type processes ($q \bar{q} \to KK$ resonance) is:

$$\sigma(q \bar{q} \to T_{KK}) \sim \frac{1}{M_*^2} \rho(M_*) \sim \frac{E^{n-1}}{M_*^{2+n}}$$

This exhibits power-law growth, not logarithmic, creating a distinct collider signature.

### Sub-millimeter Gravity

At distances $\ell < R$, the four-dimensional effective field theory breaks down. The gravitational potential transitions from Newton's law:

$$V(r) = -\frac{G_N m_1 m_2}{r}$$

to the 4+n-dimensional form:

$$V(r) = -\frac{M_{4+n}^{-(2+n)}}{r^{1+n}}$$

For n=2:

$$V(r) = -\frac{C}{r^3} \quad \text{for } r < R$$

Torsion-balance experiments (like Cavendish torsion balances) can measure this deviation, providing direct experimental tests of the ADD model.

## Key Results

1. **Fundamental scale descent**: The fundamental Planck scale can be lowered to TeV by making extra dimensions macroscopic ($R \sim 10^{-3}$ m for n=2).

2. **KK graviton signature**: Collider searches see resonances and missing energy from KK graviton production, with cross sections exceeding Standard Model backgrounds at high energies.

3. **Sub-millimeter gravity bound**: Torsion balance measurements constrain R depending on n: for n=2, R must satisfy $R < 0.2$ mm (from 1998 data). Improvements push the bound tighter.

4. **Tower density**: The density of KK states grows rapidly: $\rho(E) \propto E^{n-1}$, making the tower quasi-continuous above the fundamental scale.

5. **Unified fundamental scale**: All interactions—gravity, electromagnetism, weak, strong—meet at a single fundamental scale $M_* \sim 1$ TeV, potentially amenable to grand unification in 4+n dimensions.

## Impact and Legacy

The ADD model revolutionized thinking about extra dimensions in phenomenology. Within months, experimental groups began designing searches for KK gravitons at the Tevatron and, later, the LHC. The paper established a new paradigm: extra dimensions need not be invisible—they can have direct observational consequences.

The model motivated:

- **Precision electroweak searches** for KK graviton virtual exchange
- **Collider resonance hunts** for real KK graviton production
- **Astrophysical constraints** on extra dimensions from stellar cooling (KK gravitons escape, carrying energy)
- **Quantum gravity phenomenology** at collider scales

The ADD framework also bridged string theory and collider physics, making string-scale phenomena experimentally testable. While searches at the LHC have not yet confirmed the existence of macroscopic extra dimensions (implying R < 10^{-4} m for n=2, pushing M_* above TeV), the theoretical framework remains influential in studies of fundamental scale physics.
