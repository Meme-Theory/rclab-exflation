# A Large Mass Hierarchy from a Small Extra Dimension

**Author(s):** Lisa Randall, Raman Sundrum

**Year:** 1999

**Journal:** Physical Review Letters 83:3370–3373; arXiv:hep-ph/9905221

---

## Abstract

We present a new approach to the hierarchy problem using warped extra-dimensional geometry. Instead of invoking large extra dimensions (as in ADD), we show that an exponential mass hierarchy emerges from the metric structure itself. A single extra spatial dimension, warped via an Anti-de Sitter (AdS) metric, naturally generates a weak scale from the Planck scale through geometric exponential suppression. The Standard Model fields are localized on a brane in this warped spacetime, and gravitational interactions propagate through the bulk. The model predicts spin-2 excitations (KK gravitons) at the weak scale, coupled with weak-scale strength rather than gravitational strength to Standard Model matter—a distinct phenomenology compared to large extra dimensions.

---

## Historical Context

The Randall-Sundrum (RS) model (1999) solved the hierarchy problem in a qualitatively different way from ADD (1998). Whereas ADD used large extra dimensions to dilute gravity, RS used warped geometry—a metric that is not uniform across the extra dimension. The warp factor in the metric acts as an exponential redshift factor, compressing energy scales.

Randall and Sundrum's key insight was that the AdS geometry—naturally arising from Einstein's equations with a negative bulk cosmological constant—provides precisely the redshift needed to map the Planck scale on one brane down to the weak scale on another. This was revolutionary because it required only a small extra dimension (not macroscopic), making the scenario more compatible with traditional string theory expectations.

The RS model immediately became a cornerstone of extra-dimensional phenomenology, spawning thousands of papers on warped geometry, KK graviton searches, and holographic duality. It also provided a geometric context for understanding why the Higgs mass is naturally weak—a profound shift in thinking about fundamental physics.

## Key Arguments and Derivations

### Warped Geometry Setup

Consider a five-dimensional spacetime with coordinates $(x^\mu, y)$ where $\mu = 0,1,2,3$ and $y \in [0, \pi r_c]$. The metric is:

$$ds^2 = e^{-2ky} \eta_{\mu\nu} dx^\mu dx^\nu + dy^2$$

where $k$ is the AdS curvature scale, $\eta_{\mu\nu}$ is the 4D Minkowski metric, and $r_c$ is the size of the extra dimension. The warp factor is $e^{-2ky}$—an exponential function of position in the extra dimension.

This metric solves Einstein's equations with a bulk cosmological constant $\Lambda = -6k^2 M_*^3$ (in 5D) and a stress-energy tensor on the branes.

### Brane Localization

Two 3-branes are placed:
- Planck brane at $y = 0$: The Planck mass is defined here.
- TeV brane at $y = \pi r_c$: Standard Model fields are localized here.

A field's mass as measured on a brane is redshifted by the warp factor:

$$m_{\text{observed}} = e^{-2k\pi r_c} m_{\text{Planck}}$$

If $2k\pi r_c \approx 35$, then:

$$m_{\text{observed}} \sim e^{-35} m_{\text{Planck}} \sim 10^{-15} m_{\text{Planck}}$$

This maps a Planck-scale mass to a TeV-scale mass, solving the hierarchy without fine-tuning the fundamental parameters ($k$ and $r_c$ are of order unity in Planck units).

### Conformal Properties

The RS metric admits a conformal rescaling. Changing to conformal coordinates $z = e^{ky}$, the metric becomes:

$$ds^2 = \frac{1}{k^2 z^2} \left( \eta_{\mu\nu} dx^\mu dx^\nu + dz^2 \right)$$

This is the metric on AdS_5 in Poincaré coordinates. The conformal structure makes the model amenable to holographic analysis (later developed in the AdS/CFT context).

### KK Graviton Spectrum

The graviton propagates in the bulk and couples to matter on both branes. Solving the 5D Einstein equations with boundary conditions at the branes yields a KK decomposition:

$$g_{\mu\nu}^{(5)}(x, y) = \sum_n h_\mu^\nu{^{(n)}}(x) f_n(y)$$

where $f_n(y)$ satisfies a Sturm-Liouville equation with appropriate boundary conditions. The KK masses are not uniformly spaced; instead, the spectrum has a particular structure. The lightest KK graviton has mass:

$$m_1 \sim k e^{-k\pi r_c} \approx k e^{-35}$$

For $k \sim$ weak scale (a few hundred GeV), the first KK mode has mass $m_1 \sim$ a few TeV. Crucially, its coupling to Standard Model matter is $O(1)$ in units of its mass, making it strongly interacting—not gravitationally weak.

### Hierarchy Generation Without Tuning

The key advantage over ADD: the hierarchy does not require exponentially large extra dimensions. The exponential hierarchy comes from the warp factor $e^{-2k\pi r_c}$, which is generated by classical geometry, not by taking volume factors to enormous sizes.

For $k \sim 10-100$ GeV and $r_c \sim 10-100$ (in Planck units or as a ratio $r_c / M_*^{-1}$), one naturally gets $e^{-k\pi r_c} \sim 10^{-15}$, solving the hierarchy. No millimeter-scale extra dimensions needed.

### Radion Field

The radion—the 4D scalar corresponding to variations in $r_c$—plays a crucial role. Its potential in the original RS model is flat; the Goldberger-Wise mechanism (see Paper #21) adds a bulk scalar to stabilize it.

The effective radion coupling to the Standard Model Higgs is:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\text{int}} \sim \frac{r_c}{M_*} \left( \partial_\mu r_c \right) T^\mu{_\mu}$$

where $T^\mu{_\mu}$ is the trace of the Standard Model stress-energy tensor. The radion mass, once stabilized, is typically 10–100 GeV, making it accessible at colliders.

## Key Results

1. **Warped hierarchy**: An exponential hierarchy emerges from conformal warping without large extra dimensions, requiring only $e^{-2k\pi r_c} \approx 10^{-15}$.

2. **KK graviton phenomenology**: The lightest KK graviton has mass of order 1–10 TeV and couples with weak-scale strength, producing distinctive collider signatures (dilepton resonances, graviton production in $t\bar{t}$ events, etc.).

3. **Radion dynamics**: The radion is a light scalar at the weak scale, distinct from the Higgs, with couplings that can be probed experimentally.

4. **Natural weak scale**: The Higgs mass and weak-scale physics emerge naturally without fine-tuning the fundamental scale or requiring SUSY partners.

5. **UV/IR correspondence**: The model exhibits early hints of holographic duality—bulk physics in AdS relates to 4D conformal field theory (later formalized in AdS/CFT).

## Impact and Legacy

The Randall-Sundrum model became one of the most influential papers in theoretical physics, with thousands of citations. It changed how theorists think about hierarchy problems and extra dimensions:

- **Warped geometry phenomenology**: An entire subfield emerged, exploring KK gluons, KK quarks, warped Higgs physics, and more.
- **Holographic duality**: The RS geometry provided a concrete realization of AdS/CFT, leading to deep connections between gravity and gauge theory.
- **Collider searches**: Experiments at the Tevatron and LHC searched intensively for KK graviton resonances predicted by RS. While null results impose constraints (favoring $k / M_* > 10^{-1}$ to keep KK masses above LHC sensitivity), the model remains viable for higher scales.
- **Beyond the Standard Model physics**: RS models with custodial symmetry, composite Higgs scenarios, and other extensions became major research directions.
