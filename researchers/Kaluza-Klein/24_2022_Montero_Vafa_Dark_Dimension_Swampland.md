# The Dark Dimension and the Swampland

**Author(s):** Miguel Montero, Cumrun Vafa, Irene Valenzuela

**Year:** 2022

**Journal:** arXiv:2205.12293

---

## Abstract

We apply the Swampland Program—the program of determining which low-energy theories can be consistently embedded in quantum gravity—to predict novel physics beyond the Standard Model. We argue that quantum gravity constraints on the scalar potential (the de Sitter Conjecture, the Distance Conjecture) require a new mesoscopic extra dimension with length scale $\ell \sim \Lambda^{-1/4} \sim 10^{-6}$ m, where $\Lambda$ is the current dark energy density scale. This extra dimension hosts massless fermions (sterile neutrinos), establishing a connection between the scale of dark energy and the scale of particle physics. The framework predicts a tower of sterile neutrino masses controlled by the Swampland conditions and links the Higgs vacuum instability scale (~$10^{11}$ GeV) to the dark energy scale through a species scale $\sim 10^9$ GeV. We show that this scenario is consistent with current cosmological observations and makes specific predictions for future experiments.

## Historical Context

The Swampland Program, initiated by Vafa around 2005, aims to characterize which effective field theories are consistent with quantum gravity. The program has produced several conjectures:

1. **de Sitter Conjecture (2019)**: All de Sitter vacua in quantum gravity are unstable or correspond to non-singular geometries. This severely constrains dark energy models.

2. **Distance Conjecture (2018)**: In any effective field theory descent from the Planck scale, traversing large distances in moduli space requires an infinite tower of states to become light, preventing smooth interpolation between distant theories.

3. **Convexity Conjecture**: Scalar potentials must be convex (or have controlled violations), limiting quintessence and other time-varying scalar scenarios.

These conjectures, derived from string theory and black hole physics, have profound implications for cosmology. The Montero-Vafa paper uses Swampland constraints to make a concrete prediction: dark energy and particle physics are linked by a new intermediate-scale extra dimension.

This prediction is remarkable because it suggests that dark energy, usually thought to be a cosmological-scale phenomenon, is actually connected to TeV-scale physics through higher-dimensional geometry.

## Key Arguments and Derivations

### The Swampland Distance Conjecture

In quantum gravity, any scalar field $\phi$ with range $\Delta \phi$ can traverse at most a distance:

$$\Delta \phi \lesssim M_P$$

(a few Planck masses). Traversing larger distances requires an infinite tower of light states to become asymptotically light, with masses:

$$m_n \sim M_P e^{-\lambda n}$$

where $\lambda$ is a coefficient of order unity.

The "distance" is measured in the geometry of the moduli space (the space of scalar fields). In string theory, moduli spaces (spaces of Kähler structures, Calabi-Yau shape parameters, etc.) have intrinsic geometric distance measures. Traversing large distances in such spaces is "difficult" because new states become light and dominate the dynamics.

### Application to Dark Energy

Current dark energy corresponds to an energy density:

$$\rho_\Lambda \sim (\Lambda)^4 \sim (10^{-12} \text{ GeV})^4$$

In the Swampland picture, this extremely small scale cannot be understood as a "frozen" value of a scalar field at large field excursion. Instead, it must arise from a dynamical mechanism involving many states at intermediate scales.

The Montero-Vafa proposal: introduce a new extra dimension with length:

$$\ell \sim \Lambda^{-1/4} \sim 10^{-6} \text{ m}$$

This length scale relates the dark energy density (an inverse-fourth power) to a geometric length. In this extra dimension, massless fermions propagate, creating a Kaluza-Klein tower of sterile neutrino states.

### Sterile Neutrino Tower

Fermionic fields in a compact dimension of size $\ell$ have Kaluza-Klein decomposition:

$$\Psi(x, y) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} \psi_n(x) \exp(i n y / \ell)$$

where $y \in [0, 2\pi \ell]$. The KK masses are:

$$m_n = \sqrt{m_0^2 + (n/\ell)^2}$$

For massless bulk fermions ($m_0 = 0$), the spectrum is:

$$m_n = |n| / \ell \sim n \times 10^{-6} \text{ eV}$$

This creates a "tower" of sterile neutrinos at micro-eV scales, exactly in the range sensitive to cosmological observations (CMB, large-scale structure).

### Connection to Active Neutrino Masses

The framework predicts active neutrino masses through mixing with the sterile tower. The mixing is suppressed by the compactification scale, yielding:

$$m_\nu \sim \frac{(\text{SM coupling})^2}{M_c}$$

where $M_c \sim 1/\ell \sim 10^{6}$ eV is the compactification scale. This naturally gives $m_\nu \sim 0.1$ eV, consistent with oscillation data.

### Species Scale and Higgs Instability

The "species scale" $M_s$ is the energy at which the tower of KK states becomes numerous enough to modify quantum gravity effects. Above $M_s$, the effective field theory cannot be trusted.

Montero-Vafa argue:

$$M_s \sim \sqrt{N} M_P$$

where $N$ is the number of light degrees of freedom. For the dark dimension scenario with many sterile neutrinos, $N$ is large, pushing $M_s$ down to:

$$M_s \sim 10^9 - 10^{10} \text{ GeV}$$

Remarkably, this is close to the Higgs potential instability scale (~$10^{11}$ GeV), where the Higgs self-coupling becomes negative and the potential becomes unbounded below in the Standard Model.

The coincidence is not accidental: both scales arise from the same physics (quantum gravity constraints on towers of states).

### Cosmological Evolution

The dark dimension does not expand with the universe in this framework. Instead, it remains frozen at $\ell \sim 10^{-6}$ m, serving as a cosmic "scale setter." The dark energy density is then related to properties of this extra dimension through Swampland conjectures.

In particular, the de Sitter Conjecture states that de Sitter vacua (constant-$\Lambda$ solutions) are unstable, so the dark energy evolves slightly with time. The rate of evolution is controlled by the tower of KK states, giving:

$$\frac{d\rho_\Lambda}{d\log a} \sim (\text{tower effect}) \sim 10^{-3} \rho_\Lambda$$

This predicts a subtle departure from the cosmological constant, potentially detectable by DESI and other surveys.

### Swampland Conjecture Constraints

The effective field theory remains valid only below $M_s$. This constrains any scalar potential $V(\phi)$:

$$|\frac{d\log V}{d\phi}| \gtrsim c$$

for some constant $c \sim 0.1 - 1$. Potentials that are too flat cannot exist in quantum gravity above $M_s$.

## Key Results

1. **Dark dimension scale**: A new extra dimension at $\ell \sim 10^{-6}$ m is predicted by Swampland constraints, connecting dark energy to particle physics scales.

2. **Sterile neutrino tower**: Massless fermions in the dark dimension produce a tower of sterile neutrinos with masses $m_n \sim n \times 10^{-6}$ eV, potentially detectable cosmologically.

3. **Active neutrino mass**: Active neutrino masses are naturally predicted at $m_\nu \sim 0.1$ eV through mixing with the sterile tower.

4. **Species scale**: The quantum gravity scale is lowered to $M_s \sim 10^9$ GeV, coinciding with Higgs instability and dark energy scales.

5. **De Sitter instability**: Dark energy is not a true vacuum but a slowly rolling field, predicting weak time-dependence: $w(z) = -1 + \epsilon(z)$ with $\epsilon \sim 10^{-3}$.

6. **Self-consistency**: All Swampland conjectures (Distance, de Sitter, Convexity) are satisfied simultaneously in this framework.

## Impact and Legacy

The Montero-Vafa paper is recent (2022) but highly influential:

- **Swampland implications for cosmology**: It demonstrated that abstract quantum gravity principles have concrete phenomenological consequences.
- **Sterile neutrino searches**: The prediction of a specific sterile neutrino tower has motivated experimental searches (e.g., using cosmological data to constrain $m_\nu$).
- **Dark energy dynamics**: DESI and other surveys are testing whether dark energy is truly constant ($w = -1$) or evolves dynamically (as the paper predicts).
- **Hierarchy and scale setting**: The framework provides a quantum-gravity-based explanation for the appearance of many different scales in nature (Planck, GUT, TeV, dark energy).
- **Theoretical challenges**: The paper's radical proposal (a macroscopic extra dimension at millimeter scales) has spurred debate about Swampland conjectures and their validity.
