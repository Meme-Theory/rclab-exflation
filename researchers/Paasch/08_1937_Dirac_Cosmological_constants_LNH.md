# The Cosmological Constants

**Authors**: Dirac P.A.M.
**Year**: 1937
**Journal**: Nature, vol. 139, p. 323
**DOI**: 10.1038/139323a0
**Source**: https://www.nature.com/articles/139323a0

---

## Abstract

Dirac observes that certain dimensionless numbers constructed from fundamental constants of
physics and cosmological parameters are all of order $\sim 10^{40}$ or its square. He
proposes that this cannot be a coincidence: these large numbers must be RELATED by simple
mathematical expressions. Since some of these numbers involve the age of the universe (which
changes), the relations can only be maintained if the fundamental "constants" -- including
Newton's gravitational constant $G$ -- actually vary with cosmic time.

This is the ORIGINAL paper proposing the Large Numbers Hypothesis (LNH).

---

## Historical Context

By 1937, the expanding universe was a new and revolutionary concept. Hubble's observations
(1929) had confirmed that galaxies recede with velocities proportional to their distance,
and the theoretical framework of General Relativity (1915) provided a natural setting for
cosmological expansion. But the physical interpretation of the expansion was far from settled.

Against this backdrop, Dirac -- already the most celebrated theoretical physicist of his
generation, having predicted the positron and shared the 1933 Nobel Prize -- turned his
attention to an observation first noted by Hermann Weyl (1919) and elaborated by Arthur
Eddington (1931): the dimensionless ratios formed from fundamental constants and cosmological
parameters cluster around enormous values of order $10^{40}$ and $10^{80}$.

Where Weyl and Eddington had noted the coincidence, Dirac elevated it to a PRINCIPLE. In a
brief but profoundly influential letter to Nature, he proposed that these large numbers are
not coincidences but reflections of a deep connection between microphysics and cosmology.
The hypothesis required that fundamental "constants" vary with time -- a radical departure
from the prevailing view.

This short paper launched an entire research program that persists to this day, influenced
Brans-Dicke scalar-tensor gravity, motivated Lunar Laser Ranging experiments, and provided
the cosmological scaffolding for Paasch's mass quantization framework.

## Key Arguments and Derivations

### The Large Dimensionless Numbers

Dirac begins by listing the fundamental constants of physics: the velocity of light $c$,
Planck's constant $\hbar$, the electron charge $e$, the electron mass $m_e$, and the
proton mass $m_p$. He notes that from these and the cosmological parameters (age of the
universe $t_0$, Hubble constant $H_0$), one can form dimensionless ratios.

**Ratio 1: Electromagnetic to gravitational force**

$$N_1 = \frac{e^2}{4\pi\epsilon_0 G m_e m_p} \approx 2.3 \times 10^{39}$$

This measures the relative strength of electromagnetism vs. gravity at the atomic scale.
It is an enormous number that appears to have no explanation within either electromagnetism
or General Relativity.

**Ratio 2: Age of universe in atomic units**

$$N_2 = \frac{t_0}{e^2/(4\pi\epsilon_0 m_e c^3)} \approx 6.6 \times 10^{40}$$

The denominator $e^2/(4\pi\epsilon_0 m_e c^3) \approx 10^{-23}$ s is the time for light to
cross the classical electron radius. The ratio $N_2$ counts how many "electron times" have
elapsed since the Big Bang.

**Ratio 3: Observable universe radius in atomic units**

$$N_3 = \frac{R_U}{r_e} \approx 4.2 \times 10^{42}$$

where $R_U \sim ct_0$ is the radius of the observable universe and $r_e = e^2/(4\pi\epsilon_0 m_e c^2)$
is the classical electron radius.

**Ratio 4: Number of particles in the observable universe**

$$N_4 = \frac{M_U}{m_p} \approx 10^{78-80} \approx N_1^2$$

### Dirac's Observation

The key observation is:

$$N_1 \sim N_2 \sim 10^{40}$$

and

$$N_4 \sim N_1^2 \sim 10^{80}$$

These are NOT predicted by any theory. In the Standard Model and General Relativity, $N_1$
is determined by the fundamental constants (which have no predicted values), while $N_2$
depends on the age of the universe (which is contingent on initial conditions). Their
near-equality appears to be a pure coincidence.

### The Hypothesis

Dirac refuses to accept this as coincidence. He proposes:

> "Any two of the very large dimensionless numbers occurring in Nature are connected by a
> simple mathematical relation, in which the coefficients are of the order of magnitude unity."

This is the Large Numbers Hypothesis (LNH).

### The Consequence: $G \propto 1/t$

The LNH has a dramatic consequence. Since $N_2$ depends on $t_0$, which increases as the
universe ages, and $N_1 \sim N_2$ must be maintained, $N_1$ must also increase with time.
Since $e$, $m_e$, $m_p$ are assumed constant in atomic units, the only variable available
is $G$:

$$G \propto \frac{1}{t}$$

The gravitational "constant" must DECREASE as the universe ages. This yields:

$$\frac{\dot{G}}{G} = -\frac{1}{t_0} \approx -7 \times 10^{-11} \text{ yr}^{-1}$$

### Continuous Matter Creation

Maintaining $N_4 \sim N_1^2$ as $N_1$ increases requires $N_4 \propto t^2$. Since the mass
of the universe grows as $t^2$, matter must be continuously created. This connects to (but
predates) the Steady State cosmology of Bondi, Gold, and Hoyle (1948).

## Key Results

1. Dimensionless ratios from fundamental constants cluster around $10^{40}$ and $10^{80}$
2. If this clustering is not coincidental, fundamental "constants" must vary with time
3. Specifically, $G \propto 1/t$ (gravitational constant decreases with cosmic age)
4. $\dot{G}/G \approx -7 \times 10^{-11}$ yr$^{-1}$
5. Continuous matter creation is required ($M_U \propto t^2$)
6. The hypothesis connects microphysics (atomic constants) to cosmology (universe age)
7. This was the FIRST proposal that fundamental constants might vary

## Impact and Legacy

Dirac's 1937 letter is one of the most influential short papers in 20th-century physics.
Its impact includes:

**Theoretical:**
- Inspired Brans-Dicke theory (1961): a scalar-tensor extension of GR with naturally
  varying $G$, parameterized by the Brans-Dicke coupling $\omega$
- Motivated the varying-constants research program (Barrow, Magueijo, Albrecht-Magueijo
  varying speed of light, Bekenstein varying $e$, etc.)
- Anthropic principle (Dicke 1961): argued that $N_1 \sim N_2$ is a SELECTION EFFECT, not a
  dynamical law -- we observe these values because we exist during the main-sequence
  stellar epoch

**Experimental:**
- Lunar Laser Ranging: $|\dot{G}/G| < 10^{-13}$ yr$^{-1}$ (Williams et al. 2004),
  ruling out $G \propto 1/t$ by a factor of ~100
- Binary pulsar timing: independent constraints of comparable precision
- Big Bang nucleosynthesis: constrains $G$ variation between $t \sim 1$ min and today
- Solar evolution models: varying $G$ would change stellar luminosity evolution, constrained
  by geological evidence (Teller 1948)

**Current status:** The LNH is empirically refuted in its original form. However, the
intellectual program it launched -- taking dimensionless ratios seriously, probing the
constancy of "constants," connecting micro- and macro-physics -- remains active and
productive. The anthropic explanation (Dicke) is the dominant modern interpretation of the
large number coincidences.
