## Topic Map

### A. Paasch Core Framework
Papers: 02, 03, 04
The three Paasch papers form a self-contained mass quantization program. Paper 02 derives phi_paasch = 1.53158 from x = e^{-x^2}, organizes particles into six sequences S1-S6 on a logarithmic spiral at 45-degree separation. Paper 03 constructs an exponential model from Planck mass to observable universe, derives proton mass to 6 digits, neutron to 8 digits, discovers integer mass numbers N(j) = 7n and the golden ratio 0.618 in successive M-ratios. Paper 04 derives alpha = 0.007297359 (0.9 ppm from CODATA) from n3=10 and the solution of ln(x) = -x.

### B. Historical Mass Quantization Lineage
Papers: 05, 08, 13, 14, 16, 17, 28
The intellectual ancestry: Nambu (1952) proposed m_n = (n/2)(1/alpha)m_e producing ~70 MeV quanta. Muraki (1978) extended to logarithmic mass formulae with integer quantum numbers -- the direct ancestor Paasch cites. Eddington (1929) and Wyler (1969) attempted alpha derivations from algebraic/geometric structures. MacGregor (2007) compiled alpha-quantized masses and lifetimes. Gsponer-Hurni (1996) extended Barut's N^4 self-energy formula to quarks with generation limits.

### C. Empirical Mass Relations & Algebraic Approaches
Papers: 07, 09, 24, 25, 26, 31, 40, 41, 42
Koide (1983): Q = 2/3 to 0.001% for charged leptons. Zenczykowski (2015): Clifford algebra Cl(6) provides integer grading for mass elements. Furey (2025): division algebras encode SM quantum numbers as Z2^5 superalgebra. Singh (2022): octonionic geometry predicts mass ratios from G2 automorphisms. Palazzi (2003): cube-root stablines at CERN. Giani (2004): comprehensive catalog of 50+ empirical mass formulae. Kosinov (2024): extended Koide with alpha dependence. Luhn (2021): Koide from SU(3) flavor nonet scalar potentials.

### D. Cosmological Scaffolding & Constraints
Papers: 06, 08, 10, 12, 15, 38, 39
Dirac's LNH (1937, 1974): G ~ 1/t. Williams LLR (2004): |dG/G| < 10^{-12} yr^{-1}, ruling out G ~ 1/t by ~100x. Planck 2015: t_0 = 13.799 Gyr, Omega_m = 0.308. Barrow (2005): comprehensive varying-constants review. Jiang (2025): modern LNH reassessment. 2025 LLR+DESI joint constraints.

### E. Dark Energy Observational Tests
Papers: 18, 19, 30, 39
DESI DR1 (2024): 2.6sigma preference for w != -1. DESI DR2 (2025): 3.1sigma dynamical DE with 14M galaxies. Nature Astronomy (2025): robust across parametrizations. Framework predicts w = -1 + O(10^{-29}); DESI is the primary observational challenge.

### F. NCG & Spectral Action Foundations

Van Suijlekom 2nd ed (2024): finite-density spectral action, BCS from geometry, KK-NCG bridge. Dong-Khalkhali-vS (2019): Bessel-function spectral coefficients at finite mu. Chamseddine-Connes-vS (2019): entropy IS a spectral action; zeta-function structure. Hitchin (2025): Dirac operator universality, heat kernel, index theorem.

### G. Froggatt-Nielsen & Flavor Symmetry Mechanisms

## Paper Entries

### Paper 02 -- Paasch (2009): Logarithmic Potential and Exponential Mass Function
- **Citation**: Paasch K., Progress in Physics 1, 36-39 (2009)
- **Content**: Derives quantization factor phi = 1/x where x = e^{-x^2}, yielding phi = 1.53158. Places all elementary particle masses on a logarithmic spiral m(phi) = m_0 * e^{k*phi} with k = (1/2pi)ln(phi). Identifies six principal sequences S1-S6 at 45-degree angular separation. Spiral constant: one turn multiplies mass by phi^{2pi}. All allocations accurate within delta_m/m = 4e-3, angular positions within +/-0.5 degrees. Planck mass falls on S6.
- **Key equations**: (2g) phi = 1.53158; (2j) k = (1/2pi)ln(phi); (2k) m_n = m_0 * e^{k*phi_n}
- **Relevance**: CRITICAL. Foundational paper for the entire mass quantization program.

### Paper 03 -- Paasch (2016): On the Calculation of Elementary Particle Masses
- **Citation**: Paasch K., hal-01368054v3 (2017)
- **Content**: Constructs exponential model spanning Planck mass to observable universe. Introduces generalized equilibrium mass m*(i,j) = sqrt(m_i * m_j). Derives proton mass mp = 1.67262110e-27 kg (measured 1.67262190e-27, delta_m/m = 6e-7). Derives neutron mass mn = 1.67492745e-27 kg (measured 1.67492747e-27, delta_m/m ~ 10^{-8}). Discovers integer mass numbers N(j): N(mu)=35, N(pi)=42, N(K)=98, N(eta)=105, N(rho,omega)=133, N(K*)=145, N(p,n)=150. All masses in range N=35-133 satisfy N=7n. Golden ratio phi=0.618 in successive M-value ratios. Exponential factor fN = 2*phi_golden = 1.23607. Assumes G(t) ~ 1/t (Dirac LNH). Tau mass from mp and me.
- **Key equations**: (3.0) mE = (me*mp)^{1/2}; (5.1) N(j) = m*(e,j)/me; (5.5) fN = 2*phi_golden; (6.8) mp derived; (7.2) mn derived
- **Relevance**: CRITICAL. Source of N(j) integers, golden ratio scaling, proton/neutron mass derivations.

### Paper 04 -- Paasch (2016): Derivation of the Fine Structure Constant
- **Citation**: Paasch K., hal-01375989v3 (2017)
- **Content**: Derives alpha = (1/n3^2)(f/2)^{1/4} = 0.007297359 where n3=10 (from proton mass derivation) and f = 0.5671433 (from ln(f)=-f). Measured: 0.007297353. Relative deviation 8e-7. Result independent of epsilon_0, e, hbar, c.
- **Key equations**: (2.6) ln(f) = -f, f = 0.5671433; (2.9) alpha = (1/100)(f/2)^{1/4}
- **Relevance**: CRITICAL. Alpha from pure number theory. n3 = 10 = dim(3,0) of SU(3).
