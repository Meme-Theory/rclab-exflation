# Dirac's Large Number Hypothesis: An Updated Review

**Author(s):** Jonathan Jiang et al.

**Year:** 2025

**Journal:** Qeios, preprint T7G07S.2

---

## Abstract

Dirac's Large Number Hypothesis (LNH), proposed in 1937, posits that the dimensionless ratios between cosmic-scale constants and atomic-scale constants are not coincidental but reflect fundamental relationships between the macroscopic universe and microscopic quantum realm. Modern observations give ratios like the proton-to-electron mass ratio (1836), the fine structure constant inverse (137), and the ratio of electromagnetic to gravitational force (10^36) — all suspiciously order-unity combinations of other large numbers. This 2025 review examines LNH through the lens of contemporary cosmology, quantum gravity, and holographic principles, explores connections to variable gravitational constants and continuous mass creation scenarios, and assesses the hypothesis's status in light of modern observational constraints. The work concludes that while LNH remains unproven, its structure offers insights into dimensional analysis, fine-tuning, and the anthropic principle.

## Historical Context

**Dirac's Original Proposal** (1937):
Dirac observed that the ratio of the age of the universe (~2×10^10 years ~ 10^18 seconds) to the atomic time scale (Planck time ~10^-44 seconds) is:

$$\frac{t_\text{universe}}{t_\text{Planck}} \sim 10^{40}$$

Simultaneously, the ratio of electromagnetic to gravitational force strengths:

$$\frac{F_e}{F_g} = \frac{e^2}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \cdot \frac{m_\text{Planck}^2}{G m_e m_p} \sim 10^{40}$$

Dirac proposed that these two large numbers being equal (both ~10^40) was **not accidental** but pointed to a deep principle. This coincidence seemed to relate the lifetime of the universe directly to fundamental constants.

**Classical interpretation**: If the large numbers are not independent, then:
- Gravitational constant must evolve: $G(t) = G_0 / t_0$ (Dirac's "varying G")
- Or the universe's geometry couples directly to particle physics constants
- Or a continuous creation process (Fred Hoyle's universe) exists

**20th-century status** (1970s-1990s):
- Variable G theories (Brans-Dicke) were tested and largely excluded by solar system constraints
- The LNH was considered speculative or numerologically interesting but not scientifically central
- Focus shifted to anthropic principle and fine-tuning as alternative explanations

**Modern revival** (2000s-2020s):
- Holographic principle (AdS/CFT) rekindled interest in cosmic-scale/atomic-scale duality
- Modified gravity theories (MOND, f(R) gravity) revisited variable-constant scenarios
- Quantum cosmology (Wheeler-DeWitt) suggested the universe's age is fundamentally related to microscopic physics
- Fine-tuning problems in standard ΛCDM made the search for unifying principles urgent

## Key Arguments and Derivations

### The Large Number Coincidences

Dirac identified several dimensionless ratios:

**1. Cosmological ratio**:
$$\alpha_c = \frac{t_0}{t_\text{Planck}} \sim 10^{40}$$

where $t_0 \sim 10^{10}$ years is the cosmic age, $t_\text{Planck} = \sqrt{\hbar G / c^5} \sim 10^{-43}$ s.

**2. Gravitational strength**:
$$\alpha_g = \frac{e^2}{4\pi\epsilon_0 G m_e m_p} \sim 2.3 \times 10^{39}$$

**3. Proton-to-electron mass ratio**:
$$\alpha_m = \frac{m_p}{m_e} \sim 1836 \sim 10^{3.3}$$

(Not a "large number" in Dirac's original sense, but related via $\alpha_c^{1/2} \sim \alpha_m$)

**4. Fine structure constant**:
$$\alpha_\text{EM} = \frac{e^2}{4\pi\epsilon_0 \hbar c} \sim 1/137$$

The observation that these appear in combinations—$\alpha_c / \alpha_m^2 \sim 10^{30}$, etc.—suggested a hidden organizing principle.

### Variable Gravitational Constant

If LNH implies that the "large numbers" are dynamical, then:

$$G(t) = \frac{G_0}{t_0 / t}$$

where $t_0$ is the cosmic age at present time. Taking the time derivative:

$$\dot{G} = -\frac{G}{t}$$

or in natural units:

$$\frac{\dot{G}}{G} = -\frac{1}{t_0}$$

This yields a present-day rate of change:

$$\left| \frac{\dot{G}}{G} \right|_0 \sim 10^{-11} \text{ year}^{-1} \sim 3 \times 10^{-20} \text{ s}^{-1}$$

**Observational tests**:
- Lunar laser ranging (Williams et al., 2004, 2012): $|\dot{G}/G| < 4 \times 10^{-14}$ year^{-1} (95% CL)
- Pulsar timing: $|\dot{G}/G| < 10^{-12}$ year^{-1}
- Big Bang nucleosynthesis: constraints on G at early universe
- **Verdict**: Dirac's simple $G \propto 1/t$ is excluded at > 99.9% CL

### Continuous Mass Creation

An alternative interpretation: the universe's expansion is not empty space expansion but the continuous creation of matter. Steady-state cosmology (Hoyle, 1948; Bondi, Gold, 1948) proposed:

$$\rho(t) = \text{constant}$$

as the universe expands. This requires a creation rate:

$$\dot{n} = 3H n$$

where n is number density, H is Hubble rate. The mass creation rate per unit volume is:

$$\dot{\rho}_\text{creation} = m_p \dot{n} = 3 H \rho$$

**Consistency with LNH**: If the total energy of created matter equals the kinetic energy of recession, a coincidence emerges:

$$\int_0^{t_0} \dot{E}_\text{creation} dt \sim (H_0 t_0)^{-1} \times E_\text{total}$$

The numbers align if $H_0 t_0 \sim O(1)$, which is observed (currently $H_0 t_0 \sim 1.4$).

**Modern status**: Steady-state cosmology is ruled out by CBR isotropy and Big Bang nucleosynthesis measurements. However, continuous creation in an expanding ΛCDM framework (via quantum field theory in curved spacetime) remains viable, motivated by particle creation near event horizons (Hawking evaporation analog).

### Holographic Principle Interpretation

The holographic principle (Juan Maldacena, 1997; AdS/CFT correspondence) naturally relates macroscopic and microscopic scales. In AdS/CFT:

$$\text{Bulk gravity physics at scale } L \leftrightarrow \text{Boundary CFT at scale } L / g_s$$

where $g_s$ is the string coupling. If the cosmological horizon (at distance $c / H_0 \sim 10^{26}$ m) is the "boundary" of our universe, then:

$$\text{Cosmic scale} \sim \text{Atomic scale} \times \text{(holographic ratio)}$$

This provides a modern realization of Dirac's intuition: large and small scales are not independent but related by the holographic geometry of spacetime.

**LNH as holographic**: The product of cosmic and atomic scales:

$$\ell_\text{Planck} \times c / H_0 \sim \ell_\text{Compton}$$

where $\ell_\text{Compton} = \hbar / (m_e c)$ is the electron Compton wavelength. Rearranging:

$$\frac{c / H_0}{\ell_\text{Planck}} \sim \frac{\ell_\text{Compton}}{\ell_\text{Planck}} \sim 10^{20}$$

This can be reinterpreted as a holographic surface area relationship: the observable universe contains ~10^120 Planck-area pixels, consistent with the holographic bound.

## Key Results

1. **Dirac's Original Large Numbers**:
   - $\alpha_c = t_0 / t_\text{Planck} \approx 1.3 \times 10^{40}$
   - $\alpha_g = F_e / F_g \approx 2.3 \times 10^{39}$
   - **Ratio**: $\alpha_c / \alpha_g \approx 0.6$, suggesting possible unification

2. **Variable G Constraints**: All variable-G theories with Dirac's timescale are excluded at > 99% CL by lunar laser ranging (Paper 10, Williams et al.).

3. **Fine-Tuning Severity**:
   - The cosmological constant is fine-tuned to ~1 part in 10^120
   - The weak scale is fine-tuned relative to Planck scale (hierarchy problem)
   - These are independent fine-tunings, not explained by LNH alone

4. **Anthropic Reasoning**:
   - IF the universe is a multiverse with varying constants, the observed large numbers are unsurprising (we observe ourselves in a universe compatible with structure formation)
   - This is a anthropic solution, not a dynamical explanation
   - Validity depends on existence of multiverse (not yet observationally supported)

5. **Holographic Interpretation**:
   - Modern AdS/CFT provides a framework where cosmic and atomic scales are related through gauge/gravity duality
   - The LNH large numbers can be reinterpreted as holographic entropy bounds
   - Suggests LNH is a manifestation of quantum gravity, not classical cosmology

## Impact and Legacy

Dirac's 1937 hypothesis, while not explicitly proven, catalyzed decades of research into:
- Alternative theories of gravity
- Fine-tuning and anthropic principles
- Quantum cosmology and Wheeler-DeWitt equation
- Modern holographic duality

The hypothesis has evolved from a numerological curiosity into a window on quantum gravity. While varying-G theories are excluded, the underlying intuition—that cosmic and atomic scales are connected—resonates in contemporary holographic frameworks.
