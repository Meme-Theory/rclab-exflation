# A Superalgebra Within: Representations of Lightest SM Particles Form a Z_2^5-Graded Algebra

**Author(s):** Nichol Furey
**Year:** 2025
**Journal:** Annalen der Physik, 2500229
**arXiv:** 2505.07923

---

## Abstract

The representations of the lightest Standard Model particles—gauge bosons and three generations of fermions (excluding the top quark)—collectively form a superalgebra. This superalgebra is isomorphic to the Euclidean Jordan algebra of 16×16 hermitian matrices, H₁₆(ℂ), and is generated by normed division algebras (ℝ, ℂ, ℍ, 𝕆). The algebra admits a natural five-fold ℤ₂^5 grading, with internal symmetries yielding the Standard Model gauge group su(3)_C ⊕ su(2)_L ⊕ u(1)_Y plus four additional u(1) factors. Unlike supersymmetric approaches, this framework requires no doubling of particles.

---

## Historical Context

Furey's program extends a two-decade investigation into whether division algebras provide the foundational algebraic structure underlying particle physics. The key insight is that all four normed division algebras over the reals (ℝ, ℂ, ℍ, 𝕆) possess finite dimension and occur naturally in physics through Clifford algebras and representation theory. Earlier work (2014-2023) demonstrated that SU(3)_C ⊗ SU(2)_L ⊗ U(1)_Y emerges from the triality symmetries of tensor products C ⊗ ℍ ⊗ 𝕆. The 2025 paper refines this: rather than deriving the SM gauge group *from* division algebras, Furey shows that SM particle representations *themselves* form a superalgebra with explicit division-algebraic generators.

This work connects to a parallel discovery in NCG (noncommutative geometry) and KK theories: spectral triples on M₄ × S¹ × SU(3) yield the same quantum numbers through Dirac operator eigenspaces. Both approaches—algebraic and spectral—reach the same particle content without assuming supersymmetry.

## Key Arguments and Derivations

### The Jordan Algebra Framework

A Jordan algebra is a nonassociative algebra with product $a \circ b$ satisfying:
- Commutativity: $a \circ b = b \circ a$
- Jordan identity: $(a \circ (b \circ a)) \circ a = a \circ ((b \circ a) \circ a)$

The space of 16×16 hermitian matrices, H₁₆(ℂ), forms a Jordan algebra under $A \circ B = \frac{1}{2}(AB + BA)$.

**Theorem (Furey 2025):** The direct sum of representation spaces for:
- Gauge bosons: 𝔰𝔲(3)_C ⊕ 𝔰𝔲(2)_L ⊕ 𝔲(1)_Y (adjoint + singlets)
- Leptons: (ν_e, e)_L, e_R, (ν_μ, μ)_L, μ_R, (ν_τ, τ)_L, τ_R
- Quarks (u,d)_L, u_R, d_R; (c,s)_L, c_R, s_R; (t,b)_L, b_R [top excluded]

is isomorphic to H₁₆(ℂ) viewed as a superalgebra over ℤ₂^5.

### Division Algebraic Generation

Let $e_ℝ, e_ℂ, e_ℍ, e_𝕆$ denote identity elements of ℝ, ℂ, ℍ, 𝕆 respectively. The tensor product

$$\Psi = \Psi_+ ⊗ e_ℝ + \Psi_- ⊗ e_ℂ$$

where $\Psi_±$ carry left and right Weyl spinor representations, generates fermion spaces. Quaternionic and octonionic indices label internal quantum numbers (color, flavor).

The grading $\mathbb{Z}_2^5$ arises from:
1. Lorentz spinor chirality (L/R): $\mathbb{Z}_2$
2. Lepton number mod 2: $\mathbb{Z}_2$
3. Three color charges mod 2 each (SU(3) → ℤ₂³): three factors $\mathbb{Z}_2$

Total: $\mathbb{Z}_2^5 = \mathbb{Z}_2 × \mathbb{Z}_2 × \mathbb{Z}_2 × \mathbb{Z}_2 × \mathbb{Z}_2$.

### Gauge Symmetry Closure

Under the action of su(3) ⊕ su(2) ⊕ u(1), the representation spaces decompose into irreducible multiplets. Furey verifies that all SM multiplets fit within H₁₆(ℂ) with no overcounting and no missing states (except the top quark, which requires additional structure).

The four extra u(1) factors arise as:
- u(1)_Y (hypercharge)
- u(1)_B (baryon number)
- u(1)_L (lepton number)
- u(1)_em (electromagnetic, combination of Y and isospin)

Each is preserved by the division algebraic structure separately, providing internal consistency.

## Key Results

1. **Superalgebra Isomorphism**: SM particle representations collectively form H₁₆(ℂ), a 256-dimensional complex Jordan algebra, with explicit ℤ₂^5 grading structure.

2. **No Supersymmetry Required**: Unlike SUSY models, this framework requires no scalar superpartners or gaugino doublets. Particles are fundamental elements of a graded algebra, not related by fermionic symmetries.

3. **Division Algebra Factorization**: The SM emerges from tensor products of division algebras without matrix enlargement—representations are direct elements of the algebras themselves.

4. **Top Quark Anomaly**: The lightest three generations fit naturally; the top quark requires independent explanation, consistent with its unusual mass scale.

5. **Quantum Number Preservation**: All baryon and lepton numbers, color, isospin, and hypercharge emerge as conserved quantum numbers from the ℤ₂^5 grading.

## Impact and Legacy

This work bridges algebraic and spectral approaches to SM derivation. It suggests that division algebras may be *fundamental* rather than emergent, motivating:
- Pure algebraic approaches to beyond-SM physics (supersymmetry or extra generations would require larger algebras)
- Unification with quantum computing (Jordan algebra structure connects to qubit evolution)
- Reconciliation with NCG approaches (Connes, Chamseddine, van Suijlekom)

The paper has been cited in discussions of algebraic foundations of particle physics and appears in the context of broader programs seeking geometric principles underlying the SM.
