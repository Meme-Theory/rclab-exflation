# Observation of Antiprotons (1955)

**Authors**: Owen Chamberlain, Emilio Segrè, Clyde Wiegand, Thomas Ypsilantis
**Published**: Physical Review 100, 947-950 (1955)
**Nobel Prize**: 1959 (Chamberlain and Segrè)

## Abstract

Using the Bevatron accelerator at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, Chamberlain and Segrè observe the antiproton — the antimatter counterpart of the proton — confirming that Dirac's particle-antiparticle symmetry extends beyond the electron to baryons.

## Historical Context

After Anderson's positron discovery (1932), it was natural to ask whether ALL particles have antiparticles. For the proton, producing an antiproton required:

    p + p → p + p + p + p̄

The threshold energy for this reaction in a fixed-target experiment is:

    E_threshold = 7 × m_p c² ≈ 6.6 GeV

(The factor of 7, rather than 4, comes from conservation of both energy AND momentum in the lab frame.)

No accelerator before the Bevatron could reach this energy.

## The Bevatron

- **Location**: Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, California
- **Type**: Weak-focusing proton synchrotron
- **Maximum energy**: 6.2 GeV (just above threshold)
- **First beam**: 1954
- **Design motivation**: Explicitly built to be powerful enough to produce antiprotons

## Detection Strategy

### The Challenge
At 6 GeV, many particles are produced: pions, kaons, protons, etc. Antiprotons constitute only a tiny fraction (~1 in 40,000 negative particles). The experiment needed to:
1. Select negatively charged particles (antiproton has charge -e)
2. Measure their mass to distinguish from π⁻ and K⁻
3. Mass measurement requires TWO independent measurements: momentum (from magnetic deflection) and velocity (from time-of-flight or Cherenkov radiation)

### Apparatus
1. **Magnetic spectrometer**: Selected particles with momentum p = 1.19 GeV/c
2. **Scintillation counters**: Two counters separated by 12 meters measured time-of-flight
   - π⁻ at 1.19 GeV/c: v ≈ 0.99c → t ≈ 40 ns
   - p̄ at 1.19 GeV/c: v ≈ 0.78c → t ≈ 51 ns
   - Clear separation!
3. **Cherenkov counter**: Threshold Cherenkov detector set to respond to particles with v > 0.79c
   - Pions: above threshold (detected)
   - Antiprotons: below threshold (NOT detected → selection by absence)

### Signal
Antiproton candidates = negative particles that:
- Have the correct time-of-flight for mass ≈ 938 MeV/c²
- Do NOT trigger the Cherenkov counter
- Are deflected by the correct angle in the magnetic spectrometer

## Results

- **60 antiproton events** detected in the initial run
- Measured mass: consistent with proton mass (938.3 MeV/c²)
- Charge: -e (opposite to proton)
- Cross-section for p̄ production: consistent with theoretical estimates

## Modern Antiproton Sources

### CERN Antiproton Decelerator (AD)
- 26 GeV protons from PS hit iridium target
- Antiprotons collected at 3.57 GeV/c
- Decelerated to 100 MeV/c
- Further decelerated by ELENA ring to 100 keV
- Feeds experiments: ALPHA, BASE, AEgIS, GBAR, ASACUSA

### Production Rate
- ~3 × 10⁷ antiprotons per minute at AD
- Improved to ~5 × 10⁷ with ELENA
- Total accumulated: trillions of antiprotons over AD lifetime
