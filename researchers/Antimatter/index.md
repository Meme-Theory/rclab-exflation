## Topic Map

### A. Dirac Equation and Antimatter Prediction (1928-1932)
Papers: 01, 02, 03, 04
The Dirac equation (01), its four-component spinor structure, and the negative-energy solutions
that led through the hole theory (02) to the prediction and discovery (04) of the positron.
Paper 03 extends to magnetic monopoles and charge quantization. These papers establish the
algebraic origin of antimatter: {gamma^mu, gamma^nu} = 2g^{mu,nu} forces 4 components,
which forces antiparticles.

### B. CPT Theorem and Baryon Asymmetry
Papers: 05, 06
The Luders-Pauli-Jost CPT theorem (05) proves CPT invariance for any local Lorentz-invariant
QFT. Sakharov (06) identifies three necessary conditions for baryogenesis: B-violation,
C and CP violation, and departure from thermal equilibrium. Together they frame the central
tension: CPT guarantees equal particle-antiparticle masses, yet the universe is matter-dominated.

### C. Experimental Discovery and Production
Papers: 04, 07
Anderson's positron (04) and Chamberlain-Segre's antiproton (07) confirmed Dirac's prediction
for leptons and baryons respectively. The antiproton required the Bevatron at 6.2 GeV.

### D. Precision CPT Tests (Penning Traps and Spectroscopy)
Papers: 08, 09, 17, 23, 27
Penning trap techniques (08) achieve q/m to 16 ppt and magnetic moment to 1.5 ppb (BASE).
ALPHA spectroscopy (09) tests 1S-2S to 2 ppt. Hyperfine components resolved in 2024 (17).
BASE-STEP (23) demonstrates first antiproton transport. AD/ELENA review (27) consolidates
the program.

### E. Antimatter Gravity
Papers: 10, 32, 27
ALPHA-g (10) measures a_g/g = 0.75 +/- 0.29, ruling out antigravity at >99.97% CL.
Villata (32) analyzes implications for CPT gravity theories. The AD/ELENA review (27)
covers AEgIS, GBAR, and future precision targets.

### F. Positronium and Interferometry
Papers: 11
AEgIS (11) produces positronium, achieves first laser cooling of Ps (2024), and targets
antimatter gravity via moire deflectometry. Positronium BEC is a long-term goal.

### G. NCG Real Structure and Charge Conjugation
Papers: 12, 28, 29, 30, 31
The spectral action principle (28) unifies SM + gravity. The J operator (12) encodes
charge conjugation algebraically via the KO-dimension 6 conditions J^2=+1, JD=DJ,
J*gamma=-gamma*J. Boyle-Farnsworth (29) introduce the super-algebra formulation.
Venselaar (30) classifies real structures on almost-commutative triples. Filaci-Landi
(31) generalize to twisted real structures where J a J^{-1} = sigma(a).

### H. Fermion Integrals, Doubling, and One-Loop
Papers: 19, 22, 33
Bochniak-Sitarz (22) solve fermion doubling via KO-dim regrading. Their fermion integrals
paper (19) provides explicit Pfaffian formulas for each KO-dim. Van Suijlekom (33) proves
one-loop renormalizability of the spectral action while preserving geometric structure.

### I. BDI Topological Classification
Papers: 15, 16, 25
Schnyder et al. (15) establish the tenfold periodic table in 3D. Ryu et al. (16) prove
the eight-fold Bott periodicity and dimensional hierarchy. Zirnbauer (25) provides the

with T^2=+1, C^2=+1 is the framework's symmetry class.

### J. CPT Violation Searches and SME

Kostelecky-Russell (18) compile all Lorentz and CPT violation limits across all sectors.
Roberts (26) reviews neutral meson CPT tests. Both confirm CPT to 10^{-10} to 10^{-25} GeV.

### K. CP Violation and Pati-Salam

### L. Entropy and Spectral Action
