## Abstract

The LHCb Collaboration reports the first observation of charge-parity (CP) violation in baryon decays, a major milestone achieved over six decades after the initial CP violation discovery in the meson sector (1964). Using proton-proton collision data at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV corresponding to an integrated luminosity of 9 fb^‚àí1, the collaboration analyzes the three-body baryon decay Œõ_b^0 ‚Üí pK^‚àíœÄ^+œÄ^‚àí. These measurements open new research avenues for understanding strong interaction dynamics in baryonic systems and constrain CP-violating interactions at the quark level.

## Historical Context

CP violation has shaped our understanding of the matter-antimatter asymmetry of the universe for six decades. The first observation came from Christenson, Cronin, Fitch, and Turlay in 1964 when they measured CP violation in neutral K meson decays‚Äîa discovery that earned Cronin and Fitch the 1980 Nobel Prize. Since then, CP violation has been extensively studied in the meson sector, particularly in kaons, D mesons, and B mesons, providing stringent tests of the Standard Model's Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix.

The baryon sector, by contrast, has remained relatively unexplored from the CP violation perspective. Baryon decays involve three valence quarks rather than the quark-antiquark structure of mesons, introducing fundamentally different decay dynamics. Theoretical predictions by Hiller, Schmeling, and others in the 2010s suggested that CP asymmetries in baryon decays could reach the percent level, but experimental challenges‚Äîthe complexity of three-body baryon decays, smaller production cross sections, and intricate interference patterns‚Äîdelayed observation.

The LHCb experiment, with its forward-detector geometry and specialized trigger system optimized for rare decays, uniquely positioned itself to observe this effect. The measurement represents not just a discovery but a validation of theoretical frameworks predicting asymmetries beyond the Standard Model in the baryon sector. It also provides complementary constraints to B meson and K meson CP measurements, probing CP violation in regions of parameter space inaccessible to other systems.

## Key Arguments and Derivations

### Three-Body Decay Kinematics and Amplitude Decomposition

The decay Œõ_b^0 ‚Üí pK^‚àíœÄ^+œÄ^‚àí is a multi-body final state that requires careful kinematic reconstruction. The amplitude can be decomposed using Dalitz plot analysis, parametrizing the m^2_{pK‚àí} and m^2_{œÄ+œÄ‚àí} invariant masses. The full amplitude is a coherent superposition of intermediate resonances:

$$ M(Œõ_b^0 ‚Üí pK^‚àíœÄ^+œÄ^‚àí) = \sum_i A_i e^{iŒ¥_i} $$

where A_i are the individual resonance amplitudes (e.g., K^*_0(1430), K_2^*(1430), ...) and Œ¥_i are strong-interaction phases.

The CP-conjugate process ŒõÃÑ_b^0 ‚Üí pÃÑK^+œÄ^‚àíœÄ^+ has amplitude:

$$ M(ŒõÃÑ_b^0 ‚Üí pÃÑK^+œÄ^‚àíœÄ^+) = \sum_i A_i^* e^{-iŒ¥_i} $$

The key insight: interference between amplitudes with different weak phases (from CKM elements) and strong phases (from final-state interactions) produces a non-zero CP asymmetry.

### Direct CP Asymmetry Measurement

The local CP asymmetry in an invariant-mass region is defined as:

$$ A_{CP}(m) = \frac{dN(Œõ_b^0)/dm - dN(ŒõÃÑ_b^0)/dm}{dN(Œõ_b^0)/dm + dN(ŒõÃÑ_b^0)/dm} $$

For the global measurement integrating over the full phase space:

$$ A_{CP}^{global} = \frac{N(Œõ_b^0) - N(ŒõÃÑ_b^0)}{N(Œõ_b^0) + N(ŒõÃÑ_b^0)} $$

LHCb measures:
- **Global**: A_{CP}^{global} = (2.45 ¬± 0.46 ¬± 0.10)%, with significance 5.2œÉ
- **Local (low-mass region)**: A_{CP}^{local} = (5.4 ¬± 0.9 ¬± 0.1)%, with significance 6.0œÉ

### Comparison with Theoretical Predictions

N-pion rescattering models predict baryon CP asymmetries of 5.6‚Äì5.9% in certain regions. The local measurement (5.4%) aligns remarkably well with these predictions, suggesting that final-state interactions involving pion pairs dominate the CP-violating signal. This differs fundamentally from meson decays, where color-suppressed amplitudes often control CP violation.

The theoretical framework employs QCD factorization for baryon decays:

$$ M ~ (V_{cb} or V_{ub}) √ó [hard-scattering kernel] √ó [meson form factors] √ó [baryon form factors] $$

Baryon form factors introduce additional complexity compared to meson decays, with transitions from Œõ_b to three-body final states receiving contributions from both factorizable and non-factorizable (penguin-like) diagrams.

### Systematic Uncertainties

The total systematic uncertainty of 0.10% combines:
- Detector efficiency variations across the Dalitz plot
- Kaon and proton PID asymmetries
- Trigger efficiency asymmetries
- Background contamination asymmetries
- Final-state interaction phase variations

LHCb achieved 100√ó improvement in PID systematic uncertainties by exploiting the RICH detector's momentum-dependent response functions.

## Impact and Legacy

This discovery opens the baryon sector as a new frontier for CP violation studies. Unlike meson CP measurements, which have been refined to sub-percent precisions and constrain CKM elements with high precision, baryon CP asymmetries access new parameter space in beyond-Standard-Model physics searches. Future measurements at the LHC and at next-generation facilities (CERN's High-Luminosity LHC, Belle II upgrades) will map CP asymmetries across the full baryon spectrum‚Äîincluding Œõ_c, Œû_b, and Œ©_b decays‚Äîconstraining right-handed gauge bosons, leptoquarks, and other exotica.

The result validates precision QCD calculations of baryon final-state interactions, critical for extracting weak phases from three-body decays. This has implications for rare decays like Œõ_b ‚Üí pœÄ^‚àíœÄ^+ (which are theoretically cleaner and measure |V_{ub}|) and semi-leptonic transitions like Œõ_b ‚Üí Œõ_c ‚Ñì ŒΩ.
