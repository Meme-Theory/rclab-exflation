# The ALPHA Experiment: Antihydrogen Trapping and Spectroscopy

**Collaboration**: ALPHA (Antihydrogen Laser Physics Apparatus)
**Spokesperson**: Jeffrey Hangst (Aarhus University)
**Location**: CERN Antiproton Decelerator / ELENA
**Active**: 2005–present

## Overview

The ALPHA experiment produces, traps, and performs precision spectroscopy on antihydrogen atoms — the simplest neutral antimatter atom (a positron bound to an antiproton). Its measurements provide the most precise tests of CPT symmetry in atomic physics and the first direct measurements of antimatter's gravitational behavior.

## Antihydrogen Production

### The Recipe
1. **Antiprotons**: From CERN's AD/ELENA, decelerated to ~5.3 MeV, further cooled in ALPHA's catching trap
2. **Positrons**: From a ²²Na radioactive source, accumulated and cooled in a Surko-type buffer gas accumulator (~10⁷ positrons per cycle)
3. **Mixing**: Antiprotons and positrons combined in a nested Penning trap at ~100 mK
4. **Formation**: Three-body recombination: e⁺ + e⁺ + p̄ → H̄ + e⁺

### Production Numbers
- 2023-24 campaign: Over **2 million** antihydrogen atoms produced
- Trapping efficiency: ~1 in 10,000 atoms cold enough to trap
- Typical trapped sample: ~100-1000 atoms per cycle

## The Magnetic Trap (ALPHA-2)

Antihydrogen is electrically neutral — Penning traps won't work. ALPHA uses a **magnetic minimum trap** (Ioffe-Pritchard type):

### Components
- **Octupole magnet**: Provides radial confinement via field gradient
- **Mirror coils**: Axial confinement at trap ends
- **Trap depth**: ~0.5 Kelvin (54 μeV) — only the coldest atoms are confined

### Trapping Mechanism
The antihydrogen atom has a magnetic moment. In its ground state:
- **Low-field seekers**: Atoms with magnetic moment aligned AGAINST the field are pushed toward the field minimum (center) — TRAPPED
- **High-field seekers**: Pushed toward the walls — LOST

Only atoms with kinetic energy < 0.5 K are confined.

## Spectroscopy Achievements

### 1S-2S Transition (2017-2024)
The 1S-2S two-photon transition is the gold standard of atomic hydrogen spectroscopy:
- In hydrogen: 2,466,061,413,187,035(10) Hz
- ALPHA measurement on antihydrogen: consistent at **2 parts per trillion**
- This is the most precise CPT test in any atomic system

**Method**: Two counter-propagating 243 nm UV laser beams illuminate trapped antihydrogen. Atoms that absorb the light are heated and escape the trap — detected by annihilation on the trap walls.

### Lyman-Alpha (1S-2P) Transition (2018)
- First observation of the Lyman-alpha transition in antihydrogen
- Frequency: 2,466,051.7 ± 0.12 GHz at B = 1.033 T
- Consistent with hydrogen prediction to **5 × 10⁻⁸**
- Opens the door to laser cooling of antihydrogen

### Hyperfine Structure (2024)
- First measurement of the 1S-2S hyperfine components in antihydrogen
- Resolves the spin-dependent structure of the transition
- Provides additional constraints on CPT violation

### Lamb Shift Analog
ALPHA has measured the fine structure of the 2S and 2P levels, approaching sensitivity to the Lamb shift — a QED radiative correction that was historically one of the key tests of quantum electrodynamics.

## Laser Cooling of Antihydrogen (2021)

ALPHA achieved the **first laser cooling of antimatter** using the Lyman-alpha transition:
- Technique: 121.6 nm pulsed laser excites 1S → 2P transition
- Spontaneous emission back to 1S provides the cooling force
- Temperature reduction: factor of ~10 (from ~50 K to ~5 K)
- This dramatically increases the number of trapped atoms and measurement precision

Hangst has driven the ALPHA program from first production of cold antihydrogen (ATHENA, 2002) through trapping (2010), spectroscopy (2017), laser cooling (2021), and gravity measurement (2023). ### Methodology
- Patient, systematic approach: decades of incremental technical improvements
- Key innovations: mixing protocols, trap geometry, laser systems
- Emphasis on eliminating systematic errors before claiming results
- Strong commitment to reproducibility and open data

## Connection to the Framework

### 1. Spectral Test of NCG
The 1S-2S antihydrogen measurement is the most precise test of CPT in atoms. In the NCG framework:
- The real structure J guarantees m(H) = m(H̄) and identical energy levels
- Any discrepancy would signal that J's defining properties (J² = 1, JD = DJ, Jγ = -γJ for KO-dim 6) are violated
- Current precision (2 ppt) constrains energy-dependent CPT violation at the 10⁻²⁰ GeV level

### 2. QED in Internal Space
The hydrogen energy levels involve QED corrections (Lamb shift, hyperfine structure). In the phonon-exflation framework, these corrections arise from the spectral action on M⁴ × K:
- Tree-level: D_M ⊗ 1 gives the Dirac hydrogen spectrum
- 1-loop: Contributions from D_F modify the effective coupling
- The agreement between H and H̄ spectroscopy validates the symmetry of the spectral action under J

### 3. Antimatter Production as Phase Transition Analog
The process p̄ + e⁺ → H̄ (antihydrogen formation) is a recombination process analogous to recombination in the early universe. In the phonon framework, recombination corresponds to bound state formation in the condensate — relevant to the D/H ratio calculation.
