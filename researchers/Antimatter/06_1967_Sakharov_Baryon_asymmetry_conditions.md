# Violation of CP Invariance, C Asymmetry, and Baryon Asymmetry of the Universe (1967)

**Author**: Andrei Dmitrievich Sakharov
**Published**: JETP Letters 5, 24-27 (1967)
**Original**: Pis'ma Zh. Eksp. Teor. Fiz. 5, 32-35 (1967)

## Abstract

Sakharov identifies three necessary conditions for generating the observed matter-antimatter asymmetry of the universe from initially symmetric conditions. These "Sakharov conditions" remain the foundation of all baryogenesis theories.

## The Problem: Where Is the Antimatter?

### Observational Facts
The observable universe is overwhelmingly composed of matter:
- No significant antimatter regions detected (annihilation gamma rays would be visible)
- Cosmic rays are ~99.99% matter
- The baryon-to-photon ratio η = n_B/n_γ ≈ 6.1 × 10⁻¹⁰ (measured from CMB + BBN)

### The Quantitative Asymmetry
In the early universe at T >> 1 GeV:
- Matter and antimatter exist in nearly equal amounts
- For every ~10⁹ antiquarks, there were ~10⁹ + 1 quarks
- After annihilation, the tiny excess (1 part in 10⁹) became ALL the matter we observe
- This is parametrized as: η_B = (n_B - n_B̄)/n_γ ≈ 6 × 10⁻¹⁰

## The Three Sakharov Conditions

### Condition 1: Baryon Number Violation

There must exist processes that change total baryon number. Without B-violation, a universe starting with B = 0 must remain at B = 0 forever.

**Status in the Standard Model**:
- Classical: Baryon number is conserved (no tree-level B-violating processes)
- Quantum: 't Hooft (1976) showed B is violated by **sphalerons** — non-perturbative electroweak processes mediated by instantons
- Sphaleron rate: suppressed at T < T_EW (~160 GeV) by factor exp(-E_sph/T), but unsuppressed at T > T_EW
- Sphalerons conserve B - L but violate B + L

**Beyond the Standard Model**:
- GUT theories (SU(5), SO(10)): Heavy leptoquark bosons mediate B-violating decays
- SUSY: Additional B-violating channels

### Condition 2: C and CP Violation

The laws of physics must distinguish matter from antimatter. If C and CP are exact symmetries, then any process creating baryons would be exactly matched by a process creating antibaryons.

**Status in the Standard Model**:
- C violation: Maximal in weak interactions (left-handed neutrinos only)
- CP violation: Observed in K meson (1964), B meson (2001), D meson (2019) systems
- Parametrized by the CKM phase δ ≈ 1.2 radians
- **Problem**: SM CP violation is TOO SMALL by ~10 orders of magnitude to explain observed η_B

**Beyond the Standard Model**:
- Additional CP phases in SUSY, extra Higgs doublets, leptogenesis (PMNS phases)
- Strong CP problem: θ_QCD ≈ 0 (unnaturally small; axion hypothesis)

### Condition 3: Departure from Thermal Equilibrium

In thermal equilibrium, CPT guarantees that matter and antimatter have equal abundances (same mass → same Boltzmann factor). Asymmetry requires OUT-OF-EQUILIBRIUM dynamics.

**Mechanisms**:
- **First-order phase transition**: Electroweak phase transition at T ~ 160 GeV. SM predicts crossover (too smooth); BSM models can make it first-order.
- **Heavy particle decay**: GUT-scale bosons (M ~ 10¹⁶ GeV) decay out of equilibrium when Γ < H (decay rate < Hubble rate)
- **Leptogenesis**: Heavy right-handed neutrinos decay CP-asymmetrically; sphalerons convert lepton asymmetry to baryon asymmetry (Fukugita-Yanagida, 1986)

## Open Questions

1. **The source of CP violation**: Is it the CKM matrix, PMNS matrix, or new physics?
2. **The nature of the EW phase transition**: First-order (BSM) or crossover (SM)?
3. **The scale of baryogenesis**: GUT scale (~10¹⁶ GeV), EW scale (~10² GeV), or intermediate?
4. **Connection to dark matter**: Do dark matter and baryon asymmetry share a common origin?
