# Extra Dimensions: 2024 Review

**Author(s):** Particle Data Group Collaboration
**Year:** 2024
**Source:** Particle Data Group (PDG) 2024 Review of Particle Physics, Section on Extra Dimensions

---

## Abstract

The 2024 Particle Data Group review on extra dimensions compiles the latest experimental constraints on theories that posit spatial dimensions beyond the three observed in everyday experience. These include universal extra dimensions (UED), large extra dimensions (ADD), warped extra dimensions (Randall-Sundrum), and their KK tower signatures. The review integrates bounds from the LHC (ATLAS, CMS, 13 TeV, up to 2024), precision electroweak measurements, flavor physics, astrophysical probes (neutron stars, white dwarfs), precision gravity tests (collider, table-top, space missions), and high-energy cosmic rays. The tightest bounds place KK graviton masses above ~2-5 TeV (depending on scenario), exclude universal extra dimensions below 1 TeV scale, and constrain TeV-scale warped geometries to coupling constants inconsistent with naturalness arguments.

---

## Historical Context

After 1998 (ADD model) and 1999 (Randall-Sundrum), extra-dimensional models became mainstream attempts to address the hierarchy problem and unify gravity with the standard model. The original motivation was naturalness: if electroweak symmetry breaking is protected by a fundamental scale at the weak scale or TeV scale, why is gravity so much weaker (Planck scale 10^16 TeV)? Extra dimensions offer geometric solutions: perhaps gravity is strong in higher dimensions but diluted in four dimensions by compactification volume or warping.

By 2024, the LHC has performed nearly 15 years of Kaluza-Klein searches. The fact that NO KK resonances have been discovered, despite design sensitivity to masses below 5 TeV in many models, is a major constraint. The Sagan empiricist asks: if the framework requires a large KK mass scale (~10^17 GeV as phonon-exflation predicts), how does this compare to naturalness? The PDG review is the community's answer: naturalness arguments prefer M_KK < 5 TeV, but bounds do NOT forbid ultra-high scales.

The review documents a critical gap: naturalness and the hierarchy problem do NOT uniquely determine M_KK. Different theoretical assumptions yield different preferred scales. This makes the problem amenable to test but not yet solved.

## Key Arguments and Derivations

### Universal Extra Dimensions (UED)

In UED, the SM is embedded in higher-dimensional spacetime with compactified extra dimensions (radius R or geometry). KK modes are towers of increasingly massive replicas of SM particles, spaced roughly by 1/R.

**Bound**: From precision electroweak fits and flavor-changing neutral currents (FCNCs), the minimal UED model with one extra dimension is excluded below R^{-1} ~ 1 TeV (99% CL). With 6 universal extra dimensions (as in some extensions), bounds weaken to R^{-1} ~ 500 GeV but fine-tuning increases.

**Why**: KK leptons contribute to g-2 (electron anomalous magnetic moment), b -> s gamma, Z-pole precision observables. Direct searches at LHC (pair production of KK quarks/leptons) set limits R^{-1} > 0.7-1.2 TeV depending on decay modes.

### Large Extra Dimensions (ADD)

In the ADD model (Arkani-Hamed, Dimopoulos, Dvali, 1998), the Planck scale M_Pl is not fundamental; instead, the true fundamental scale M_* is lowered to ~1-10 TeV. Gravity is strong at M_*, but appears weak in 3+1D because it dilutes into (3+n)-dimensional space.

**Relationship**:
M_Pl^2 ~ M_* ^{2+n} * V_n

where V_n is the volume of n compact dimensions and M_Pl ~ 10^18 GeV is the effective 4D Planck scale.

For n=2 (two large extra dimensions) and M_* = 1 TeV, the extra dimensions must be macroscopic (~100 micrometers). This is excluded by tabletop gravity tests (e.g., Stanford torsion balance, BIPM superconductor experiments).

For n=6 and M_* = 1-10 TeV, extra dimensions are compressed to sizes ~ 0.1 mm to 1 nm, consistent with short-range gravity constraints.

**LHC bounds on ADD**:
- Direct KK graviton production: g g -> G_{KK} -> gamma gamma, dilepton, etc. Bounds: M_* > 4-5.5 TeV (95% CL, 13 TeV LHC)
- Virtual graviton exchange (contact interaction signature): Bounds comparable
- Mono-jet + missing energy: Bounds M_* > 2-3 TeV

**Astrophysical bounds**:
- Neutron stars: If extra dimensions are large, KK modes of SM particles are produced in core. Energy loss -> tighter bounds M_* > 200 TeV (Hannestad & Raffelt)
- White dwarfs: Similar constraints from cooling, M_* > 100-200 TeV

**Conclusion for phonon-exflation**: A framework predicting M_KK ~ 10^17 GeV (Planck-scale KK modes) sits OPPOSITE the naturalness/hierarchy problem. If the true scale is M_* ~ 10^17 GeV, then EITHER (a) extra dimensions are so tiny (< 10^{-35} m) that they are unobservable by any known probe, OR (b) the framework is not addressing the hierarchy problem. The empiricist notes: naturalness is NOT a law of nature, only a human preference.

### Warped Extra Dimensions (Randall-Sundrum)

In RS (1999), a 5D geometry is warped: g_{MN} = e^{-2 k |y|} g^{(4)}_{mu nu}, where k is a curvature and y is the extra dimension. The SM is localized on a brane at y=0, gravity spreads in the bulk.

**KK graviton spectrum**: m_n ~ m_1 * x_n, where x_n are roots of J_1 (Bessel function). m_1 ~ 10 TeV / k L, where L is the size of the extra dimension and k is the warping curvature.

**LHC bounds**:
- Spin-2 resonance searches (ATLAS, CMS) for g -> W W, Z Z, gamma gamma, t tbar.
- Bounds: m_1 > 3-5 TeV (95% CL), depending on couplings.

**Flavor constraints**: KK Z' and KK gluon mediate FCNCs. Bounds on KK Z' mass > 10-50 TeV depending on coupling assumptions.

**Key result**: If M_KK = 10^17 GeV, the Randall-Sundrum geometry would require k ~ 10 GeV and L >> 1, or compactification radius >> string scale. This is not ruled out but is not motivated by the hierarchy problem.

### Composite Higgs / Strongly-Interacting Models

Some approaches interpret KK modes not as geometric but as composite states from a strong dynamics at scale M_KK. This circumvents the question "where are the extra dimensions?" by saying the dynamics is 4D but generates a tower of states.

**Bounds**: Similar to RS bounds from precision tests. M_* > 1-5 TeV from precision electroweak fits, flavor, and direct searches.

## Key Results

1. **LHC 13 TeV (ATLAS+CMS, 2024)**: No KK graviton resonances detected. Bounds M_1 (first KK graviton) > 3-5 TeV (95% CL) for RS scenarios; M_* > 4-5.5 TeV for ADD with n=2-6.

2. **Precision Electroweak**: Indirect bounds from Z-pole observables, g-2, b -> s gamma exclude UED with R^{-1} < 1 TeV. Stronger limits if multiple extra dimensions coupled.

3. **Flavor Physics**: Rare decays (K -> pi nu nubar, B -> K nu nubar, B_s -> mu mu) constrain KK Z' and KK gluon contributions. Bounds m_{Z'} > 10-50 TeV; m_g* > 5-10 TeV.

4. **Astrophysical**: Neutron stars and white dwarfs constrain new light particles (KK neutrinos, extra scalars). Bounds M_* > 100-200 TeV if extra dimensions couple significantly to matter.

5. **Gravity Tests**: BIPM superconductor, Stanford torsion balance, Casimir effect experiments test submillimeter gravity for large extra dimensions. Exclude n=2 at M_* < 1 TeV. n=6+ or M_* > 10 TeV compatible with data.

6. **Collider Signatures Not Found**: Despite 15 years of LHC operation, no confirmed extra-dimensional signature. This RULES OUT naturalness at < 5 TeV but does NOT rule out existence at higher scales.

7. **Combined Constraints**: The most stringent multi-channel constraint from 2024 LHC + flavor + precision data: M_KK or M_* > 3-5 TeV (collider) and > 100+ TeV (astrophysics). Ultra-high scales (10^17 GeV) are NOT excluded but require extreme fine-tuning of geometry or couplings.

## Impact and Legacy

The 2024 PDG review is the empirical benchmark. It defines the landscape of "allowed" extra-dimensional models and tightens bounds annually as new LHC data arrive. The review's significance for phonon-exflation is clarification of what "constraint" means:

- **Bound vs. Limit**: A bound of M_KK > 3 TeV does NOT mean M_KK = 3 TeV is preferred. It means M_KK < 3 TeV is excluded. Higher scales are permitted.
- **Naturalness vs. Logic**: The LHC rules out naturalness at the weak scale but does not forbid Planck-scale KK modes on mathematical grounds.
- **The Empiricist's Role**: Sagan asks, "Is the theory falsifiable?" The answer is YES for M_KK < 5 TeV (ruled out). For M_KK = 10^17 GeV, the framework makes no falsifiable prediction at collider scales—it is decoupled from current experiments.

The review's trajectory (tightening bounds, no discoveries) suggests either (a) extra dimensions are at very high scales (beyond LHC reach), or (b) the hierarchy problem requires a different solution (supersymmetry, composite Higgs without extra dimensions, etc.).

## Connection to Phonon-Exflation Framework

**Phonon-exflation predicts M_KK ~ 10^17 GeV** (Planck scale), placing KK modes vastly beyond LHC discovery reach. The PDG 2024 constraints:
- **Do NOT rule out** M_KK = 10^17 GeV (bounds only exclude < 5 TeV)
- **Do rule out** any claim that KK modes explain the hierarchy problem (naturalness requires M_KK < 5 TeV for fine-tuning to be justified)
- **Imply** that if M_KK = 10^17 GeV, the framework must solve the cosmological constant problem or explain dark energy by OTHER mechanisms (e.g., BCS instability on M4 x SU(3), as phonon-exflation proposes)

**Sagan's verdict**: The framework is empirically consistent with current bounds but makes no testable LHC predictions. The claim that NCG + spectral action + M4 x SU(3) geometry naturally selects M_KK = 10^17 GeV is a mathematical assertion, not an empirical one. The burden of proof is on the theorist to show: (1) Why does this particular geometry naturally set M_KK this high? (2) What falsifiable predictions at accessible scales follow? Without answers, the framework occupies a phenomenologically sterile region.
