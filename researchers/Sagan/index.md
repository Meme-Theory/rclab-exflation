## Topic Map

### A. Sagan's Scientific Method (Competing Hypotheses)
Papers: 01, 03, 04, 05, 06, 07
Sagan's foundational approach: construct multiple competing hypotheses for the same observations, compute quantitative predictions for each, then let data discriminate. The Venus greenhouse (01), Mars dust transport (03), Faint Young Sun (05), and Io sulfur flows (06) all exemplify this method. Papers 04 and 07 show hypothesis-generation for Mars biology and Titan prebiotic chemistry respectively.

### B. Detection Standards and Biosignature Science
Papers: 08, 09, 10, 11, 12, 13, 14
The TTAPS nuclear winter paper (08) demonstrates quantitative prediction with acknowledged limitations. The Galileo life-detection experiment (10) is the gold standard for biosignature methodology: four independent lines of evidence on a known positive. ALH84001 (12) is the cautionary tale of premature claims. Seager (13) systematizes biosignature assessment for exoplanets. The Venus phosphine controversy (14) is the modern test case for the entire framework.

### C. Bayesian Inference and Model Selection
Papers: 15, 16, 17, 25
Trotta (15) is the canonical reference for Bayesian evidence and Jeffreys scale. Gross-Vitells (16) provides the look-elsewhere effect correction essential for any multi-parameter search. Lyons (17) surveys open statistical issues including blind analysis, nuisance parameters, and p-value interpretation. Kilbinger (25) demonstrates Population Monte Carlo for cosmological model comparison.

### D. Falsifiability, Non-Empirical Confirmation, and Underdetermination
Papers: 18, 19, 28, 29, 32
Ellis-Silk (18) demands falsifiability as the demarcation criterion. Dawid (19) defends non-empirical confirmation via structural constraints, no-alternatives, and explanatory promise. Rovelli (29) rejects Dawid, arguing non-empirical confirmation destroys science's self-correcting mechanism. Copan-Craig (28) formalizes the Sagan Standard as a Bayesian likelihood ratio. Stanford (32) shows that underdetermination is generic: multiple theories fit the same data, and theory choice requires pragmatic criteria beyond data.

### E. DESI Dark Energy Observations
Papers: 20, 27, 33
DESI DR2 BAO constraints (20) report w_0 = -0.72 +/- 0.04, deviating from LCDM at 2.5--3.9 sigma. The Nature Astronomy companion (27) reports 3.1 sigma tension and phantom crossing at z ~ 0.4. Giare (33) demonstrates that this significance is prior-dependent: extended priors reverse the conclusion, favoring LCDM.

### F. Extra-Dimensional Bounds and LHC Constraints
Papers: 21, 22
The PDG 2024 review (21) compiles all experimental bounds on extra dimensions: M_KK > 3--5 TeV (collider), > 100+ TeV (astrophysical). The CMS 2025 search (22) extends to power-law warped geometries. Neither constrains M_KK ~ 10^17 GeV as predicted by phonon-exflation, but neither validates it either.

### G. Spectral Action and NCG Foundations
Papers: 23, 24, 30
Chamseddine-Connes 1997 (24) introduces the spectral action principle: Tr(chi(D/Lambda)) yields Einstein-Cartan + SM from geometry. The 2010 paper (23) shows "uncanny precision" (99% from two terms) but also the sin^2(theta_W) = 3/8 discrepancy. Van Suijlekom (30) provides the finite-density extension critical for BCS on M4 x SU(3).

### H. Cosmological Constant Problem and Inflation
Papers: 26, 31
Martin (26) is the comprehensive review of the CC fine-tuning problem (80--127 orders of magnitude). Mukhanov (31) establishes the standard for predictive power: inflation predicted n_s ~ 0.96 before WMAP, a genuine falsifiable prediction confirmed by data.

### Paper 01: The Planet Venus
- **File**: `01_1961_Sagan_The_planet_Venus.md`
- **arXiv**: N/A
- **Year**: 1961
- **Relevance**: HIGH
- **Tags**: Venus, greenhouse, radiative transfer, competing hypotheses, prediction confirmed

**Summary**: Sagan's doctoral work systematically evaluated three competing hypotheses for Venus's anomalous 600 K microwave brightness temperature: hot ionosphere, warm ocean, and greenhouse effect. Through radiative transfer modeling, he showed only the greenhouse model self-consistently explained limb-darkening, spectral shape, and phase-angle dependence. His prediction of ~700 K surface temperature was confirmed by Venera landers a decade later.

**Key Results**:
- Three hypotheses tested against same data; two eliminated by specific observational signatures
- Surface temperature prediction ~700 K confirmed (Venera 7: 735 K, ~90 atm)
- Methodology template: quantitative atmospheric modeling applied to planetary problems

### Paper 16: Look-Elsewhere Effect (Gross-Vitells)

- **arXiv**: 1005.1891
- **Year**: 2010
- **Relevance**: CRITICAL
- **Tags**: trial factors, look-elsewhere effect, global significance, Davies method, autocorrelation

**Summary**: Practical method for computing trial factors in multi-parameter searches. A 3-sigma local excess in a 1000-bin scan is only ~1.6 sigma global. Uses Davies' extreme value theory.
