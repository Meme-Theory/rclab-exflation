## Thematic Organization

### PART I: Foundations and Dualities (1994-1998)

The second superstring revolution, when the five superstring theories were revealed to be limits of a single M-theory connected by duality.

| File | Title | Authors | Year | Key Contribution |
|:-----|:------|:--------|:-----|:-----------------|

**Narrative:** Dualities reveal that string theories are not independent but facets of a deeper structure (M-theory). Holography emerges as the deepest duality, connecting gravity to lower-dimensional quantum field theory.

### PART V: Swampland and Quantum Gravity Constraints (2005-2007)

Not all consistent-looking EFTs are compatible with quantum gravity. The swampland separates viable from unviable theories.

| File | Title | Authors | Year | Key Contribution |
|:-----|:------|:--------|:-----|:-----------------|
| 09 | String Landscape and Swampland | Vafa | 2005 | Swampland definition, finiteness, de Sitter conjecture, distance conjecture preview |
| 17 | Distance Conjecture | Ooguri, Vafa | 2007 | Light tower of particles, field range limits, inflation constraints |

**Narrative:** The swampland program identifies universal constraints from quantum gravity. These rules restrict which low-energy theories can be UV-completed, reducing landscape degeneracy and enabling predictions.
