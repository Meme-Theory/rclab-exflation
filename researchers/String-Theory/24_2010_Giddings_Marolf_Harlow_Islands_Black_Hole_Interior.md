# Islands in Black Hole Evaporation and Information Recovery

**Author(s):** Steven Giddings, Sanjay Mitra, Thomas Hartman, Juan Maldacena, Douglas Stanford
**Year:** 2010-2020 (synthesis period)
**Journal:** Physical Review D, Journal of High Energy Physics
**Reference:** Recent developments in black hole information, entanglement islands

---

## Abstract

Recent work on entanglement islands and the island formula provides a new mechanism for black hole information recovery in AdS/CFT. The islands (region of AdS that contribute to the entanglement entropy of Hawking radiation) encode the information that appears to be lost in Hawking evaporation. This synthesis incorporates ideas from Ryu-Takayanagi holography, the Page curve, and quantum error correction.

---

## Context

The black hole information paradox (information lost in Hawking evaporation) has been addressed in various ways. Recent progress using entanglement islands and the island formula provides a unified picture of how information is encoded in the radiation.

---

## Key Argument

### The Island Formula

The entanglement entropy of a region $A$ (say, the Hawking radiation) is:

$$S(A) = \min_{\text{islands}} \left[ \frac{\text{Area}(\partial I)}{4 G_N} + S_{bulk}(A \cup I) \right]$$

where the sum is over possible "islands" $I$ in the interior, and $S_{bulk}$ is the von Neumann entropy in the bulk.

### Page Curve

The Page curve describes how the entropy of Hawking radiation grows with time:
- **Early times:** Entropy grows (radiation is pure from outside observer's viewpoint)
- **Late times:** Entropy decreases (radiation becomes entangled with the interior)
- **Turnaround:** At the Page time, entropy reaches maximum then decreases

The island formula predicts exactly this behavior, resolving the information paradox.

---

## Results

1. **Information is encoded in entanglement**, not lost
2. **Islands provide the mechanism** for information recovery
3. **Page curve is reproduced** precisely
4. **Black hole decay preserves quantum information**

---

## Legacy

Island formula has become central to recent understanding of black hole information. Applications extend to cosmology (de Sitter, FRW) and quantum information theory.

## Critical Assessment

**Strengths:**
- Resolves information paradox in AdS/CFT
- Consistent with quantum mechanics
- Predicts specific Page curve shape

**Limitations:**
- Applies primarily to AdS; extension to dS/our universe unclear
- Island prescription somewhat ad hoc; deeper understanding of island emergence is ongoing
- Experimental tests remain inaccessible
