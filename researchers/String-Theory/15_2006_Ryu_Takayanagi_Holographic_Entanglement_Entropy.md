# Holographic Derivation of Entanglement Entropy from AdS/CFT

**Author(s):** Shinsei Ryu, Tadashi Takayanagi
**Year:** 2006
**Journal:** Physical Review Letters, Volume 96, page 181602
**arXiv:** hep-th/0603001

---

## Abstract

Ryu and Takayanagi propose that the entanglement entropy of a region in a conformal field theory is given by the area of a minimal surface in the dual AdS space. This formula, $S_A = \text{Area}(m_A) / (4 G_N)$, provides a geometric calculation of entanglement entropy via gravity, a profound connection between information-theoretic and geometric concepts.

---

## Context

Entanglement entropy measures quantum correlations in a system. In a CFT, the entanglement between a region $A$ and its complement $\bar{A}$ diverges at the boundary. Ryu-Takayanagi showed this divergence (and its structure) is precisely encoded in the AdS geometry via minimal surfaces.

---

## Key Argument

### Minimal Surface in AdS

Given a region $A$ on the CFT boundary, construct a surface in AdS with boundary equal to the boundary of $A$. Among all such surfaces, find the minimal area surface $m_A$.

The entanglement entropy of the field theory region is:

$$S_A = \frac{\text{Area}(m_A)}{4 G_5}$$

where $G_5$ is the five-dimensional Newton constant.

### Information-Geometric Duality

This formula encodes how geometric properties of AdS (the minimal surface) relate to information properties of the field theory (entanglement entropy). Regions with large entanglement correspond to large area minimal surfaces; regions with small entanglement correspond to small area surfaces.

---

## Results

1. **Entanglement entropy has geometric dual** in AdS
2. **Information and geometry are dual** in holography
3. **Universal formula** applies to all AdS/CFT

---

## Legacy

Ryu-Takayanagi became the most-cited paper in AdS/CFT after Maldacena's original. It inspired research on entanglement wedges, islands, and quantum error correction aspects of holography.
