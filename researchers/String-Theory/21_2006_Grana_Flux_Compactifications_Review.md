# Flux Compactifications in String Theory: A Comprehensive Review

**Author(s):** Mariana Grana
**Year:** 2006
**Journal:** Physics Reports, Volume 423
**arXiv:** hep-th/0509045

---

## Abstract

Grana provides an encyclopedic review of flux compactifications in string theory, covering Type IIB on Calabi-Yau, M-theory on G2 manifolds, heterotic theory, and their duals. The review explains how fluxes fix moduli, stabilize the compactification, and generate low-energy effective actions.

---

## Context

Flux compactifications are the foundation of modern string phenomenology. Grana's review became the standard reference for the field.

---

## Key Content

### Types of Fluxes

In Type IIB, fluxes are:
- NS-NS 3-form $H_3$
- RR 3-form $F_3$
- RR 5-form $F_5$

The combination of different fluxes through different cycles on the Calabi-Yau generates a superpotential:

$$W_{\text{flux}} = \int \Omega \wedge (H - i \tau F)$$

where $\Omega$ is the holomorphic 3-form and $\tau$ is the axion-dilaton.

### Moduli Fixing

By choosing the flux distribution, complex structure moduli are stabilized. The resulting potential has minima corresponding to vacua with different values of the coupling constant and moduli fields.

### RG Flow and the Landscape

The landscape emerges from the vast number of flux choices on a given Calabi-Yau. Different flux choices give different low-energy physics.
