# A Cosmological Constant from the Heterotic String

**Author(s):** Raphael Bousso, Joseph Polchinski
**Year:** 2000
**Journal:** Journal of High Energy Physics, 0008:002
**arXiv:** hep-th/0004134

---

## Abstract

Bousso and Polchinski demonstrate that a small positive cosmological constant can arise naturally in the heterotic string through the interplay of flux quanta and instantons. By considering the discrete spectrum of flux values allowed on a compactified manifold, they show that the cosmological constant takes discrete values. In the limit of many flux choices, the spacing between values becomes small, allowing fine-tuning to match the observed dark energy without requiring unnatural cancellations.

---

## Context

The problem of the cosmological constant has been central to cosmology since Einstein. Bousso-Polchinski's insight: in string theory, fluxes are quantized, creating a "ratchet" of discrete vacua densely packed in energy space. This provides a framework for understanding why the observed cosmological constant is so small without invoking improbable cancellations.

---

## Key Argument

### Flux Quantization and Discrete Vacua

In Type IIB on a Calabi-Yau with flux, the number of independent three-form fluxes (NS-NS and RR) determines the number of allowed configurations:

$$\Omega = \sum_i f_i H_i + \sum_i g_i C_i$$

where $H_i$ and $C_i$ are harmonic forms and $f_i, g_i$ are integers (quantized). The number of independent flux choices is roughly:

$$N_{\text{vacua}} \sim (\Delta f)^{2h^{2,1}}$$

where $h^{2,1}$ is the number of complex structure moduli. For moderate Calabi-Yaus, $N \sim 10^{100}-10^{500}$.

The cosmological constant in each vacuum is:

$$\Lambda_{\text{vac}} = \text{const} + \Delta \lambda_i$$

where $\Delta \lambda_i$ depends on the flux choice. With many flux options, $\Lambda$ takes a near-continuous range of values from large positive to large negative. Statistical selection (anthropic reasoning) suggests we find ourselves in a vacuum with $\Lambda \approx 0$—coincidentally the observed value.

### The Discretuum

Bousso and Polchinski introduce the term "discretuum": a dense set of discrete vacua. This is not a true continuum but discretized. The spacing is small ($\sim \Lambda_P / 10^{120}$) but non-zero. This solves the fine-tuning problem: there exists a vacuum near zero without requiring unnatural cancellations.

## Results

1. **Cosmological constant can be arbitrarily small** by choosing appropriate fluxes
2. **Discrete spacing of vacua** provides "room" for a near-zero vacuum
3. **Landscape picture** is quantitatively realized

---

## Legacy

Bousso-Polchinski is foundational for the landscape concept. It provided the first concrete mechanism for achieving a small cosmological constant in a consistent quantum gravity framework.
