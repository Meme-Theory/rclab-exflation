# Axions in String Theory

**Author(s):** Peter Svrcek, Edward Witten
**Year:** 2006
**Journal:** Journal of High Energy Physics, 0606:051
**arXiv:** hep-th/0605206

---

## Abstract

Svrcek and Witten show that string theory naturally contains numerous axion fields arising from the reduction of higher-form fields on internal cycles. These axions have decay constants controlled by the internal geometry, typically much larger than in particle physics models. This makes string axions excellent candidates for dark matter and inflation.

---

## Context

Axions (originally proposed to solve the strong CP problem) are pseudoscalars with tiny masses. String theory produces them automatically, without external fine-tuning.

---

## Key Argument

### Axions from Dimensional Reduction

In Type IIB on a Calabi-Yau, the ten-dimensional form fields reduce to four-dimensional scalars:

$$C_2 = \sum_i c_i(x) \omega^{(2)}_i$$

where $\omega^{(2)}_i$ are two-form harmonic forms on the Calabi-Yau. The coefficients $c_i(x)$ are four-dimensional axion fields.

The decay constant is related to the internal geometry:

$$f_a \sim M_P \cdot V^{1/n}$$

where $V$ is the internal volume. For large-volume Calabi-Yaus, $f_a$ can be enormous ($> 10^{15}$ GeV), making axions very light and excellent dark matter.

---

## Results

1. **Axions are generic** in string compactifications
2. **Decay constants determined by geometry**
3. **Axion inflation and dark matter are natural**

---

## Legacy

String axions became major candidates for dark matter, with experimental searches ongoing (ADMX, IAXO).
