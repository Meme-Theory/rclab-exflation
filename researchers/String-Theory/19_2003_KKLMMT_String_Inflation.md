# String Cosmology: Inflation via Moduli Fields

**Author(s):** Shamit Kachru, Renata Kallosh, Andrei Linde, Juan Maldacena, Lawrence McAllister, Sandip Trivedi
**Year:** 2003
**Journal:** Journal of Cosmology and Astroparticle Physics, 0310:013
**arXiv:** hep-th/0308055

---

## Abstract

KKLMMT extends KKLT by studying inflation in the moduli-stabilized Type IIB framework. They show that the volume modulus (the size of the internal Calabi-Yau) can play the role of the inflaton, driving early-universe acceleration. The model is called "Large Volume Scenario" and predicts specific inflationary observables (spectral tilt, tensor modes).

---

## Context

Inflation is the most successful early-universe model, but embedding it in string theory was difficult. KKLMMT provided one concrete realization via moduli inflation.

---

## Key Argument

### The Volume Modulus as Inflaton

In KKLT, the volume $V$ of the Calabi-Yau is one of the moduli to be stabilized. KKLMMT observes that $V$ can play the role of the inflaton if we allow it to slowly roll down an effective potential.

The effective potential for $V$ is:

$$V_{\text{eff}}(V) \sim \frac{\Lambda}{V^3} + \frac{W_0 A e^{-aT}}{V^2}$$

where the first term is from fluxes and the second is from gaugino condensation. Slow-roll inflation occurs in the region where the potential is flat.

### Predictions

Inflationary observables (spectral index $n_s$, tensor-to-scalar ratio $r$, etc.) are determined by the geometry:

$$n_s - 1 \sim -\frac{1}{N_e}, \quad r \sim \frac{1}{N_e}$$

where $N_e$ is the number of e-folds. Predictions are falsifiable against CMB data.

## Results

1. **Moduli inflation in string theory is possible**
2. **Specific, testable predictions**
3. **Geometric origin of inflationary parameters**
