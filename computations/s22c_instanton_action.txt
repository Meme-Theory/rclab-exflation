========================================================================

Berry's proposal (R2 Novel Physics #13): at a level crossing
(monopole), the WKB connection formulas change. The instanton
action can undergo a discontinuous change in its analytic
continuation.

At each monopole M_i, two eigenvalue branches swap order.
The Stokes phenomenon occurs when arg(xi_+ - xi_-) = pi/2,
i.e., when the gap between crossing levels is purely imaginary
in the complexified tau-plane.

Physical consequence: the instanton action picks up a phase
(-1)^n from n Stokes crossings. For a REAL action, this means
the sign of the instanton contribution can FLIP at a monopole.

PART 5: GAUSS-BONNET TOPOLOGICAL TERM
========================================================================

The Euler characteristic chi(SU(3)) = 0 (compact Lie group).
The Gauss-Bonnet integral is therefore tau-INDEPENDENT:

  integral (R^2 - 4*Ric^2 + Weyl^2) dvol = 32*pi^4 * chi = 0

This provides a CONSTRAINT relating R^2, Ric^2, and Weyl^2,
but does NOT contribute a tau-dependent instanton action.

chi(SU(3)) = 0 (standard result for non-trivial compact Lie groups)
=> Gauss-Bonnet topological instanton: tau-independent, contributes nothing.
