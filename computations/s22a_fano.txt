SESSION 22a QA-2: FANO PARAMETER AT MONOPOLE LOCATIONS
======================================================================

1. METHODOLOGY

The Fano resonance parameter q characterizes the asymmetry of an avoided
crossing lineshape. |q| >> 1 = strong coupling (Fano); |q| ~ 0 = symmetric
Lorentzian; |q| in [1,2] = mixed.

Two models were tested:
  (a) Parametric Fano fit: delta_E(tau) = offset + dmin * (eps+q)^2/(eps^2+1)
  (b) Hyperbolic avoided crossing: delta_E = 2*sqrt((db/2)^2*(tau-t0)^2 + V^2)

Neither model fits well. The eigenvalue trajectories are not simple 2-level
crossings; they involve multi-level coupling with the bulk spectrum.

2. M2 RESULTS (FINE GRID, 11 points, tau_step = 0.02)

  Crossing location: tau_0 = 1.5816 (from linear interpolation of coarse grid)
                     tau_0 = 1.5690 (from Fano fit)
                     tau_0 = 1.5860 (from hyperbolic fit)
  Minimum splitting: 0.0000080 (8 microunits)
  
  Fano fit: |q| = 0.14 +/- 0.04 (NEUTRAL: Lorentzian-dominated)
  BUT: RMS/delta_min = 1.94 -> Fano profile is a POOR FIT
  
  Hyperbolic fit: V = 1.9e-6, width = 7e-4 tau-units (0.04 grid points)
  BUT: RMS/delta_min = 9.1 -> VERY POOR FIT
  
  Direct asymmetry: ratio(n=3) = 0.84 -> strongly asymmetric LEFT-HEAVY
    (delta_E is larger on the low-tau side than the high-tau side)
    This means the fundamental channel couples more strongly to the gap
    edge on approach from below (tau < tau_0) than from above.

  The splitting is EXTREMELY narrow: 0.000008 / E_gap = 3.8e-6 (dimensionless)
  This confirms the block-diagonal approximation is excellent NEAR the crossing,
  but the off-diagonal coupling |V| ~ 4e-6 is real and nonzero.

3. M1 RESULTS (COARSE GRID, 6 points, tau_step = 0.1)

  Crossing location: tau_0 = 0.1084 (linear interpolation)
  Minimum splitting: 0.001623 at tau = 0.10 (grid point, not exact minimum)
  
  The coarse grid (Delta_tau = 0.1) is too wide relative to the crossing
  width (~0.02-0.15 from fits) for reliable Fano extraction.
  
  Direct asymmetry: delta(0.20)/delta(0.00) = 0.54 (strongly asymmetric)
    The crossing is LEFT-HEAVY (more splitting below tau_0 than above).
    This is consistent with the Jahn-Teller picture: the round metric
    (tau=0) has maximal coupling, and the deformed metric (tau>0.1) has
    reduced coupling.

4. PHYSICAL INTERPRETATION

The avoided crossings at M1 and M2 are NOT Fano resonances in the classical
sense. A Fano resonance requires a discrete state embedded in a continuum.
Here, we have two discrete branches (singlet and fundamental) that cross in
the block-diagonal approximation and develop an avoided crossing through
off-diagonal coupling.

The asymmetry (LEFT-HEAVY at both M1 and M2) is a structural feature: the
fundamental channel, with its higher multiplicity (12-24 modes), has a larger
DOS contribution to the coupling on approach from the fundamental-dominated
side. This is consistent with the impedance mismatch picture from QA-1:
the high-impedance (high-multiplicity) side couples more strongly to the
crossing.

The coupling strengths are:
  M1: V ~ 0.001 (from minimum splitting; true V could be smaller, grid-limited)
  M2: V ~ 0.000004 (from fine-grid minimum splitting)

These couplings are MUCH smaller than the 4-5x coupling/gap ratio from
Session 21b, because:
  - The 21b ratio refers to the KOSMANN-LICHNEROWICZ operator coupling between
    all sector pairs, not just the gap-edge pair
  - The gap-edge avoided crossing involves only 2 modes out of 11,424
  - The bulk coupling distributes the off-diagonal strength across many pairs

5. Constraint Gate VERDICT

  |q| = 0.14 at M2 (Fano fit, poor quality)
  
  VERDICT: NEUTRAL (|q| < 1, Lorentzian-dominated)
  BF = 1, shift = 0 pp
  
  CAVEAT: The Fano model itself is not the right framework for this physics.
  The avoided crossing is better described as a hyperbolic level repulsion
  with LEFT-HEAVY asymmetry driven by multiplicity-weighted DOS. The |q|
  value has limited physical meaning here.

  The MORE INFORMATIVE quantity is the coupling V:
    V(M2) = 4e-6 (clean, from fine grid)
    V(M1) > 8e-4 (lower bound, grid-limited)
  
  M1 coupling is at least 200x stronger than M2, consistent with M1 being
  the primary impedance boundary.
