GATE L-5: Per-Sector Pomeranchuk Map — DIAGNOSTIC (ALL SECTORS UNSTABLE)
=========================================================================

Definition: f_0^{(p,q)}(tau) = -N(0) * V_max, where N(0) = 1/delta_lambda
            and V_max = max eigenvalue of Kosmann pairing matrix.
            Pomeranchuk unstable when f_0 < -1 (s-wave channel).

Results:

  D_K basis (S27, 9 sectors, 9 tau values):
    ALL 9/9 sectors Pomeranchuk-unstable at some tau
    Deepest: (1,0) at tau=0.35, f_0 = -312.8

    Key sectors at tau=0.30:
      (0,0) singlet: f_0 = -6.6  (cf. S22c flow-based: -4.687)
      (1,0)/(0,1):   f_0 = -83.1
      (1,1) adjoint:  f_0 = -1.2
      (2,1):          f_0 = -3.0
      (3,0)/(0,3):    f_0 = -6.1

  D_can basis (S28a, 8 sectors, 10 tau values):
    ALL 8/8 sectors Pomeranchuk-unstable
    Deepest: (2,1) at tau=0.05, f_0 = -434.1

    NOTE: Extreme f_0 values near tau=0 are driven by near-degeneracy
    splitting (delta_lambda ~ 3e-4) giving N(0) ~ 3000. This is the regime
    where the density of states diverges as the bi-invariant degeneracy is
    just being lifted. The large N(0) is physical (Fermi-liquid analog of
    van Hove singularity), but the precise f_0 value is regulator-sensitive.

  Torsion enhancement (f0_can / f0_K):
    Mean enhancement: 5.59x (torsion STRENGTHENS Pomeranchuk instability)
    Largest enhancement: (2,1) at tau=0.10, 105x (degeneracy-driven)
    At physical tau=0.30: (1,1) 4.07x, (2,0)/(0,2) 2.49x, (2,1) 4.20x

VERDICT L-5: HIGH-MULTIPLICITY — (2,1) dominates in D_can basis (dim=15
drives physics). Inconsistent with the "(3,0)+(0,3) at tau~0.30-0.35"
prediction for BCS interior minimum. The instability is UNIVERSAL: every
sector is Pomeranchuk-unstable at every tau > 0.

Physical interpretation: The Pomeranchuk criterion is necessary but not
sufficient for BCS condensation. f_0 < -1 everywhere says the system
WANTS to condense, but K-1e showed M_max(mu=0) < 1 — the BCS kernel
eigenvalue is too small to sustain a self-consistent gap. The tension:
Pomeranchuk says "attractive", but BCS gap equation says "too weak".
This is consistent with a strong-coupling / BEC-crossover regime where
Landau Fermi-liquid theory breaks down.

=========================================================================
GATE L-6: Quasiparticle Weight Z(tau) — MODERATE MIXING
=========================================================================

Definition: Z(tau) = max_m |<psi_gap^{can} | psi_m^{K}>|^2
            where psi_gap^{can} is D_can gap-edge eigenstate
            and psi_m^{K} runs over all D_K eigenstates.
            Z < 0.5 = strong mixing, Z in [0.5, 0.7) = moderate mixing.

CRITICAL SUBTLETY (tau=0 degeneracy artifact):
    At tau=0 (bi-invariant metric), massive degeneracies exist:
      (3,0): 70-fold, 54-fold, 20-fold, 16-fold
      (2,1): 84-fold
    Eigenvectors within degenerate subspaces are NOT unique (gauge freedom).
    eigh() picks one arbitrary basis. The low Z(0) values (0.12-0.37)
    reflect this gauge freedom, NOT physical torsion mixing.

    MUST exclude tau=0 for physically meaningful Z analysis.

Results (tau > 0):

  Sector    Z_min(tau>0)   at tau    Interpretation
  -------   -----------   ------    --------------
  (1,0)     0.817         0.20     Weak mixing
  (0,1)     0.585         0.15     MODERATE mixing
  (1,1)     0.836         0.50     Weak mixing
  (2,0)     0.646         0.15     MODERATE mixing
  (0,2)     0.666         0.35     MODERATE mixing
  (3,0)     0.799         0.50     Weak mixing
  (0,3)     0.713         0.30     Weak mixing
  (2,1)     0.793         0.50     Weak mixing

  GLOBAL Z_min(tau>0) = 0.585 in (0,1) at tau = 0.15

  Gap-to-gap overlap Z_gg: NEAR-ZERO everywhere
    Explanation: D_can lambda_min is 2-5x smaller than D_K lambda_min.
    The gap-edge states live at completely different energy scales.
    D_can gap-edge overlaps with INTERIOR D_K modes, not D_K gap-edge.

  IPR (inverse participation ratio, tau > 0):
    Most sectors: IPR = 1.0-2.5 (gap-edge can state is 1-3 K modes)
    (1,1) and (2,1) at large tau: IPR up to 3.9-3.0 (moderate mixing)

VERDICT L-6: MODERATE TORSION MIXING (Z_min = 0.585 at tau=0.15).
    Z > 0.5 everywhere at tau > 0: no sector reaches strong mixing threshold.
    D_can gap-edge states are predominantly single D_K eigenstates (Z ~ 0.6-0.98)
    at the physically interesting tau range.

    Key insight: Torsion reshuffles EIGENVALUES (2-5x gap reduction) much more
    than WAVEFUNCTIONS (Z > 0.5). The D_can basis sees a compressed spectrum
    with similar-looking modes but at dramatically lower energies. This explains
    the 4.83x M_max enhancement from Session 28a: the BCS kernel benefits from
    the smaller gap (smaller denominators) more than from wavefunction mixing.

=========================================================================
COMBINED INTERPRETATION
=========================================================================

1. L-5 + L-6 together paint a consistent picture:
   - Pomeranchuk instability is UNIVERSAL (f_0 << -1 everywhere)
   - Torsion strengthens instability 2-100x (sector and tau dependent)
   - But the gap-edge wavefunctions barely change (Z > 0.5)
   - The dominant torsion effect is SPECTRAL COMPRESSION, not mode mixing

2. The (2,1) sector (dim=15) has the deepest instability in D_can basis,
   consistent with the multiplicity-drives-physics picture. Higher-dimensional
   representations have more modes near the gap edge, amplifying both
   the density of states N(0) and the V_max coupling.

3. The tension between "Pomeranchuk unstable" and "BCS subcritical" remains:
   f_0 << -1 says the interaction is attractive, M_max < 1 says it's not
   strong enough for self-consistent condensation at mu=0. This is the
   spectral-gap problem: the discrete KK spectrum has too few modes near
   the gap edge to sustain a macroscopic condensate.
