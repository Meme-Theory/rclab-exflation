- **Biggest gap in current library**: The absence of NuFit-6.0 (the authoritative global fit) and any accelerator neutrino experiment (T2K, NOvA) is the most critical gap. The library has discovery papers but lacks the precision-era results that define the target values for all framework predictions. Every neutrino gate in the registry uses NuFit values that are cited from memory rather than from a paper in the library. JUNO's first results (November 2025) are also absent -- these are the newest and most precise measurements of the solar oscillation parameters.

- **Most promising new direction**: The JUNO mass ordering determination (expected ~2028-2030) is the single most promising testable prediction from the framework. Normal ordering is a ZERO-PARAMETER structural prediction from B1 < B2 < B3 branch topology. No other framework prediction in the neutrino sector has this level of falsifiability. The second most promising direction is the intra-B2 quartet splitting as the source of Delta m^2_21 (proposed gate B2-SPLIT-41), which requires off-Jensen eigenvalue computation along the full 28D moduli space.

- **Confidence in recommendations**: HIGH for Priority A papers (all are authoritative, highly cited, directly relevant). MEDIUM for Priority B papers (some are design reports whose relevance depends on the experiment's timeline). MEDIUM for adversarial recommendations (the see-saw originators and anarchic mixing advocates are standard counterarguments, not novel challenges).
