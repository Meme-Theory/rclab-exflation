## 1. Geometry and Deformation

### 1.1 Manifolds and Spaces

`P` -- Total space of the Kaluza-Klein product manifold, P = M^4 x K. (B13 eq 1.4)

`M^4` -- External 4D spacetime manifold. (B13)

`K` -- Internal (compact) manifold, K = SU(3) throughout this project. (B13)

`su(3)` -- Lie algebra of SU(3), 8-dimensional. Decomposed as su(3) = u(1) + su(2) + C^2. (B15 eq 3.58, tier1_dirac_spectrum.py)

`u(1)` -- 1D subalgebra of su(3), generated by lambda_8. Index: [7] in Gell-Mann basis. (tier1_dirac_spectrum.py)

`su(2)` -- 3D subalgebra of su(3), generated by lambda_1, lambda_2, lambda_3. Indices: [0,1,2]. (tier1_dirac_spectrum.py)

`C^2` -- 4D complement of u(2) in su(3), generated by lambda_4, lambda_5, lambda_6, lambda_7. Indices: [3,4,5,6]. (B13 eq 1.1, tier1_dirac_spectrum.py)

`u(2)` -- 4D subalgebra u(2) = su(2) + u(1). Indices: [0,1,2,7]. (tier1_dirac_spectrum.py)

`m` -- Complement of u(2) in su(3); m = C^2. The "horizontal" directions for the reductive decomposition. (B15)

### 1.2 Jensen Deformation

`tau` -- Jensen deformation parameter (tau >= 0; tau = 0 is the bi-invariant round metric). Primary modulus of the framework. (B15 eq 3.68). See also: `s`.

`s` -- Alternative name for the Jensen deformation parameter (Baptista's notation in Papers 15-18). Identical to tau in project code. (B15 eq 3.68)

`lambda_1(tau)` -- Jensen scale factor for u(1) direction: lambda_1 = e^{2tau}. (B15 eq 3.68, tier1_dirac_spectrum.py)

`lambda_2(tau)` -- Jensen scale factor for su(2) directions: lambda_2 = e^{-2tau}. (B15 eq 3.68)

`lambda_3(tau)` -- Jensen scale factor for C^2 directions: lambda_3 = e^{tau}. (B15 eq 3.68)

`g_tau` -- Jensen-deformed left-invariant metric on SU(3). g_tau = e^{2tau} g_0|_{u(1)} + e^{-2tau} g_0|_{su(2)} + e^{tau} g_0|_{C^2}. Also written g_s, g_K, g_{K,s}. (B15 eq 3.68, tier1_dirac_spectrum.py)

`g_0` -- Bi-invariant metric on SU(3) (round metric). The tau = 0 reference. Derived from |B_{ab}| where B is the Killing form. (tier1_dirac_spectrum.py)

`g_P` -- Full KK product metric on P = M^4 x K, constructed from (g_M, A_L, A_R, g_K). (B13 eq 1.4)

`g_phi` -- Deformed metric on SU(3) parameterized by phi in C^2 (Paper 13 notation; superseded by g_tau in Paper 15). (B13 eq 1.2)

`phi` -- Deformation parameter in C^2 (Paper 13 notation, NOT the golden ratio). (B13 eq 1.3)

`g_hat_K` -- G-averaged metric on K, used in Paper 18 for the L_tilde construction. (B18)

### 1.3 Lie Algebra Infrastructure

`e_a` -- Orthonormal frame vectors on (SU(3), g_tau). In code: E_{ab} X_b where X_b are coordinate basis vectors. a = 0,...,7. (tier1_dirac_spectrum.py)

`e_j^L` -- Left-invariant vector fields on K = SU(3). (B13, B14)

`e_j^R` -- Right-invariant vector fields on K = SU(3). (B13, B14)

`lambda_a` -- Gell-Mann matrices (a = 1,...,8). Basis for su(3) in the fundamental representation. (tier1_dirac_spectrum.py)

`f_{abc}` -- Structure constants of su(3) in the anti-Hermitian convention: [e_a, e_b] = f_{abc} e_c. Computed via f_{abc} = -2 Tr([e_a, e_b] e_c). (tier1_dirac_spectrum.py)

`B_{ab}` -- Killing form on su(3): B_{ab} = f_{acd} f_{bcd}. For su(3) in anti-Hermitian convention: B_{ab} = -3 delta_{ab}. (tier1_dirac_spectrum.py)

`ft_{abc}` -- Structure constants in the orthonormal frame: ft^c_{ab} = E_{ad} E_{be} f_{def} (E^{-1})_{fc}. NOT totally antisymmetric for tau > 0. (tier1_dirac_spectrum.py)

`E_{ab}` -- Orthonormal frame matrix. e_a = E_{ab} X_b. Computed as inv(cholesky(g_tau)). (tier1_dirac_spectrum.py)

### 1.4 Connection and Curvature

`Gamma^c_{ab}` -- Levi-Civita connection coefficients in the ON frame. 2 Gamma_{cab} = ft_{abc} - ft_{bca} + ft_{cab}. Satisfies metric compatibility: Gamma^c_{ab} + Gamma^b_{ac} = 0. (tier1_dirac_spectrum.py)

`K^a_{bc}` -- Contorsion tensor. K = Gamma_canonical - Gamma_LC. On a Lie group with left-invariant metric: K = -Gamma_LC exactly. (S27 T-1, s27_torsion_gap_gate.py)

`Omega_LC` -- Spinor connection offset from the Levi-Civita connection. Omega = (1/4) Gamma^a_{bc} gamma_b gamma_c. (tier1_dirac_spectrum.py)

`Omega_T` -- Spinor connection offset from contorsion. On a Lie group: Omega_T = -Omega_LC. (S27 T-1)

`R_K(tau)` -- Scalar curvature of (SU(3), g_tau). R_K(tau) = -(1/4)e^{-4tau} + 2e^{-tau} - 1/4 + (1/2)e^{2tau}. R_K(0) = 2. (sp2_curvature_invariants.py, B15 eq 3.80)

`R_{abcd}(tau)` -- Riemann tensor on (SU(3), g_tau). 147/147 independent components verified at machine epsilon. (S20a, r20a_riemann_tensor.py)

`Ric_{ab}(tau)` -- Ricci tensor on (SU(3), g_tau). (sp2_curvature_invariants.py)

`|Ric|^2(tau)` -- Ricci squared invariant: sum Ric_{ab}^2. |Ric|^2(0) = 1/2. Exact analytic formula as sum of exponentials. (sd20a_seeley_dewitt_gate.py)

`|Riem|^2(tau)` -- Kretschner scalar. |Riem|^2(0) = 1/2. Exact analytic formula. Also written K(tau) in the code. (sd20a_seeley_dewitt_gate.py)

`R_P` -- Scalar curvature of the total space P. Decomposition: R_P = R_M + R_K - |F|^2 - |S|^2 - |N|^2 - 2 div(N). (B13 eq 1.5)

### 1.5 Submersion Geometry

`S` -- Second fundamental form of fibres (shape operator). |S|^2 maps to the Higgs covariant derivative |d_{A_L} phi|^2. (B13, B15)

`N` -- Mean curvature vector (trace of S). (B13, B15)

`F_A` -- Curvature of gauge connection (field strength). (B13, B15)

`A_L` -- Left gauge field on M^4, valued in u(2). Electroweak gauge field. (B13, B14)

`A_R` -- Right gauge field on M^4, valued in su(3). Strong gauge field. (B13, B14)

`d_A g_K` -- Covariant derivative of internal metric = second fundamental form. d_A g_K = 0 iff only massless gauge fields present. (B13, B15, B16 eq 1.3)

### 1.6 Torsion

`T^a_{bc}` -- Torsion tensor of the canonical connection on (SU(3), g_tau). (S26, S27)

`|T^0|^2 / R_K` -- Torsion-to-curvature ratio. Transitions from exactly 2/3 (tau=0) to exactly 4/3 (tau large). (S26 T-2 CLOSED)

## 2. Dirac Operator and Spectrum

### 2.1 Dirac Operators

`D_K` -- Dirac operator on (K, g_tau) with Levi-Civita spin connection. The central operator of the framework. D_K = sum_a rho(e_a) x gamma_a + I x Omega_LC. Exactly block-diagonal in Peter-Weyl decomposition. (B17 eq 1.3, tier1_dirac_spectrum.py, S22b Theorem 2)

`D_P` -- Full Dirac operator on P = M^4 x K. Decomposes as D_M + D_K + gauge coupling terms. (B14 eq 1.1, B17 eq 1.3)

`D_M` -- Dirac operator on external M^4. (B17)

`D_can` -- Dirac operator with canonical (flat) connection. D_can = M_Lie (the Lie derivative term only, with spin connection removed). (S27 T-1, s27_torsion_gap_gate.py)

`M_Lie` -- Lie derivative term in the Dirac operator: M_Lie = sum_a rho(e_a) x gamma_a. (tier1_dirac_spectrum.py, S27)

`D_pi` -- Dirac operator restricted to irrep (p,q): D_pi = sum_a rho_pi(e_a) x gamma_a + I x Omega(tau). Finite matrix of size dim(p,q)*16. (tier1_dirac_spectrum.py)

### 2.2 Clifford Algebra and Spinors

`gamma_a` -- Clifford generators of Cliff(R^8). 16x16 Hermitian matrices satisfying {gamma_a, gamma_b} = 2 delta_{ab} I. (tier1_dirac_spectrum.py)

`gamma_F` -- Chirality operator on the internal space. gamma_F = gamma_PA x gamma_CHI (Barrett classification). (S11)

`Psi_+` -- Positive chirality subspace, Psi_+ = C^{16}. Decomposes into one SM generation. (S7)

`S(h)` -- Vertical spinor transformation on K. Prescribes the spinor's behaviour along the internal SU(3). Psi_P(x,h) = S(h) psi(x). (B14 eq 1.6)

`dim(S)` -- Spinor fiber dimension. dim(S) = 2^{d/2} = 2^4 = 16 for d = 8 = dim(SU(3)). (sd20a_seeley_dewitt_gate.py)

### 2.3 Spectrum and Eigenvalues

`lambda_n(tau)` -- Eigenvalues of D_K at deformation parameter tau. Indexed by n within each Peter-Weyl sector. (tier1_dirac_spectrum.py, S12)

`lambda_min(tau)` -- Smallest nonzero |eigenvalue| of D_K at tau. The spectral gap. lambda_min(0) = 0.866 (code units). (S12, S23a)

`2*lambda_min` -- Full spectral gap of D_K. 2*lambda_min(0) = 1.644 (code units). (S23a K-1e)

`gap_T(tau)` -- Spectral gap of the canonical Dirac operator D_can. gap_T/gap_K ranges from 0.22 to 0.67 across sectors. (S27 T-1)

`gap_K(tau)` -- Spectral gap of the Levi-Civita Dirac operator D_K. (S27 T-1)

### 2.4 Peter-Weyl Decomposition

`(p,q)` -- Labels for irreducible representations of SU(3). Non-negative integers. The rep has dimension dim(p,q) = (p+1)(q+1)(p+q+2)/2. (tier1_dirac_spectrum.py)

`dim(p,q)` -- Dimension of the (p,q) irrep of SU(3). dim(0,0)=1, dim(1,0)=3, dim(1,1)=8, dim(3,0)=10, dim(2,1)=15, dim(2,2)=27. (tier1_dirac_spectrum.py)

`d_{(p,q)}` -- Alternative notation for dim(p,q), used in spectral sums. (framework-mechanism-discussion.md)

`rho_pi(e_a)` -- Representation matrix of generator e_a in irrep pi = (p,q). Size dim(p,q) x dim(p,q). (tier1_dirac_spectrum.py)

`mult(p,q)` -- Peter-Weyl multiplicity: dim(p,q)^2. Each irrep appears dim^2 times in the left-regular representation. (s27_multisector_bcs.py)

`max_pq_sum` -- Truncation parameter: only include irreps with p+q <= max_pq_sum. Typical value: 6. (tier1_dirac_spectrum.py)

`C_2(p,q)` -- Quadratic Casimir of the (p,q) irrep. C_2 = (p^2 + q^2 + pq + 3p + 3q)/3. (S27 addendum)

### 2.5 Real Structure and Discrete Symmetries

`J` -- Real structure operator (charge conjugation). Satisfies [J, D_K(tau)] = 0 identically for all tau. (S17a D-1)

`T` -- Time-reversal operator. T^2 = +1 (BDI class). [K_a, T] = 0. (S17c D-4)

`gamma_PA` -- Parity part of the chirality operator. (S11)

`gamma_CHI` -- Chi part of the chirality operator. (S11)

### 2.6 Kosmann-Lichnerowicz Derivative

`L_X` -- Kosmann-Lichnerowicz derivative of spinors along vector field X. L_X^{KL} in some references. (B17 eq 1.4, B18 eq 1.3)

`L_tilde_V` -- New Lie derivative for non-isometric group actions (Baptista original). Satisfies closure: [L_tilde_U, L_tilde_V] = L_tilde_{[U,V]}. (B18 eq 1.4)

`K_a` -- Kosmann spinorial correction operator. K_a = -(1/8) sum_{r,s} [Gamma^s_{ra} - Gamma^r_{sa}] gamma_r gamma_s. Nonzero only for non-Killing directions (a in C^2). (s23a_kosmann_singlet.py, B17 eq 4.1)

`||K_a||` -- Frobenius norm of the Kosmann operator. ||K_a|| = 0.77-1.76 across tau and C^2 directions. (S23a K-0)

`A^a_{rs}` -- Antisymmetric part of the connection: A^a_{rs} = Gamma^s_{ra} - Gamma^r_{sa}. Measures the rotation generated by covariant derivative of e_a. (s23a_kosmann_singlet.py)

`[D_K, L_X]` -- Commutator of Dirac operator with Kosmann derivative. Zero for Killing X (Prop 1.1), nonzero for non-Killing X (Props 1.2, 1.3). The chiral symmetry breaking mechanism. (B17 eq 1.4)

### 2.7 Conjugation Maps

`j_pm` -- Conjugation maps between spinor bundles for different metrics (S_{g_P} and S_{P*g_P}). (B18)

`a_0(tau)` -- Zeroth Seeley-DeWitt coefficient. a_0 = (4pi)^{-4} * 16 * Vol(K). Tau-INDEPENDENT (volume-preserving deformation). (sd20a_seeley_dewitt_gate.py)

`a_6(tau)` -- Sixth Seeley-DeWitt coefficient. Involves cubic curvature invariants. Monotonically increasing. (S26 P3, s26_p3_a6_computation.py)

`a_4/a_2` -- Ratio of fourth to second coefficient at tau=0. Equals ~985:1 -- the structural reason Starobinsky fails on SU(3). (S24b)

`f_0, f_2, f_4` -- Moments of the test function f: f_k = integral_0^infty f(x) x^{k/2-1} dx. Appear as coefficients in the spectral action expansion. (sd20a_seeley_dewitt_gate.py)

`E` -- Endomorphism in the Lichnerowicz formula: D^2 = nabla*nabla + E. For spinors: E = R/4 * I_{16}. (sd20a_seeley_dewitt_gate.py)

`Omega_{ij}` -- Curvature of the spin connection: Omega_{ij} = (1/4) R_{ijkl} gamma^k gamma^l. Appears in a_4. (sd20a_seeley_dewitt_gate.py)

### 3.3 Gilkey Invariants

`V_eff(tau, Delta)` -- Full effective potential including condensate: V_Baptista + condensation energy + Matsubara corrections. (framework-mechanism-discussion.md eq V.2)

`V_FR(tau)` -- Freund-Rubin potential (double-well). UV minimum at tau=0, barrier at tau~0.251, IR minimum at tau=0.30. (S22d)

## 4. Condensed Matter / BCS

### 4.1 Gap Equation Variables

`Delta` -- BCS gap parameter. Delta > 0 indicates condensation. (framework-mechanism-discussion.md, S23a)

`E_k` -- Quasiparticle energy: E_k = sqrt((lambda_k^2 - mu^2)^2 + Delta^2). (framework-mechanism-discussion.md eq V.2)

`mu` -- Chemical potential. mu = 0 is the self-consistent spectral action value. mu = lambda_min gives M ~ 11 (supercritical). Physical interpretation: energy cost of adding one more excitation. (framework-mechanism-discussion.md, S23a)

`mu_eff` -- Effective chemical potential from substrate energy density: mu_eff ~ sqrt(rho_4 / M_KK^2). At Planck epoch, mu_eff >> lambda_min. (framework-mechanism-discussion.md)

`V_{nm}` -- BCS pairing matrix: V_{nm} = -sum_{a in C^2} |<n|K_a|m>|^2. Purely off-diagonal in the eigenbasis at all tau > 0 (structural selection rule). (s23a_kosmann_singlet.py, S23a)

`V(gap,gap)` -- Self-coupling of the gap-edge modes. V(gap,gap) = 0 EXACTLY (selection rule). (S23a)

`V(gap,nearest)` -- Coupling between gap-edge and nearest modes. V = 0.070-0.131 across tau. (S23a)

### 4.2 BCS Criteria and Diagnostics

`T_c` -- Critical temperature for BCS transition. (S22c F-1)

`g` -- Effective Kosmann coupling strength in the gap equation. (framework-mechanism-discussion.md)

`N(0)` -- Density of states at the Fermi surface. For singlet: N(0) = 2 (from Session 22c L-2). (S22c)

`g*N(0)` -- Dimensionless BCS coupling. g*N(0) = 3.24 (moderate BEC regime). (S22c F-1)

`f(0,0)` -- Pomeranchuk parameter in (0,0) singlet: f = -4.687 < -3. BCS prerequisites met. (S22c F-1)

### 4.3 Condensation Energy and Free Energy

`F_cond` -- Condensation free energy per sector. F_cond < 0 indicates stable condensate. (S23a, s27_multisector_bcs.py)

`F_total(tau, mu)` -- Total BCS condensation energy: F_total = sum_{(p,q)} dim(p,q)^2 * F_cond^{(p,q)}. Interior minimum at tau=0.35 for mu/lambda_min=1.20 (erratic). (S27 P3)
